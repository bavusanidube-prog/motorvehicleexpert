Can You Tax a Car Without MOT?

Usually, no. In the UK, most vehicles that need an MOT must have a valid MOT before you can renew vehicle tax. If the MOT has expired, the usual next step is to book and pass an MOT before taxing the car.

Quick answer

For most cars that are old enough to need an MOT, you cannot normally tax the vehicle unless it has a valid MOT when the tax starts.

If the MOT has expired, book an MOT first. Once the car passes, you should usually be able to tax it, although MOT records may take a little time to update online.

Why an MOT is usually needed to tax a car

Vehicle tax, MOT and insurance are separate legal requirements, but they are closely linked when a vehicle is used or kept on public roads.

Roadworthiness

An MOT confirms the vehicle meets minimum safety and environmental standards at the time of the test.

Tax checks

When you tax a vehicle, MOT status may be checked electronically.

Public road use

A vehicle used or kept on public roads usually needs tax, MOT and insurance.

MOT expiry

If MOT has expired, renewal of vehicle tax is commonly blocked until the vehicle passes.

Insurance matters

Insurance may still be required, and an expired MOT can create complications after an incident.

Records update

After a pass, MOT data may not appear instantly in every system.

Possible exceptions

Some vehicles do not need an MOT, or do not need one yet. If the vehicle is MOT exempt, the usual MOT requirement for taxing may not apply in the same way.

  • 1Newer vehicles: many cars do not need an MOT until they reach the required age.
  • 2Historic vehicles: some older vehicles may be MOT exempt if they meet the rules.
  • 3Specialist exemptions: some vehicle types have different requirements.
  • 4Declared exemption: you may still need to confirm exemption status when taxing.

If the vehicle is exempt, make sure the exemption genuinely applies before relying on it.

What to do if your MOT has expired

1. Stop normal use

Do not continue using the car normally without a valid MOT.

2. Book an MOT

Arrange a genuine MOT appointment at an approved test centre.

3. Check roadworthiness

The car must still be safe to drive, even if travelling to a test.

4. Keep booking proof

Save the confirmation email, text or booking reference.

5. Tax after it passes

Once the MOT pass is recorded, you can usually renew tax.

6. Use SORN if off road

If you are not using or keeping it on the road, consider a SORN declaration.

Can you drive to an MOT without tax?

There is a limited situation where you may be able to drive an untaxed vehicle to a pre-booked MOT appointment, but the journey should be direct, the appointment genuine and the vehicle insured.

This does not mean you can use the car normally. Do not make unrelated trips, detours or daily journeys while waiting for the test.

  • !Only travel to a genuine pre-booked MOT or relevant repair appointment.
  • !Keep evidence of the booking with you.
  • !Make sure valid insurance is in place.
  • !Do not drive if the car is unsafe or has dangerous defects.

For more detail, read can you drive to an MOT without tax?

What if the car is off the road?

If the car is not taxed and is not being used or kept on a public road, it may need to be declared SORN. A SORN vehicle should stay off public roads except in very limited circumstances such as travelling to a pre-booked MOT.

Do not leave an untaxed car parked on the road while waiting for an MOT. Keep it on private land if it is off the road.

Can you insure a car without MOT?

Insurance and MOT are separate, so a vehicle may still have insurance even if the MOT has expired. However, driving without MOT can create legal and insurance problems, especially if the vehicle is unsafe or involved in an accident.

Check your policy wording and contact your insurer if you are unsure.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • !Trying to tax the car before the MOT pass is recorded.
  • !Assuming tax, MOT and insurance are the same thing.
  • !Driving normally just because an MOT is booked.
  • !Parking an untaxed car on a public road.
  • !Driving an unsafe car to the test centre.
  • !Forgetting that a failed MOT may create further restrictions.

Prepare the car before the MOT

Before booking, check the easy failure items such as lights, tyres, wipers, washers, mirrors, number plates and warning lights. Fixing simple faults first can reduce the chance of a fail and retest.

Frequently asked questions

Can I tax my car if the MOT has expired?

Usually no. For most vehicles that need an MOT, you need a valid MOT before taxing it.

Can I tax it as soon as it passes MOT?

Usually yes, but online records may take time to update after the pass.

Can I drive to the MOT without tax?

Only in limited circumstances, such as a genuine pre-booked MOT journey, with insurance and a roadworthy vehicle.

Can I keep an untaxed car on the road?

No. If it is untaxed and not being used, it should normally be kept off the public road and declared SORN if required.

Do new cars need MOT before tax?

Not usually before they reach MOT age, but they still need to be taxed and insured for road use.

What is the best next step?

Book the MOT, make sure the car is safe and insured, then tax it once it has passed.
