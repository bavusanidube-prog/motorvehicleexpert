Wheel Bearing Replacement Cost UK

A worn wheel bearing can cause humming, grinding or droning noises that get worse with speed. Replacing it in good time can prevent further damage and help keep the car safe and smooth on the road.

Quick Answer

How much does wheel bearing replacement cost in the UK?

Wheel bearing replacement in the UK often costs between £180 and £450+ per corner, depending on the vehicle, whether the bearing is supplied separately or as part of a hub assembly, and how much labour is involved.

Smaller mainstream cars are often cheaper, while larger vehicles and models with integrated hub units may cost more.

Why it matters

Do not ignore bearing noise

A noisy wheel bearing usually gets worse over time. What starts as a humming sound can eventually affect handling, tyre wear and driving comfort.

In more serious cases, leaving it too long can damage related parts or create a safety concern.

Common warning signs

Symptoms of a bad wheel bearing

  • 1Humming or droning noise while driving
  • 2Noise changes when cornering
  • 3Grinding or roughness from one wheel area
  • 4Vibration or rough running feel
  • 5Play detected in the wheel during inspection
What affects the cost?

Why quotes can vary

  • 1Vehicle make and model
  • 2Front or rear bearing location
  • 3Separate bearing or complete hub unit
  • 4Labour time and access
  • 5Garage hourly rate and part quality
Can you keep driving?

Is it safe to drive with a noisy wheel bearing?

Mild bearing noise may not mean immediate breakdown, but it should still be investigated promptly. If the noise is getting louder, the car feels rough or there is wheel play, it should not be ignored.

Early stage

Often noticed as a low humming noise at speed.

Worse stage

Noise becomes louder and may affect smoothness and confidence.

Best action

Get the bearing checked before extra wear or damage develops.

FAQ

Common wheel bearing questions

What does a bad wheel bearing sound like?

Usually a humming, droning or grinding noise that changes with speed.

Can tyre noise sound like a wheel bearing?

Yes. Uneven tyre wear can sometimes sound similar, so proper diagnosis matters.

Do both wheel bearings need replacing together?

Not usually. Only the faulty side may need replacing unless both show wear.

How long does replacement take?

Often 1 to 3 hours depending on design and access.
