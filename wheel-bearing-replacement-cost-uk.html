Wheel Bearing Replacement Cost UK

Wheel bearing replacement in the UK usually costs between £180 and £450+ per corner, depending on the vehicle, labour time and whether the bearing is replaced separately or as part of a complete hub assembly.

Quick answer

A typical wheel bearing replacement costs around £180 to £450+ fitted in the UK. Smaller cars are often cheaper, while premium vehicles, seized parts and complete hub assemblies can cost more.

The most common sign is a humming, droning or grinding noise that gets louder with speed.

Approximate wheel bearing replacement costs

  • £Small hatchback: £180–£260 fitted
  • £Family car or crossover: £220–£350 fitted
  • £Premium car or hub assembly type: £300–£450+ fitted
  • £Seized bolts, ABS sensor issues or extra suspension work may increase cost

Prices vary by garage labour rate, part quality, wheel location and how the bearing is fitted.

Symptoms of a bad wheel bearing

  • 1Humming, droning or rumbling noise while driving.
  • 2Noise gets louder as road speed increases.
  • 3Noise changes when cornering or loading one side of the car.
  • 4Grinding or roughness from one wheel area.
  • 5Vibration or rough running feel through the car.
  • 6Play detected in the wheel during inspection.

For more detail, read wheel bearing noise when speeding up.

What affects wheel bearing replacement cost?

Separate bearing or hub unit

Some cars use a separate bearing, while others require a complete hub assembly.

Front or rear bearing

Front and rear designs can differ, affecting parts and labour time.

Seized components

Rusty bolts, hubs or suspension parts can make the job take longer.

ABS sensor issues

Some hub assemblies include or affect ABS sensors, which can add cost.

Vehicle make and model

Premium vehicles and larger cars often use more expensive parts.

Garage labour rate

Labour rates vary by location and workshop type.

Is it safe to drive with a noisy wheel bearing?

Mild wheel bearing noise may not mean immediate breakdown, but it should be checked promptly. Bearing noise usually gets worse rather than better.

  • !Do not ignore noise that is getting louder.
  • !Do not continue driving if there is wheel play or severe grinding.
  • !A failing bearing can affect handling and tyre wear.
  • !Arrange inspection if the noise changes with speed or cornering.

What else can sound like a wheel bearing?

Wheel bearing noise can be confused with other issues, so diagnosis matters before replacing parts.

  • ✓Uneven or noisy tyres.
  • ✓Brake rubbing or worn brake components.
  • ✓CV joint noise when turning.
  • ✓Differential or gearbox noise on some vehicles.
  • ✓Suspension or steering component wear.

Frequently asked questions

What does a bad wheel bearing sound like?

Usually a humming, droning, rumbling or grinding noise that changes with road speed.

Can tyre noise sound like a wheel bearing?

Yes. Uneven tyre wear can sound very similar, so proper diagnosis is important.

Do both wheel bearings need replacing together?

Not usually. Only the faulty side normally needs replacing unless both bearings show wear.

How long does wheel bearing replacement take?

Often 1 to 3 hours depending on vehicle design, access and whether parts are seized.

Can a bad wheel bearing fail an MOT?

Yes, if there is excessive play, roughness or a safety-related defect. Read more in can wheel bearing fail MOT.
