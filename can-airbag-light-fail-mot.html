MOT Warning Light Guide

Can Airbag Light Fail MOT?

Yes, an illuminated airbag warning light can fail an MOT in the UK. If the SRS or airbag warning lamp shows a fault, the car may fail because the restraint system may not work correctly in a collision.

Quick answer

If the airbag light stays on after startup, flashes unexpectedly or indicates an active SRS fault, the vehicle can fail its MOT.

The airbag light should normally come on briefly when the ignition is switched on, then go out after the self-check. If it remains on, the system has detected a problem.

Why the airbag light can fail an MOT

The airbag and seatbelt pretensioner system is safety-related. If the warning light is on, the system may not protect occupants correctly in a crash.

Safety system fault

Airbags or pretensioners may not operate correctly during a collision.

Warning lamp active

An illuminated airbag light usually means the SRS module has detected a fault.

MOT failure risk

A visible active SRS warning light can be treated as a test failure item.

Common reasons the airbag light comes on

  • 1Loose or disturbed connector under a front seat.
  • 2Low battery voltage or previous flat battery event.
  • 3Clock spring fault behind the steering wheel.
  • 4Seat occupancy sensor fault.
  • 5Seatbelt pretensioner fault.
  • 6Crash sensor, wiring or SRS control module issue.
  • 7Seats, steering wheel or interior parts removed and refitted incorrectly.

Can you drive with the airbag light on?

The car may still drive normally, but the airbag or restraint system may not work properly if there is a crash. That makes it different from a simple service reminder or minor comfort fault.

  • !Do not ignore an active airbag warning light.
  • !The fault may affect airbags, seatbelt pretensioners or crash sensors.
  • !A warning light can return if the fault is only cleared and not repaired.
  • !Arrange diagnosis before the MOT rather than waiting until the test.

What to do before the MOT

1. Scan the SRS system

Use diagnostic equipment that can read airbag and restraint system fault codes.

2. Do not guess

Airbag faults can come from sensors, wiring, pretensioners, seats, steering components or modules.

3. Check recent work

If seats, steering wheel, battery or interior trim were recently touched, the fault may be linked.

4. Repair it properly

Because the system is safety-critical, repairs should use suitable parts and correct procedures.

Airbag light and used car buying

Be cautious if a used car has an airbag warning light showing. It may be a simple connector issue, but it can also point to accident damage, missing components, poor repairs or restraint system faults.

Before buying, check the MOT history, ask whether the car has had accident repairs and consider an independent inspection. Read the used car inspection checklist and Cat S vs Cat N guide if there is any repair history.

Frequently asked questions

If the airbag light comes on then goes off, is that normal?

Yes. It is normal for the airbag light to appear briefly during the startup self-check and then go out.

Will an airbag light always fail an MOT?

If the airbag warning light stays on and shows an active fault, it can fail the MOT.

Can a weak battery trigger the airbag light?

Yes. Low voltage or a flat battery event can sometimes trigger SRS warning lights, but the fault still needs checking.

Can I clear the airbag light myself?

Some diagnostic tools can clear codes, but the light will usually return if the fault has not been repaired.

Is it safe to ignore an airbag warning light?

No. The restraint system may not work correctly in a collision, so the warning should be diagnosed.

What is the best next step before an MOT?

Have the SRS system scanned, identify the fault code and repair the underlying issue before the test.
