Can Wipers Fail MOT?

Yes, windscreen wipers can fail an MOT in the UK if they do not clear the screen effectively, are damaged, insecure, missing, or if the washer system does not work correctly. Wipers are a safety item because clear vision is essential in rain, spray and poor weather.

Quick answer

Your vehicle can fail an MOT if the windscreen wipers do not operate correctly or fail to clear the driver’s view properly. Split blades, smearing, seized movement, damaged arms or faulty washers can all cause problems.

Many wiper-related failures are simple and inexpensive to fix before the test.

When can wipers fail an MOT?

Worn blades

Blades that smear or streak badly may fail.

Split rubber

Damaged rubber edges may not clear water properly.

Not operating

If the wipers do not move correctly, it can fail.

Loose arms

Insecure or damaged wiper arms may be rejected.

Washer fault

Front washers normally need to function correctly.

Poor visibility

If the swept area is not cleared properly, it is an MOT issue.

Signs your wipers need replacing

  • 1Smearing across the windscreen.
  • 2Juddering or skipping movement.
  • 3Squeaking noises.
  • 4Missed patches left uncleared.
  • 5Split or cracked blade rubber.
  • 6Slow or sticking movement.

Why wipers matter for safety

Even a mechanically sound car can become unsafe if the driver cannot see clearly in rain, road spray or winter grime. That is why MOT tests include visibility items such as wipers, washers and windscreen condition.

Fresh blades often improve visibility far more than drivers expect.

What to check before your MOT

1. Test both speeds

Check normal and fast wipe operation.

2. Use washers

Make sure fluid sprays onto the windscreen properly.

3. Inspect blades

Look for splits, cracks or bent sections.

4. Check clearing

See if they wipe cleanly without smearing.

5. Top up fluid

Low washer fluid is an easy fix.

6. Replace early

Wiper blades are usually low-cost maintenance items.

Should you replace wipers before MOT?

Usually yes. If blades smear, skip, squeak or leave missed areas, replacement is often the quickest solution.

If the wipers do not move at all, further checks may be needed for fuses, linkages, motors or switches.

Typical UK repair costs

New blades

Usually low cost and easy to fit.

Washer jet or pump fix

Moderate cost depending on the fault.

Motor or linkage

Higher cost if the mechanism has failed.

Frequently asked questions

Can worn blades fail MOT?

Yes, if they do not clear the screen properly.

Do washers need to work?

Usually yes, front washers are normally tested.

Can rear wiper fail MOT?

Rear wipers are treated differently depending on vehicle fitment and test scope.

How often replace blades?

Many drivers replace them every 6 to 12 months depending on use.

Can smearing alone fail?

If visibility is reduced enough, yes it can be an issue.

Best pre-test action?

Fit fresh blades and top up washer fluid.
