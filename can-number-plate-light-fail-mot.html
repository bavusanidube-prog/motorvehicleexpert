Can Number Plate Light Fail MOT?

Yes, a faulty number plate light can fail an MOT if the rear registration plate is not properly illuminated. Testers check that the rear plate lamp works and allows the number plate to be clearly visible.

Quick answer

If the rear number plate lamp does not work, is missing or does not illuminate the plate properly, it can cause MOT failure.

This is often a simple fix such as a blown bulb, poor connection or corroded holder.

When number plate lights can fail MOT

Bulb not working

No light output from one or more lamps.

Plate not visible

Registration plate cannot be clearly illuminated.

Lens missing

Broken or missing lens units may be a defect.

Loose fitting

Lamp units should be secure.

Water ingress

Moisture can damage holders and contacts.

Wiring fault

Broken wires or connectors can stop the light working.

Common reasons the light stops working

The most common cause is simply a failed bulb. On older cars, corrosion in the bulb holder or damaged wiring near the boot lid is also common.

Some newer vehicles use LED units, where the full lamp may need replacement.

What to check before the MOT

1. Turn lights on

Switch sidelights or headlights on and inspect rear plate lamps.

2. Check both sides

Some cars use two lamps and one may have failed.

3. Clean lenses

Mud and dirt can reduce brightness.

4. Inspect cracks

Broken housings can allow water inside.

5. Replace failed bulb

Often quick and inexpensive.

6. Check plate condition too

Dirty or damaged plates can also create issues.

Usually an easy repair

Number plate light faults are often among the simplest MOT issues to fix. Replacing bulbs or cleaning contacts may solve the problem quickly.

If wiring is damaged, further repair may be needed.

Frequently asked questions

Will one bulb out fail MOT?

Is it an easy fix?

Usually yes, often just a bulb replacement.

Do LED lights fail too?

Yes, LED units can fail and may need replacement.

Can dirt cause failure?

Heavy dirt can reduce visibility and should be cleaned.

Should I check before MOT?
