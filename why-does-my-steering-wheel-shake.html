Why Does My Steering Wheel Shake?

A shaking steering wheel is usually caused by a tyre, wheel, brake, suspension or alignment problem. The biggest clue is when it happens: at motorway speed, under braking, after a pothole, or all the time.

Quick answer

If the steering wheel shakes mainly at certain speeds, suspect wheel balancing, tyre damage, uneven tyre wear or a bent wheel. If it shakes mostly when braking, brake discs, pads, calipers or suspension movement are more likely.

Do not ignore vibration that is severe, sudden, getting worse, or linked with braking instability, knocking noises, tyre damage, humming, grinding or pulling to one side.

Common causes of steering wheel vibration

  • 1Wheel balancing: vibration at certain road speeds is often caused by imbalance.
  • 2Tyre damage: bulges, flat spots, uneven wear or internal tyre damage can cause shaking.
  • 3Bent wheel: kerb or pothole damage can create vibration even if the tyre looks normal.
  • 4Brake disc problems: shaking while braking may come from disc runout or uneven pad deposits.
  • 5Suspension wear: worn bushes, ball joints or track rod ends can allow unwanted movement.
  • 6Wheel alignment: poor tracking can cause pulling, uneven tyre wear and unstable steering feel.
  • 7Wheel bearing issues: vibration with humming or rumbling may need urgent inspection.

Why does the steering wheel shake when braking?

If the vibration appears mainly when you press the brake pedal, the front brakes are a common suspect. Brake discs can become uneven, corroded, overheated or affected by pad deposits. Worn suspension parts can also make braking vibration feel worse through the steering wheel.

  • ✓Brake disc runout or uneven disc wear.
  • ✓Uneven pad deposits or worn brake pads.
  • ✓Sticking brake caliper or slider pins.
  • ✓Worn front suspension bushes or ball joints.
  • ✓Wheel bearing or hub play.

What to check first

  • ✓Check tyre pressures on all four tyres.
  • ✓Look for tyre bulges, cracks, nails, exposed cords or uneven tread wear.
  • ✓Think about recent pothole or kerb impacts.
  • ✓Notice whether vibration happens at speed, under braking or all the time.
  • ✓Check whether the car pulls left or right.
  • ✓Listen for humming, knocking, scraping or grinding noises.
  • ✓Ask a garage to check wheel balance, tyres, wheels, brakes, bearings and suspension.

When to get it checked urgently

Get the car inspected promptly if the vibration is strong, worsening, appears suddenly after impact, or comes with braking instability, steering looseness, tyre damage, grinding noises, humming, knocking or pulling to one side.

  • !Visible tyre bulge, cut, exposed cords or rapid pressure loss.
  • !Strong vibration through the steering wheel or whole car.
  • !Car pulls sharply left or right.
  • !Grinding, scraping, knocking or humming noises.
  • !Vibration started after wheel, tyre, brake or suspension work.
  • !Steering feels loose, heavy or unpredictable.

Can steering wheel shake fail an MOT?

The shake itself is usually a symptom, not the MOT failure item. However, the cause can fail an MOT if it involves unsafe tyres, wheel bearing play, worn suspension, steering faults, brake problems or damaged wheels.

Frequently asked questions

Can wheel balancing cause steering wheel shake?

Yes. Wheel imbalance is one of the most common causes of steering wheel vibration at certain road speeds.

Why does my steering wheel shake when braking?

Brake disc problems, uneven pad deposits, worn suspension parts, wheel bearing play or sticking calipers can cause shaking during braking.

Can bad tyres make the steering wheel vibrate?

Yes. Uneven wear, flat spots, bulges, low pressure or internal tyre damage can cause vibration.

Can alignment cause steering wheel shake?

Alignment is more likely to cause pulling or uneven tyre wear, but it can contribute to vibration if tyre wear has developed.

Is it safe to drive with steering wheel vibration?

Mild vibration may allow careful short driving, but severe, sudden or worsening vibration should be checked urgently.

What should a garage check first?

Tyres, wheel balance, wheel damage, brakes, suspension joints, track rod ends and wheel bearings are common first checks.
