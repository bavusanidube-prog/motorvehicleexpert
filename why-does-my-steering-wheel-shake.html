Why does my steering wheel shake?

A shaking steering wheel can be caused by wheel balancing problems, tyre damage, bent wheels, warped brake discs, worn suspension parts or wheel alignment issues. When the shake happens is the biggest clue.

Quick answer

If the steering wheel shakes mainly at certain speeds, wheel balancing, tyre wear or a bent wheel is often suspected. If it shakes mostly when braking, brake discs, pads or suspension movement may be more likely.

Do not ignore vibration that is severe, getting worse, or linked with braking instability, knocking noises, tyre damage or pulling to one side.

When the steering wheel shakes matters

Shakes at motorway speed

Often linked to wheel balancing, tyre defects, uneven wear or a bent alloy wheel.

Shakes when braking

Can point to brake disc runout, worn brake parts, sticking calipers or suspension movement.

Shakes after hitting a pothole

A pothole impact can damage tyres, bend wheels or disturb alignment.

Shakes with knocking noises

May suggest suspension, steering joint, wheel bearing or mounting wear.

Common causes of steering wheel vibration

  • 1Wheel balancing: vibration that appears at certain speeds is often caused by imbalance.
  • 2Tyre damage: bulges, flat spots, uneven wear or internal tyre damage can cause shaking.
  • 3Bent wheel: kerb or pothole damage can create vibration even if the tyre looks fine.
  • 4Brake disc problems: shaking while braking may come from disc runout or uneven pad deposits.
  • 5Suspension wear: worn bushes, ball joints or track rod ends can allow unwanted movement.
  • 6Wheel alignment: poor tracking can cause pulling, uneven tyre wear and unstable steering feel.
  • 7Wheel bearing issues: vibration with humming or rumbling may need urgent inspection.

Tyre and wheel faults

Check for low pressure, uneven tread, sidewall bulges, cracks, nails, flat spots and wheel damage.

Brake-related shaking

If the steering wheel shakes mainly under braking, inspect discs, pads, calipers and suspension play.

Suspension and steering

Worn joints, bushes or steering components can make vibration worse and affect vehicle control.

What to check first

  • ✓Check tyre pressures on all four tyres.
  • ✓Look for tyre bulges, cracks, nails or uneven tread wear.
  • ✓Think about recent pothole or kerb impacts.
  • ✓Notice whether vibration happens at speed, under braking or all the time.
  • ✓Check whether the car pulls left or right.
  • ✓Listen for humming, knocking, scraping or grinding noises.

When to get it checked urgently

Get the car inspected promptly if the vibration is strong, worsening, appears suddenly after impact, or comes with braking instability, steering looseness, tyre damage, grinding noises or pulling to one side.

Strong vibration can affect control of the vehicle and may indicate a tyre, wheel, brake or suspension fault that should not be ignored.

Frequently asked questions

Can wheel balancing cause steering wheel shake?

Yes. Wheel imbalance is one of the most common causes of vibration at certain road speeds.

Why does my steering wheel shake when braking?

Brake disc problems, uneven pad deposits, worn suspension parts or sticking calipers can cause shaking during braking.

Can bad tyres make the steering wheel vibrate?

Yes. Uneven wear, flat spots, bulges or internal tyre damage can cause vibration.

Is it safe to drive with steering wheel vibration?

Mild vibration may allow careful short driving, but severe, sudden or worsening vibration should be checked urgently.
