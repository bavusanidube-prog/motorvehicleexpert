Car Aircon Smells Mouldy

If your car air conditioning smells mouldy, damp or stale when switched on, moisture is often trapped somewhere in the ventilation system. This can allow bacteria, mildew or odours to build up over time.

Quick answer

A mouldy smell from car aircon is usually caused by dampness inside the evaporator area, a dirty cabin filter, blocked drainage or debris in the air intake.

Many cases improve with a new pollen filter and aircon cleaning treatment, but persistent smells may need inspection.

Common reasons car aircon smells mouldy

Damp evaporator

Moisture left on cooling components can encourage bacteria and mildew.

Dirty cabin filter

A clogged pollen filter can trap moisture, dirt and odours.

Blocked drain tube

Condensation that cannot drain away may create damp smells.

Leaves in air intake

Wet debris near the scuttle intake can smell musty through vents.

Low system use

Cars that rarely use aircon may develop stale odours more easily.

Misidentified smell

Coolant or damp carpet smells are sometimes mistaken for mouldy aircon.

Signs that help narrow it down

  • 1The smell appears only when aircon is switched on.
  • 2It is strongest after the car has stood overnight.
  • 3Airflow feels weak as well as smelling stale.
  • 4Windows mist up more easily than normal.
  • 5The smell is damp or musty rather than burning.
  • 6It improves briefly after changing fan speed.

How to fix mouldy car aircon smells

1. Replace cabin filter

An overdue pollen filter is one of the most common causes.

2. Use aircon cleaner

Vent disinfectant or evaporator cleaning products can help.

3. Check drainage

Blocked condensation drains may need clearing.

4. Remove debris

Leaves and dirt around the scuttle intake can smell when wet.

5. Run system regularly

Use aircon year-round to reduce moisture build-up.

6. Book inspection

Persistent odours may need professional cleaning or repair.

How to help prevent smells returning

  • ✓Run the aircon regularly, even in winter.
  • ✓Replace cabin filters on schedule.
  • ✓Keep scuttle drains and intake areas clear.
  • ✓Turn off cooling a minute before parking to dry vents.
  • ✓Fix water leaks into the cabin quickly.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my aircon smell mouldy at first?

Moisture and bacteria inside the system often smell strongest on start-up.

Will changing the cabin filter help?

Yes, a dirty pollen filter is a common cause of stale smells.

Can I use antibacterial spray?

Many drivers use dedicated aircon cleaners successfully.

Does low gas cause mouldy smells?

Usually no. Smells are more often moisture or filter related.

Should I worry about mould?

Persistent odours mean the system should be cleaned or checked.

When should I seek repair?

If smells return quickly, airflow is weak or water leaks appear.
