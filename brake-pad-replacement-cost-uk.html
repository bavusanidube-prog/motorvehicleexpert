Repair Cost Guide

Brake Pad Replacement Cost UK

Brake pad replacement is one of the most common maintenance jobs in the UK. Most drivers can expect to pay between £90 and £250 per axle, although costs vary depending on the vehicle, parts used and garage labour rates.

Quick Answer

Average brake pad replacement cost in the UK

For many cars, replacing front or rear brake pads usually costs between £90 and £250. Smaller cars are often cheaper, while SUVs, premium vehicles and performance models can cost more.

If the brake discs are worn and need replacing at the same time, the bill may rise to £220 to £500+.

Typical Prices

What drivers often pay

  • £Small hatchback: £90–£140
  • £Family saloon / hatchback: £120–£180
  • £Large SUV / premium car: £180–£250+
  • £Pads and discs together: £220–£500+
What affects the price?

Why quotes can vary

Vehicle make and model

Larger and premium vehicles often use more expensive brake parts.

Front or rear pads

Front pads usually wear faster because they handle more braking force.

Parts quality

Budget pads may cost less, while premium brands can offer lower noise and longer life.

Labour rates

Labour costs vary across the UK and are often higher in cities.

Brake disc condition

Worn or damaged discs may need replacing alongside the pads.

Seized components

Rusted calipers, sliders or bolts can increase labour time.

Warning Signs

Signs your brake pads may need replacing

  • 1Squealing or squeaking when braking
  • 2Grinding noise when braking
  • 3Longer stopping distances
  • 4Brake warning light on some vehicles
  • 5Very thin pads visible through the wheel
Important

Do not leave worn pads too long

If brake pads wear completely down, metal backing plates can damage the brake discs. What could have been a simple pad change can quickly become a more expensive pads-and-discs repair.

If your brakes feel weak, noisy or unsafe, arrange an inspection as soon as possible.

Front vs Rear

Front and rear brake pad costs

Front brake pads usually wear out first because most of the braking force is applied at the front wheels.

Rear pads can last longer, although some modern cars use electronic parking brake systems which may increase labour costs when rear pads are changed.

How Long They Last

Brake pad lifespan

Many brake pads last between 20,000 and 50,000 miles, depending on driving style, traffic conditions and the quality of the parts fitted.

Frequent town driving usually wears pads faster than motorway driving.

FAQ

Common questions

Do all four brake pads need replacing together?

No. Pads are normally replaced in pairs on the same axle – both front pads or both rear pads.

Can I drive with worn brake pads?

It is not recommended. Worn pads can reduce braking performance and may damage the discs.

Will worn brake pads fail an MOT?

They can if braking efficiency is poor or components are excessively worn.

Is £250 expensive for brake pads?

It depends on the vehicle. For premium cars or vehicles needing higher-spec parts, this can be normal.
