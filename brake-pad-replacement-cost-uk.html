Repair Cost Guide

Brake Pad Replacement Cost UK

Brake pad replacement cost in the UK depends on the car, garage labour rate, parts quality and whether front or rear pads are being replaced. Ignoring worn pads can lead to more expensive brake disc damage.

Quick Answer

Average UK brake pad replacement cost

For many UK vehicles, brake pad replacement usually costs between £90 and £250 per axle. Small cars may cost less, while SUVs, premium cars and performance models can cost more.

If brake discs also need replacing, the total repair bill can rise significantly.

Typical Prices

What drivers often pay

  • £Small hatchback: £90–£140
  • £Family car: £120–£180
  • £Large SUV / premium car: £180–£250+
  • £Pads + discs together: £220–£500+
What affects the price?

Why quotes can vary

Vehicle make and model

Premium brands and larger vehicles often use more expensive brake components.

Front or rear pads

Front pads commonly wear faster, but either axle may need attention depending on use.

Parts quality

Budget pads can be cheaper, but noise levels and performance may differ.

Labour rates

Prices vary by region, garage type and labour charges.

Brake disc condition

Worn or damaged discs may need replacing at the same time.

Extra seized parts

Corroded calipers or stuck components can increase labour time.

Warning Signs

Signs your brake pads may be worn

  • 1Squealing when braking
  • 2Grinding noise
  • 3Longer stopping feel
  • 4Brake warning light on some cars
  • 5Very thin pads visible through wheels
Important

Do not wait too long

If pads wear fully down, metal can contact the brake disc. This often turns a simple pad replacement into a more expensive pads-and-discs repair.

If braking feels unsafe, noisy or poor, arrange inspection promptly.

FAQ

Common questions

Do all four brake pads need replacing together?

Usually pads are replaced by axle – both fronts or both rears together.

How long do brake pads last?

Many last around 20,000–50,000 miles, depending on driving style and conditions.

Can I drive with worn brake pads?

Only cautiously and for as little as possible. Unsafe brakes should be checked immediately.

Will worn brake pads fail an MOT?

They can if braking performance or component condition falls below MOT standards.
