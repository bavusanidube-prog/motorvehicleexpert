Burning smell guide

Car smells like burning oil

A burning oil smell from a car often means oil is leaking onto hot engine components or the engine is running hotter than normal. It should not be ignored, especially if smoke, warning lights or low oil level appear at the same time.

Common causes

Why a car may smell like burning oil

The smell usually comes from engine oil contacting hot metal surfaces, but several different faults can create similar symptoms.

Oil Leak

Oil leaking onto hot engine parts

A common cause is oil dripping onto the exhaust manifold or other hot components.

Overheating

Engine running too hot

Excessive heat can intensify smells and may indicate a cooling-system issue.

Low oil level or lubrication issues

Low oil level can create engine stress and warning lights alongside unusual smells.

Servicing

Spilled oil after maintenance

Engine Fault

Engine running problems

Poor running or misfires can create extra heat and unusual smells.

Leaks

Age-related gasket seepage

Older vehicles may develop minor oil leaks that become more noticeable over time.

Take it seriously

Warning signs that matter most

  • 1Smoke from under bonnet
  • 2Oil warning light appears
  • 3Oil level dropping regularly
  • 4Engine overheating
  • 5Strong smell after every drive
What drivers should do

Better next steps

1. Check oil level

Use the dipstick or vehicle display if fitted.

2. Look for leaks

Check for oil spots under the car or visible residue in the engine bay.

3. Avoid ignoring it

Persistent burning oil smells can become more serious.

4. Get it inspected

A garage can confirm whether it is a minor leak or something more urgent.
