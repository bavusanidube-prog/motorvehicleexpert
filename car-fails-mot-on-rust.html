MOT corrosion guide

Car Fails MOT on Rust

If your car fails its MOT on rust, the corrosion is usually affecting a structural, safety-related or prescribed area. Light surface rust may pass, but serious corrosion near suspension, brakes, steering, seatbelts, sills or chassis sections often needs repair before the car can pass.

Quick answer

Rust usually fails an MOT when it weakens the vehicle structure, creates holes in important areas, affects suspension or steering mountings, compromises seatbelt anchorages, damages brake pipes, or creates sharp edges that could injure someone.

Small cosmetic rust bubbles or light surface rust do not automatically mean failure. The key questions are where the corrosion is, how severe it is, and whether it affects safety or structural strength.

Common areas where rust can fail MOT

Sills

Inner and outer sills are common corrosion areas, especially near jacking points and seatbelt zones.

Chassis sections

Rust affecting chassis rails, floor strength or load-bearing areas can fail.

Suspension mounts

Corrosion around suspension mounting points is safety-critical.

Seatbelt mounts

Seatbelt anchorages must remain strong, secure and free from serious corrosion.

Brake pipes

Heavily corroded brake pipes or fittings can fail because they affect braking safety.

Floorpan holes

Holes may fail depending on severity, sharp edges and proximity to safety-related areas.

Major vs dangerous rust defects

Major corrosion defect

The car fails the MOT and needs repair before a pass certificate can be issued.

Dangerous corrosion defect

The car is considered a direct safety risk and should not be driven until repaired.

If rust has weakened a structural area or mounting point, do not assume the vehicle is safe just because it still drives normally.

Can rust MOT failures be repaired?

  • 1Welding repairs are common for structural rust.
  • 2Replacement panels or repair sections may be needed.
  • 3Hidden corrosion can make the job larger once cutting begins.
  • 4Repairs must restore strength, not just cover the hole.
  • 5Poor plating, filler or underseal over weak metal may still fail.
  • 6A retest is normally needed after repair.

Likely rust repair costs in the UK

Small local repair

May be moderate cost if access is easy and corrosion is limited.

Sill welding

Can vary widely depending on inner sill condition and paint finishing.

Brake pipe replacement

Cost depends on pipe routing, corrosion and labour access.

Subframe corrosion

May require replacement rather than patch repair on some vehicles.

Structural welding

Can become expensive if mounting points or floor sections are affected.

Cosmetic finishing

Paint and bodywork finish can add cost beyond the MOT repair itself.

Is it worth fixing a car that failed MOT on rust?

It depends on the car’s value, mileage, service history, overall mechanical condition and how widespread the corrosion is. A small local repair on a good car may be worthwhile. Widespread rust on a low-value car can become uneconomical quickly.

Before approving repairs, ask whether there is hidden corrosion, whether more welding may be found, and whether the finished repair is likely to last more than one MOT cycle.

What happens next after a rust MOT failure?

  • 1Read the MOT failure sheet and identify the exact corrosion location.
  • 2Ask the garage whether the defect is major or dangerous.
  • 3Get a repair quote from a garage or welder experienced with MOT corrosion repairs.
  • 4Check whether hidden rust may increase the quote once work starts.
  • 5Keep repair invoices and photographs if available.
  • 6Arrange the MOT retest after the corrosion repair is complete.

For retest timing and rules, read MOT Retest Rules UK.

How to prevent rust MOT failures

1. Treat advisories early

Corrosion advisories often become failures if ignored for another year.

2. Wash underneath

Removing winter salt and dirt can slow corrosion on older cars.

3. Check sills and jacking points

These areas often rust before the rest of the body looks bad.

4. Inspect brake pipes

Heavily corroded pipes are a common safety-related MOT problem.

5. Be cautious with underseal

Underseal can protect good metal, but it should not hide weak or holed metal.

6. Check MOT history

Repeated corrosion advisories are a warning sign when buying a used car.

Frequently asked questions

Can surface rust fail an MOT?

Usually not by itself, unless it affects structure, safety or creates dangerous sharp edges.

Can rusty sills fail MOT?

Yes, especially if the sill is holed, weakened or close to safety-related mounting points.

Can rust be repaired for MOT?

Yes, but repairs must restore strength properly. Covering holes with filler is not enough.

Is welding always worth it?

Not always. It depends on vehicle value, rust extent and overall condition.

Can I drive after failing on rust?

Not if the defect is dangerous. For major defects, only drive if legal and roadworthy.

Best next step?

Get the rust location inspected properly and compare repair cost with the car’s value.
