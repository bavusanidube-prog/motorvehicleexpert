Power Steering Warning Light Meaning

If the power steering warning light comes on, the car has detected a fault with the steering assistance system. The steering may still work, but it can become much heavier, especially at low speed, so the warning should not be ignored.

Quick answer

The power steering warning light usually means the steering assistance system has detected a fault. Depending on the vehicle, this may involve electric power steering, low battery voltage, a charging issue, a steering angle sensor, wiring, or hydraulic steering fluid on older systems.

If the steering suddenly becomes very heavy, jerky or unpredictable, stop somewhere safe and do not continue driving until the fault has been checked.

What does the power steering warning light mean?

Power steering reduces the effort needed to turn the wheel. Many modern cars use electric power steering, while some older vehicles use a hydraulic system with fluid, a pump and hoses.

When the warning light appears, the system may have reduced or disabled steering assistance. The car may still turn, but it can feel much harder to control, especially when parking, turning at junctions or making quick manoeuvres.

Common causes of a power steering warning light

Low battery voltage

Electric steering systems can trigger warnings if battery voltage is weak or unstable.

Alternator fault

Charging problems can affect voltage-sensitive systems, including electric steering assistance.

Electric steering fault

EPS motor, rack, column or control module faults can reduce steering assistance.

Steering angle sensor issue

Incorrect steering position data can trigger warnings or affect calibration.

Hydraulic fluid leak

On hydraulic systems, low fluid or leaks can make steering heavy and noisy.

Wiring or connector fault

Damaged wiring, poor connections or module faults can cause intermittent warnings.

Is it safe to drive with the power steering light on?

If the steering still feels normal and the warning has just appeared, a short careful journey may be possible. However, the fault should still be checked because steering assistance can fail suddenly or become inconsistent.

If the wheel becomes very heavy, the steering feels jerky, or the car feels difficult to control, stop driving and arrange inspection or recovery.

Less urgent

Light on, steering feels normal, no other warning lights.

More urgent

Steering feels heavier, warning keeps returning or battery light appears too.

Stop driving

Very heavy steering, jerky steering, unsafe control or multiple warnings.

What to check first

1. Notice steering feel

Check whether the steering is heavier than normal, especially at parking speed.

2. Check other warnings

Battery, alternator, ABS or traction warnings can help point towards the cause.

3. Think about battery history

A recent flat battery, jump start or weak battery can sometimes trigger steering warnings.

4. Check hydraulic fluid

If your car uses hydraulic power steering, check the correct fluid level and look for leaks.

5. Avoid forcing the steering

Do not repeatedly turn the wheel against resistance if the system feels faulty.

6. Arrange diagnosis

Fault-code reading and live data checks can identify sensor, module or voltage problems.

Possible UK repair costs

Battery or charging fault

Often lower to moderate cost if the steering unit itself is not faulty.

Sensor or calibration issue

May involve diagnostic work, calibration or sensor replacement.

EPS rack or control unit

Can become expensive, especially on newer or premium vehicles.

Because costs vary widely, diagnosis matters. A weak battery or charging fault is very different from a failed steering rack or control module.

Can a power steering warning light affect an MOT?

A steering-related warning and any underlying steering defect can affect the MOT outcome if the system is not working correctly or steering assistance is unsafe.

If the warning light is on before an MOT, it is sensible to diagnose it first rather than hoping it will clear during the test.

Frequently asked questions

Can a weak battery cause the power steering warning light?

Yes. Many electric power steering systems are sensitive to low voltage and charging problems.

Will the steering still work?

Usually yes, but without normal assistance the wheel may feel much heavier.

Can the warning clear on its own?

Sometimes, especially after a voltage issue, but recurring warnings should still be investigated.

Can I drive with heavy steering?

It is not recommended. Heavy steering can make the car difficult to control in traffic, at junctions or during emergency manoeuvres.

Can low power steering fluid cause the light?

On hydraulic systems, yes. Many newer cars use electric steering and do not have power steering fluid.

What is the best next step?

Check whether the steering feels normal, look for other warning lights, then arrange proper diagnosis if the warning remains or returns.
