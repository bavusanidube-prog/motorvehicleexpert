Steering warning light guide

Power Steering Warning Light Meaning

If the power steering warning light comes on, the car has detected a fault with the steering assistance system. The steering may still work mechanically, but it can become much heavier, jerky or unpredictable, especially when parking, turning at junctions or driving slowly.

Quick answer

The power steering warning light usually means the steering assistance system has detected a fault. On many modern cars this involves electric power steering, low battery voltage, charging issues, steering angle sensor faults, wiring problems or an EPS control module issue.

On older hydraulic steering systems, heavy steering may be caused by low power steering fluid, a leak, a worn pump, a slipping belt or damaged hoses.

If the steering suddenly becomes very heavy, jerky, noisy or difficult to control, stop safely and do not continue driving until the fault has been checked.

What does the power steering warning light mean?

Power steering reduces the effort needed to turn the steering wheel. When the warning light appears, the system may have reduced or disabled steering assistance.

The car may still steer, but it may feel much harder to turn, especially at low speeds. This can make parking, roundabouts, junctions, tight turns and emergency manoeuvres more difficult.

Light only

The system has detected a fault, even if the steering still feels normal.

Heavy steering

Assistance may be reduced or unavailable.

Multiple warnings

Battery, ABS, traction or steering lights together can suggest electrical or sensor issues.

Electric vs hydraulic power steering

The checks depend on which steering system your vehicle uses. Many modern cars use electric power steering, while some older vehicles use hydraulic assistance.

Electric power steering

Common causes include low voltage, alternator faults, EPS motor problems, steering angle sensor issues, wiring faults, control module problems or calibration errors.

Hydraulic power steering

Common causes include low fluid, leaks, worn pump, slipping belt, air in the system, damaged hoses or steering rack problems.

If your car has no power steering fluid reservoir, it may use electric power steering. Always check the vehicle handbook before topping up any fluid.

Is it safe to drive with the power steering light on?

If the steering still feels normal and the warning has just appeared, a short careful journey may be possible. However, the fault should still be checked because steering assistance can fail suddenly or become inconsistent.

If the wheel becomes very heavy, the steering feels jerky, or the car feels difficult to control, stop driving and arrange inspection or recovery.

Less urgent

Light on, steering feels normal, no other warning lights.

More urgent

Steering feels heavier, warning keeps returning or battery light appears too.

Stop driving

Very heavy steering, jerky steering, unsafe control or multiple warnings.

When to stop driving immediately

  • !The steering suddenly becomes very heavy.
  • !The steering assistance cuts in and out.
  • !The car is difficult to control at junctions or roundabouts.
  • !Steering feels jerky, loose, notchy or unpredictable.
  • !Battery warning light appears at the same time.
  • !ABS, ESP, traction or brake warning lights appear too.
  • !You hear grinding, knocking or whining when turning.
  • !Power steering fluid is leaking heavily on a hydraulic system.

Steering is safety-critical. If the car does not steer predictably, stop somewhere safe and arrange recovery rather than trying to continue.

What to check first

1. Notice steering feel

Check whether the steering is heavier than normal, especially at parking speed.

2. Check other warnings

Battery, alternator, ABS, ESP or traction warnings can help point towards the cause.

3. Think about battery history

A recent flat battery, jump start or weak battery can sometimes trigger steering warnings.

4. Check hydraulic fluid

If your car uses hydraulic power steering, check the correct fluid level when safe and look for leaks.

5. Avoid forcing the steering

Do not repeatedly turn the wheel against resistance if the system feels faulty.

6. Arrange diagnosis

Fault-code reading and live data checks can identify sensor, module, voltage or steering unit problems.

How garages diagnose power steering warnings

Diagnostic fault scan

Checks EPS, ABS, battery, steering angle and communication fault codes.

Battery and charging test

Confirms whether low voltage or alternator output is causing the warning.

Live steering data

Checks steering angle, torque sensor readings and assistance commands where available.

Calibration checks

Some steering angle sensors need recalibration after battery, suspension or steering work.

Fluid and leak check

Hydraulic systems are checked for fluid level, leaks, pump noise, belt condition and hose damage.

Rack, column and wiring inspection

Confirms whether the fault is mechanical, electrical or module-related.

Can a power steering warning light affect an MOT?

A steering-related warning light and any underlying steering defect can affect the MOT outcome if the system is not working correctly, the steering is unsafe or there is a serious power steering leak.

If the warning light is on before an MOT, it is sensible to diagnose it first rather than hoping it will clear during the test.

Best mechanic-style advice

Do not assume the steering rack has failed just because the power steering warning light is on. Low voltage, charging faults, sensor calibration and wiring problems can trigger similar symptoms.

Start with the basics: steering feel, battery condition, alternator output, other warning lights, fluid level if hydraulic, then diagnostic scan data. That approach avoids expensive guessing.

Frequently asked questions

Can a weak battery cause the power steering warning light?

Yes. Many electric power steering systems are sensitive to low voltage and charging problems.

Will the steering still work?

Usually yes, but without normal assistance the wheel may feel much heavier, especially at low speed.

Can the warning clear on its own?

Sometimes, especially after a voltage issue, but recurring warnings should still be investigated.

Can I drive with heavy steering?

It is not recommended. Heavy steering can make the car difficult to control in traffic, at junctions or during emergency manoeuvres.

Can low power steering fluid cause the light?

On hydraulic systems, yes. Many newer cars use electric steering and do not have power steering fluid.

Why did the power steering light come on after a jump start?

Low voltage, battery disconnection or voltage spikes can sometimes trigger steering warnings. If the light stays on, the battery, alternator and steering system should be checked.

Can a steering angle sensor cause this warning?

Yes. Steering angle sensor faults or calibration issues can trigger steering, ESP or traction-related warnings.

Can this fail an MOT?

Yes, if the steering system has a fault, assistance is not working correctly, the steering is unsafe or a serious leak is present.

What is the best next step?

Check whether the steering feels normal, look for other warning lights, then arrange proper diagnosis if the warning remains or returns.
