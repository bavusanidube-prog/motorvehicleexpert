Power Steering Warning Light Meaning

If the power steering warning light comes on, it usually means there is a fault affecting the steering assistance system. The car may still steer, but the wheel can become much heavier and the problem should not be ignored.

Quick Answer

What does the power steering warning light mean?

The warning light usually means the system that provides steering assistance has detected a fault. Depending on the vehicle, this may involve an electric power steering system, low system voltage, a steering angle issue or, on some older cars, a hydraulic steering problem.

If steering suddenly feels heavy, the warning should be treated seriously and the car should be checked as soon as possible.

Can you keep driving?

Is it safe to drive with the warning light on?

Sometimes you may be able to drive a short distance carefully, but only if the steering still feels controllable. If the steering becomes very heavy, unpredictable or difficult to turn, it is safer not to continue driving until the cause is checked.

Heavy steering can make low-speed manoeuvres and emergency control much harder.

Common causes

Why the power steering warning light may come on

The exact cause depends on the type of steering system the vehicle uses, but several faults appear more often than others.

Low battery voltage

Electric steering systems can trigger warning lights if battery voltage is weak or unstable.

Electric power steering fault

Modern vehicles often use EPS systems, and internal faults may reduce or remove steering assistance.

Steering angle or sensor issue

Faulty sensor readings can trigger the warning and affect system calibration.

Alternator or charging problem

Charging faults can affect voltage-sensitive systems, including electric steering assistance.

Hydraulic fluid issue

On some older cars, low power steering fluid or a leak may cause steering problems and warnings.

Wiring or control fault

Electrical connectors, modules or wiring issues can also trigger the warning light.

What to check first

Useful first checks

  • 1Notice whether the steering feels heavier than normal
  • 2Check for any battery or charging warning lights
  • 3Look for recent electrical issues or flat battery history
  • 4On hydraulic systems, check for fluid leaks if appropriate
  • 5Arrange proper diagnosis if the warning stays on
When to stop driving

Do not keep driving if:

  • !The steering suddenly becomes very heavy
  • !The steering feels inconsistent or jerky
  • !Other warning lights appear at the same time
  • !The car feels unsafe to control
Repair costs

Possible repair cost range in the UK

The cost varies widely depending on whether the problem is simple electrical diagnosis or a more expensive steering component fault.

Battery or charging issue

Can be relatively moderate if the problem is low voltage rather than the steering unit itself.

Sensor or diagnostic work

Often lower cost than replacing major steering components.

EPS rack or control unit fault

Can become expensive, especially on newer or premium vehicles.

FAQ

Common questions about the power steering warning light

Can a weak battery cause the power steering warning light?

Yes. Many electric steering systems are sensitive to low voltage and charging issues.

Will the steering still work?

Usually yes, but without normal assistance the wheel may feel much heavier.

Can the warning clear on its own?

Sometimes, but recurring warnings should still be investigated properly.

Is this an MOT failure?

A steering-related warning and any underlying steering defect can affect roadworthiness and MOT outcome depending on the exact fault.
