Can an Oil Leak Fail MOT?

Yes, an oil leak can fail an MOT in the UK if it is excessive, likely to harm the environment, contaminates braking components, or creates a serious safety risk. A light misting or minor seep is different from an active leak dripping oil.

Quick answer

A small oil mist or old staining does not automatically mean MOT failure. However, a significant oil leak, fresh dripping leak, or leak affecting brakes, steering or suspension components can cause a fail.

If the underside of the vehicle is heavily coated in fresh oil, fix the leak before the MOT rather than hoping it will pass.

When an oil leak can fail an MOT

  • !Excessive leak: active leak with obvious fresh oil loss.
  • !Environmental risk: likely to drip onto the road surface.
  • !Brake contamination: oil on discs, pads, hoses or braking parts.
  • !Steering or suspension contamination: leak affecting critical components.
  • !Smoke or fire risk: oil leaking onto hot exhaust parts.

Minor dampness around seals is different from a serious leak. Severity and risk matter.

Common engine oil leak sources

Rocker cover gasket

Common seep point on older engines.

Oil filter housing

Loose filter or failed seal can leak after servicing.

Sump gasket

Leaks from the bottom of the engine.

Drain plug washer

Can leak after an oil change if damaged.

Turbo oil feed or return

More common on turbocharged engines.

Crankshaft seals

Often more labour-intensive to repair.

Minor seep vs active leak

Minor seep

Slight dampness, old residue, no dripping and oil level remains stable.

Active leak

Fresh oil drops, wet underside, burning smell or noticeable oil loss between checks.

Often advisory

Small seeps may be noted depending on condition and severity.

Higher fail risk

Leaks onto brakes, suspension, road surface or hot exhaust parts are more serious.

Pre-MOT checks for oil leaks

  • 1Check your driveway or parking space for fresh oil spots.
  • 2Inspect the engine bay for wet oil around covers and seals.
  • 3Check engine oil level regularly.
  • 4Clean old residue after repairs so fresh leaks are easier to identify.
  • 5Watch for smoke or a burning oil smell after driving.
  • 6Repair active leaks before the MOT.

Can washing the engine help?

Cleaning oily residue may help inspection visibility, but it will not cure an active leak. If fresh oil returns quickly, the fault still needs repair.

Fixing the source is always better than trying to hide symptoms.

Typical UK repair costs

Simple gasket leak

Often moderate cost depending on access.

Filter housing or seals

Usually mid-range cost.

Major seal repair

Can be expensive if major components need removal.

Repair cost often depends more on labour time than the price of the seal itself.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small oil leak fail MOT?

Not always. Minor seepage is different from a serious active leak.

Can oil on brakes fail MOT?

Yes. Contaminated braking components are a serious issue.

Will an oil leak get an advisory?

Sometimes, depending on severity and condition.

Can I drive with an oil leak?

Depends on severity, but it should be diagnosed quickly.

Can an oil leak cause smoke?

Yes, especially if leaking onto hot exhaust parts.

Should I repair before MOT?

Yes, active leaks are best fixed before the test.
