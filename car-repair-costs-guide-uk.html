What affects repair cost?

Why the final bill can vary so much

  • 1Vehicle make, model, age and engine type
  • 2Labour time and how easy the part is to access
  • 3Dealer, specialist or independent garage labour rates
  • 4Genuine, OEM-quality or budget replacement parts
  • 5Whether extra faults are found during diagnosis or repair
Smarter cost decisions

How to approach repair costs more sensibly

1. Confirm the fault

Do not assume the first suspected part is definitely the cause without checks or diagnosis.

2. Compare quotes

Ask what parts are included, whether VAT is included and if extra labour may be added.

3. Judge urgency

Some repairs can wait briefly, while others affect safety, charging, cooling or reliability.

4. Think beyond price

The cheapest quote is not always the best if diagnosis is weak or poor-quality parts are used.
