Car Overheating: What To Do

If your car is overheating, pull over safely and switch the engine off as soon as possible. Continued driving can cause severe engine damage. Common causes include low coolant, coolant leaks, radiator problems, thermostat faults, failed water pumps, cooling fan problems and internal engine faults.

Quick answer

If the temperature gauge rises high, steam appears, or an overheating warning light comes on, pull over safely and switch the engine off. Let the engine cool before opening the bonnet fully or checking coolant.

Do not continue driving if the temperature stays high, coolant is leaking, steam appears, the heater blows cold while the engine is hot, or the warning returns after topping up.

What to do if your car overheats

1. Pull over safely

Move away from traffic where possible. Do not sit in traffic while the gauge keeps climbing.

2. Switch the engine off

Stopping the engine helps prevent further heat build-up and reduces damage risk.

3. Let it cool fully

Wait before checking anything. Never remove the coolant cap while the engine is hot.

4. Check coolant level

Only when cool, inspect the expansion tank level against the minimum and maximum marks.

5. Look for leaks

Check for puddles, wet hoses, steam, staining, dried coolant residue or a sweet smell.

6. Arrange diagnosis

Recurring overheating needs proper repair before normal driving continues.

What not to do

  • !Do not keep driving with the gauge in the red.
  • !Do not open the coolant cap while hot.
  • !Do not pour cold water onto a hot engine.
  • !Do not assume topping up coolant fixes the root cause.
  • !Do not ignore repeated overheating after short journeys.
  • !Do not drive far if the heater blows cold while the engine is hot.

Common causes of car overheating

Low coolant level

Coolant loss from leaks or poor maintenance can quickly cause overheating.

Signs your car is overheating

  • ✓Temperature gauge rising higher than normal.
  • ✓Steam from the bonnet area.
  • ✓Coolant warning light or dashboard message.
  • ✓Sweet coolant smell.
  • ✓Loss of power or rough running.
  • ✓Heater stops blowing warm air.
  • ✓Coolant puddle, wet hoses or dried coolant residue.
  • ✓Fan running constantly or not running at all.

Car overheats in traffic

If overheating happens mainly in traffic, at idle or in slow queues, cooling fan faults are a common suspect. The radiator needs airflow, and when the car is not moving, the fan becomes much more important.

Likely clues

Temperature rises in traffic but drops once moving again.

Common checks

Fan operation, relay, fuse, radiator blockage and coolant level.

Read more: car overheats in traffic and engine overheating when idle.

Car overheats while driving or at motorway speed

If overheating happens at higher speeds, uphill or under load, the cause may be coolant loss, a blocked radiator, thermostat fault, water pump weakness or an internal engine issue.

Likely clues

Temperature rises during sustained load, motorway driving or long climbs.

Common checks

Coolant flow, leaks, radiator condition, thermostat, water pump and pressure issues.

Read more: engine overheating while driving.

Why overheating is serious

Severe overheating can damage the head gasket, warp cylinder head components, weaken seals, split hoses and shorten engine life. A small coolant leak can become a major repair if the car is driven while hot.

Stopping early is usually far cheaper than trying to continue the journey.

Possible repair costs

Coolant top-up or cap

Usually lower cost, but only if there is no deeper leak or overheating problem.

Hose or radiator leak

Can range from modest to more expensive depending on parts and access.

Thermostat or water pump

Cooling fan fault

Radiator replacement

Usually more than a hose repair because parts and labour are higher.

Head gasket damage

Can become very expensive and is one reason overheating should not be ignored.

Frequently asked questions

Can I drive a car that is overheating?

Only long enough to stop safely. Continued driving can cause severe engine damage.

Can low coolant cause overheating?

Yes. Low coolant is one of the most common causes and often means there is a leak or cooling-system issue.

Why does my car overheat only in traffic?

Cooling fan or radiator airflow issues are common suspects when overheating happens in traffic or at idle.

Can I add water?

Only when the engine is cool. Proper coolant mix is better than plain water, but water may help in an emergency to reach safety.

Is overheating expensive to fix?

Sometimes no, sometimes yes. A hose leak is very different from head gasket damage, which is why stopping early matters.

Should I call recovery?

Yes, if the gauge stays high, steam appears, coolant leaks badly or overheating returns after topping up.
