Car Overheating: What To Do

If your car is overheating, pull over safely and switch the engine off as soon as possible. Continued driving can cause severe engine damage. Common causes include low coolant, radiator leaks, thermostat faults, failed water pumps, cooling fan problems and internal engine faults.

Quick answer

If the temperature gauge rises high, steam appears, or an overheating warning light comes on, pull over safely and switch the engine off. Let the engine cool before opening the bonnet fully or checking coolant.

Do not continue driving unless the temperature returns to normal immediately and you are confident the issue is minor. If overheating returns, arrange recovery or repair.

Common causes of car overheating

Low coolant level

Coolant loss from leaks or poor maintenance can quickly cause overheating.

Coolant leak guide →

Radiator problem

A leaking, blocked or damaged radiator may not remove enough heat.

Thermostat fault

A stuck thermostat can stop coolant circulating correctly.

Water pump issue

If the pump cannot move coolant, engine temperature can rise quickly.

Cooling fan failure

Often most noticeable in traffic, at idle or after stopping.

Cooling fan guide →

Head gasket issue

Internal coolant loss, pressure problems or white smoke can point to serious faults.

Signs your car is overheating

  • ✓Temperature gauge rising higher than normal
  • ✓Steam from the bonnet area
  • ✓Coolant warning light or dashboard message
  • ✓Sweet coolant smell
  • ✓Loss of power or rough running
  • ✓Heater stops blowing warm air
  • ✓Coolant puddle or wet hoses

What to do if your car overheats

1. Pull over safely

Move away from traffic where possible.

2. Switch the engine off

Prevent further heat build-up and reduce damage risk.

3. Let it cool fully

Never remove the coolant cap while the engine is hot.

4. Check coolant level

Only once cool, inspect the expansion tank level.

5. Look for leaks

Check for puddles, wet hoses, staining or coolant residue.

6. Arrange diagnosis

Recurring overheating needs proper repair before normal use.

What not to do

  • !Do not keep driving with the gauge in the red.
  • !Do not open the coolant cap while hot.
  • !Do not pour cold water onto a hot engine.
  • !Do not assume topping up coolant fixes the root cause.
  • !Do not ignore repeated overheating after short journeys.

Car overheats in traffic

If overheating happens mainly in traffic, at idle or in slow queues, cooling fan faults are a common suspect. The radiator needs airflow, and when the car is not moving, the fan becomes much more important.

Likely clues

Temperature rises in traffic but drops once moving again.

Common checks

Fan operation, relay, fuse, radiator blockage and coolant level.

Car overheats at motorway speed

If overheating happens at higher speeds, uphill or under load, the cause may be coolant loss, a blocked radiator, thermostat fault, water pump weakness or an internal engine issue.

Likely clues

Temperature rises during sustained load or long climbs.

Common checks

Coolant flow, leaks, radiator condition, thermostat and pressure issues.

Why overheating is serious

Severe overheating can damage the head gasket, warp cylinder head components, weaken seals and shorten engine life. A small coolant leak can become a major repair if the car is driven while hot.

Stopping early is usually far cheaper than trying to continue the journey.

Frequently asked questions

Can I drive a car that is overheating?

Only long enough to stop safely. Continued driving can cause severe damage.

Can low coolant cause overheating?

Yes. Low coolant is one of the most common causes.

Why does my car overheat only in traffic?

Cooling fan or radiator airflow issues are common suspects.

Can I add water?

Only when the engine is cool. Proper coolant mix is better than plain water.

Is overheating expensive to fix?

Sometimes no, sometimes yes. A hose leak is very different from head gasket damage.

Should I call recovery?

Yes, if the gauge stays high, steam appears, coolant leaks badly or overheating returns.
