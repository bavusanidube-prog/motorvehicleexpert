Cooling system repair guide

Coolant Leak Repair Cost UK

Coolant leak repair in the UK can cost from around £80 for a simple hose or clip to £700+ for larger repairs such as a water pump, radiator or hard-to-access leak. The final price depends on where the leak is, how much labour is needed and whether overheating has already caused further damage.

Quick answer

How much does coolant leak repair cost?

Most coolant leak repairs fall between £80 and £700+. A loose hose clip or split pipe is usually at the lower end. A radiator, thermostat housing, expansion tank or water pump normally costs more because parts and labour increase.

If the car has already overheated, the bill can rise sharply. See our guide to what to do if your car overheats.

Do not ignore it

Repeated coolant loss means there is a fault

Coolant should not need regular topping up. If the level keeps dropping, the car may have an external leak, a pressure cap issue, a heater matrix problem or a more serious internal engine fault.

A sweet smell after driving can also point to coolant loss. Read more in our guide to why a car smells like coolant.

Common causes

Where coolant leaks usually come from

  • 1Split rubber coolant hoses or loose hose clips
  • 2Cracked radiator core or plastic radiator end tanks
  • 3Leaking thermostat housing or gasket
  • 4Water pump seal failure
  • 5Cracked expansion tank or weak coolant cap
  • 6Heater matrix leak inside the cabin
Symptoms

Signs your car has a coolant leak

  • 1Coolant warning light on the dashboard
  • 2Engine temperature rising or overheating
  • 3Sweet smell from the engine bay or cabin
  • 4Pink, orange, blue or green puddle under the car
  • 5Heater stops blowing hot air
  • 6You need to top up coolant regularly

If the heater is not getting warm, this may also be linked to low coolant. See car heater not blowing hot air.

Driving advice

Can you drive with a coolant leak?

Small slow leak

A short journey may be possible if the coolant level is correct and the temperature stays normal.

Active leak or steam

Do not continue driving. The engine can overheat quickly and suffer serious damage.

Temperature gauge rising

Stop safely, switch off the engine and let it cool before checking anything.

If your car overheats mainly when stationary, read car overheats in traffic and engine overheating when idle.

Diagnosis

How garages find coolant leaks

A garage may pressure test the cooling system, inspect hoses and the radiator, check for dried coolant residue, test the expansion tank cap and look for signs of internal leakage.

Some leaks only appear when the engine is hot and the system is under pressure, so a quick visual check when cold may not always find the fault.

Prevention

How to reduce the risk of coolant leaks

Keep the cooling system serviced, use the correct coolant type, check levels regularly and deal with small leaks before they become major failures.

FAQ

Coolant leak repair questions

Can coolant leaks stop on their own?

No. A coolant leak usually gets worse over time, especially once the system heats up and builds pressure.

Can I use coolant leak sealer?

Leak sealer may be a temporary measure for a minor leak, but it is not a proper long-term repair.

Why does coolant disappear with no puddle?

It may evaporate on hot parts, leak only while driving, escape through the cap, enter the heater system or leak internally.

Is overheating always caused by a leak?

No. Faulty fans, thermostat problems, water pump failure or blocked radiators can also cause overheating.
