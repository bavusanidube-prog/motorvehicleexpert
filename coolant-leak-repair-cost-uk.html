Coolant Leak Repair Cost UK

Coolant leak repair in the UK can cost from around £80 for a simple hose or clip to £700+ for larger repairs such as a water pump, radiator, heater matrix or hard-to-access leak. The final price depends on where the leak is, how much labour is needed and whether overheating has already caused further damage.

Quick answer

Most coolant leak repairs in the UK fall between £80 and £700+. A loose clip, split hose or pressure cap is usually cheaper. A radiator, thermostat housing, water pump, heater matrix or internal engine fault usually costs more.

Coolant should not need regular topping up. If the level keeps dropping, there is usually a leak, pressure fault, heater system issue or internal cooling-system problem that needs checking.

Where coolant leaks usually come from

  • 1Split rubber coolant hoses or loose hose clips.
  • 2Cracked radiator core or plastic radiator end tanks.
  • 3Leaking thermostat housing, seal or gasket.
  • 4Water pump seal failure or pump housing leak.
  • 5Cracked expansion tank or weak coolant pressure cap.
  • 6Heater matrix leak inside the cabin.
  • 7Metal coolant pipe corrosion or hidden pipework leaks.
  • 8Internal leak from a head gasket or engine fault.

Signs your car has a coolant leak

  • !Coolant warning light on the dashboard.
  • !Engine temperature rising or overheating.
  • !Sweet smell from the engine bay or cabin.
  • !Pink, orange, blue or green puddle under the car.
  • !Heater stops blowing hot air.
  • !You need to top up coolant regularly.
  • !Steam from under the bonnet.
  • !Dried white, pink, orange or blue residue around hoses or the expansion tank.

A sweet smell after driving can also point to coolant loss. Read car smells like coolant.

Losing coolant but no visible puddle

Not every coolant leak leaves a puddle on the driveway. Coolant can escape only when the engine is hot, evaporate on hot parts, collect on the undertray or leak into the heater system.

Only leaks when hot

Pressure can open small leaks that are not visible when the engine is cold.

Evaporates on hot parts

Coolant can smell strongly without leaving much liquid on the ground.

Internal loss

More serious faults can send coolant into the engine or exhaust system.

Can you drive with a coolant leak?

A very small leak may allow a short journey if the coolant level is correct and the temperature stays normal. But this is still a risk, because coolant leaks often get worse once the system is hot and pressurised.

Do not keep driving if the leak is active, the coolant warning light appears, the temperature gauge rises, steam appears, or the heater blows cold while the engine is hot.

Small slow leak

A short careful journey may be possible if coolant level and temperature are normal.

Active leak or steam

Do not continue driving. The engine can overheat quickly.

Temperature gauge rising

Stop safely, switch off the engine and let it cool before checking anything.

How garages find coolant leaks

A garage may pressure test the cooling system, inspect hoses and the radiator, check for dried coolant residue, test the expansion tank cap and look for signs of internal leakage.

Some leaks only appear when the engine is hot and the system is under pressure, so a quick visual check when cold may not always find the fault.

Pressure test

Helps reveal leaks by pressurising the cooling system while the engine is cool.

Visual inspection

Looks for staining, crusty residue, wet hoses, radiator leaks and tank cracks.

Cap test

Checks whether the coolant cap holds pressure properly.

Internal leak checks

Used if there are signs of head gasket or combustion gas problems.

When to stop driving immediately

  • !The coolant warning light comes on and stays on.
  • !The temperature gauge moves towards the red.
  • !Steam comes from the engine bay.
  • !Coolant is pouring out or leaving a large puddle.
  • !The heater blows cold while the engine is hot.
  • !The engine runs rough, knocks or loses power.

If any of these happen, stop safely and let the engine cool. Do not remove the coolant cap while hot.

How to avoid a bigger repair bill

Coolant leaks are often cheaper when fixed early. Waiting until the car overheats can turn a simple hose, cap or radiator job into head gasket, cylinder head or engine damage.

  • ✓Check coolant level regularly when the engine is cold.
  • ✓Investigate repeated top-ups instead of ignoring them.
  • ✓Use the correct coolant type for the vehicle.
  • ✓Do not rely on leak sealer as a permanent repair.
  • ✓Fix overheating, heater and coolant smell symptoms early.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a coolant leak repair cost in the UK?

Coolant leak repair often costs between £80 and £700+, depending on whether the leak is from a hose, radiator, thermostat housing, expansion tank, water pump, heater matrix or internal engine fault.

Can coolant leaks stop on their own?

No. A coolant leak usually gets worse over time, especially once the system heats up and builds pressure.

Can I use coolant leak sealer?

Leak sealer may be a temporary measure for a minor leak, but it is not a proper long-term repair.

Why does coolant disappear with no puddle?

It may evaporate on hot parts, leak only while driving, escape through the cap, enter the heater system or leak internally.

Is overheating always caused by a leak?

No. Faulty fans, thermostat problems, water pump failure or blocked radiators can also cause overheating.

Can a coolant leak damage the engine?

Yes. If coolant loss causes overheating, it can lead to head gasket damage, cylinder head damage and expensive repairs.
