Can Exhaust Mount Fail MOT?

Yes, an exhaust mount can fail an MOT if it is broken, missing, insecure or allows the exhaust system to hang loose, move excessively or risk detaching. Exhaust mounts and hangers support the exhaust safely underneath the vehicle.

Quick answer

An exhaust mount can fail an MOT if the exhaust is not adequately supported, is likely to detach, knocks against the vehicle, hangs too low, or creates a safety risk.

A small worn rubber hanger may only be an advisory if the exhaust is still secure, but a broken or missing mount that leaves the exhaust loose should be repaired before the test.

When an exhaust mount can fail an MOT

Broken rubber hanger

A torn or detached rubber hanger may leave the exhaust unsupported.

Loose metal bracket

A bracket that is loose, cracked or missing can make the system insecure.

Excessive movement

The exhaust should not swing, knock or move excessively under the car.

Risk of detachment

An exhaust section that could drop, drag or fall off is a serious issue.

Related exhaust leak

Movement can crack joints, loosen clamps or create a blowing exhaust.

Unsafe positioning

A hanging exhaust may sit too close to bodywork, wiring, hoses or the road.

Signs an exhaust mount may be bad

  • !Rattling or knocking from underneath the car.
  • !Noise gets worse over bumps or rough roads.
  • !Tailpipe sits lower than normal or looks uneven.
  • !Exhaust looks loose when gently moved by hand while cold.
  • !Sudden louder exhaust noise after a knock or pothole.
  • !Visible broken rubber hanger, clamp or bracket.

Pre-MOT exhaust mount checks

1. Look at the tailpipe

Check whether it hangs unusually low, sits crooked or moves more than normal.

2. Listen for rattles

A metallic rattle or knocking underneath often points to loose mounts, brackets or heat shields.

3. Inspect rubber hangers

Look for split, stretched, missing or detached rubber supports.

4. Check clamps and brackets

Rusty clamps, loose bolts or broken brackets can make the exhaust insecure.

5. Check for blowing noise

Loose mounts can put stress on joints and lead to exhaust leaks.

6. Repair obvious looseness

A visibly hanging or unstable exhaust should be fixed before the MOT.

Should you repair an exhaust mount before MOT?

Yes, if the exhaust is loose, hanging, knocking or visibly unsupported. Exhaust hangers and simple brackets are often inexpensive compared with replacing damaged pipework, silencers or joints later.

Fixing a mount early can prevent exhaust leaks, unwanted noise, road contact and extra strain on the rest of the system.

Can a loose exhaust become dangerous?

Yes. A loose exhaust can strike the underside of the car, drag on the road, damage nearby parts or detach completely. It can also create leaks that may allow exhaust fumes to enter the cabin.

If the exhaust is hanging low or making loud knocking noises, avoid delaying repair.

Frequently asked questions

Will one broken exhaust hanger fail MOT?

It can if the exhaust is insecure, hanging loose or likely to detach.

Can a loose exhaust fail MOT?

Yes. The exhaust system must be securely supported and safe.

Are rubber exhaust mounts cheap to fix?

Often yes, although cost depends on access, corrosion and vehicle design.

Can bad mounts cause a blowing exhaust?

Yes. Excessive movement can stress joints, clamps and pipework.

Can a rattling exhaust pass MOT?

It depends on the cause. If the system is insecure or likely to detach, it can fail.

Should I repair it before the test?

Yes, if there is visible looseness, hanging parts or knocking underneath.
