Can Lower Arm Fail MOT?

Yes, a lower arm can fail an MOT in the UK if it is bent, cracked, badly corroded, insecure or has worn bushes or ball joints causing excessive movement. Lower arms are key suspension and steering alignment components.

Quick answer

A lower arm may fail an MOT if bushes are excessively worn, the ball joint has play, the arm is bent or cracked, or corrosion has weakened the component.

Minor wear may sometimes receive an advisory, but unsafe movement or structural damage usually leads to failure.

Common reasons a lower arm can fail MOT

Worn bushes

Split or soft bushes allowing excessive suspension movement.

Ball joint play

Movement in the joint can affect steering safety.

Bent arm

Often caused by potholes, kerbs or accident damage.

Cracks or fractures

Structural damage can be a serious fail item.

Heavy corrosion

Rust weakening the arm or mounting points.

Loose fixings

Insecure bolts or failed mountings.

Signs a lower arm may be worn

  • !Knocking noises over bumps.
  • !Steering feels vague or unstable.
  • !Vehicle pulls to one side.
  • !Uneven front tyre wear.
  • !Clunk when braking or accelerating.
  • !Visible split bushes or rust damage.

What to check before the MOT

1. Listen for knocks

Suspension knocks over bumps often point to worn joints or bushes.

2. Check tyre wear

Uneven wear can suggest suspension or alignment issues.

3. Inspect visible bushes

Look for obvious cracking, splitting or separation.

4. Check steering feel

Loose or wandering steering can be a warning sign.

5. Look for rust damage

Inspect visible suspension arms and mounting areas.

6. Repair faults early

Ignoring wear can damage tyres and other suspension parts.

Should you repair a lower arm before MOT?

Yes. Lower arm faults can affect steering control, braking stability and tyre wear. Repairing them before the test can avoid failure and improve safety.

Many modern vehicles use complete replacement arms with pre-fitted bushes and ball joints, which can be more practical than replacing individual parts.

Typical UK repair costs

Bush replacement

Varies depending on whether bushes are replaceable separately.

Complete arm

Often the common repair on many cars.

Wheel alignment

Usually recommended after suspension work.

Can you drive with a worn lower arm?

Some minor wear may not feel severe at first, but it can quickly worsen and affect handling or tyre wear.

If knocking, looseness or poor steering is obvious, have it inspected as soon as possible.

Frequently asked questions

Will worn bushes fail MOT?

Yes, if wear causes excessive movement or insecurity.

Can a bent lower arm fail MOT?

Yes, especially if alignment or strength is affected.

Does a lower arm affect tyre wear?

Yes, worn suspension parts can cause uneven tyre wear.

Can rusted lower arms fail?

Yes, if corrosion weakens the component.

Should I replace both sides?

Not always required, but sometimes sensible depending on wear and mileage.

Do I need alignment afterwards?

Usually yes, wheel alignment is recommended after suspension repairs.
