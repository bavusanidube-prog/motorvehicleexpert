Car Shakes at High Speed

If your car feels smooth around town but starts shaking at motorway speed, the cause is often linked to wheels, tyres, balancing, alignment, suspension, wheel bearings or brake components. High-speed vibration should be checked before it gets worse.

Quick answer

A car shaking at high speed is commonly caused by wheel imbalance, tyre damage, uneven tyre wear, bent wheels, poor alignment, worn suspension parts, wheel bearing faults or brake disc issues.

If the vibration is strong, getting worse, joined by noise, or felt when braking, arrange inspection promptly.

Common causes of high-speed shaking

Wheel imbalance

A very common reason vibration appears above certain speeds.

Tyre damage

Flat spots, bulges or internal tyre faults can cause shaking.

Uneven tyre wear

Worn or feathered tyres can create vibration and noise.

Bent wheel

Pothole damage can make the car shake at speed.

Alignment issue

Poor tracking can cause wandering, tyre wear and vibration.

Suspension wear

Worn bushes, joints or arms can feel worse at motorway speeds.

Wheel bearing fault

Often joined by humming, droning or rumbling noises.

Brake disc issue

Usually most obvious when braking from higher speed.

Loose wheel fitting

Less common but urgent if suspected.

Where you feel the shake matters

Steering wheel shake

Often points to front wheel balance, front tyres, steering or front suspension.

Seat or body shake

May point more toward rear wheels, rear tyres or general wheel balance.

Brake pedal vibration

Often linked to brake discs or braking components.

Vibration plus humming

Wheel bearings or tyre wear may be involved.

Does it shake only when braking?

If the car is mostly smooth while cruising but shakes when braking from high speed, the fault may be brake-related rather than wheel balance. Common suspects include brake disc runout, uneven disc deposits, worn pads or suspension movement under braking load.

Read Car Shakes When Braking if the vibration mainly appears when slowing down.

When high-speed shaking needs urgent attention

  • !The vibration appears suddenly after hitting a pothole.
  • !The steering wheel shakes violently.
  • !You hear knocking, grinding or rumbling noises.
  • !The car pulls to one side or feels unstable.
  • !A tyre bulge, cut or exposed cord is visible.
  • !You suspect a loose wheel or damaged wheel fitting.

What to check next

1. Check tyre pressures

Incorrect pressure can worsen vibration and tyre wear.

2. Inspect tyres

Look for uneven wear, bulges, cuts, flat spots or visible damage.

3. Check when it starts

A vibration that appears at a certain speed often points to balance.

4. Notice where it is felt

Steering wheel, seat or brake pedal clues help narrow the cause.

5. Book wheel balancing

Balancing and tyre inspection are common first steps.

6. Inspect suspension

If balancing does not fix it, check joints, bushes and bearings.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car shake only at high speed?

Wheel balance, tyre damage or rotating component faults often become obvious only at higher speeds.

Can wheel balancing fix it?

Often yes, especially if the vibration starts at a specific speed.

Can tyres cause high-speed shaking?

Yes. Uneven wear, bulges, flat spots or internal tyre damage can cause vibration.

Why does it shake when braking?

That often points to brake discs, pads or suspension movement under braking load.

Is high-speed vibration dangerous?

It can be, especially if severe, sudden or linked to tyres, wheels or steering parts.

Best first step?

Inspect tyres and wheels, then check wheel balance and suspension if needed.
