Used Car Test Drive Checklist UK

A used car can look great while parked but reveal faults once driven. Use this practical UK used car test drive checklist to check the clutch, gearbox, brakes, steering, engine, suspension and warning lights before buying.

Before the test drive

  • 1Check the engine is cold if possible before startup.
  • 2Look for leaks under the car.
  • 3Check tyres for low tread or uneven wear.
  • 4Check warning lights illuminate then go out.
  • 5Adjust mirrors and seating properly before driving.
  • 6Confirm insurance cover for the drive.

During the test drive

Engine

Should pull smoothly without smoke, misfire or hesitation.

Clutch

Should engage smoothly without slip or judder.

Gearbox

Gears should shift smoothly without crunching.

Brakes

Should stop straight without grinding or vibration.

Steering

Should feel stable with no pulling or shaking.

Suspension

Listen for knocks or rattles over bumps.

Temperature

Watch the gauge for overheating signs.

Noise

Listen for whining, knocking or wheel bearing hum.

Warning lights

No warning lights should remain on.

Manual car test drive checks

  • ✓Check clutch bite point is not very high.
  • ✓Accelerate in a higher gear for clutch slip signs.
  • ✓Ensure reverse selects cleanly.
  • ✓Check pull-away is smooth without judder.

Automatic car test drive checks

  • ✓Gear changes should be smooth, not harsh.
  • ✓Reverse should engage quickly.
  • ✓No hesitation pulling away.
  • ✓No gearbox warning lights.

Used car test drive red flags

  • !Seller refuses a test drive.
  • !Engine already warm when you arrive.
  • !Warning lights stay on.
  • !Smoke, overheating or burning smells.
  • !Heavy clutch slip or rough gearbox.
  • !Pulling brakes or steering vibration.

After the drive

Check for leaks

Fresh oil or coolant leaks may appear warm.

Smell the car

Burning oil, clutch or coolant smells matter.

Review calmly

Do not let a seller rush your decision.

Walk away if unsure

There are always more cars available.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a test drive be?

Long enough to test town speeds, braking and a faster road if possible.

Should I drive from cold?

Yes if possible. Cold starts can reveal faults.

Can I buy without a test drive?

Usually not recommended unless strong buyer protection exists.

Should warning lights stay on?

No. Most warning lights should go out after startup checks.
