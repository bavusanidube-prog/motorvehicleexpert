Car Clicking Noise When Turning

A clicking noise when turning is often linked to a worn CV joint, damaged CV boot, loose suspension part, steering linkage wear or wheel-related fault. The timing, speed and direction of the noise can help narrow down the cause.

Quick answer

Repeated clicking on full-lock turns, especially at low speed, is commonly caused by a worn outer CV joint. If there is grease around the wheel arch or driveshaft area, a split CV boot may have let grease escape.

Do not ignore clicking that is getting louder, happens on one side, or is joined by vibration, knocking or steering changes.

Common reasons a car clicks when turning

Worn outer CV joint

A classic cause of rhythmic clicking on tight turns or full lock.

Split CV boot

Grease loss allows dirt into the joint and can quickly cause wear.

Loose suspension joint

Bushes, ball joints or links can click or knock as load shifts.

Steering linkage wear

Track rod ends or steering joints can make noises during direction changes.

Wheel fitting issue

Loose wheel bolts, trims or fittings can create clicking under load.

Tyre scrub

At full lock, some tyres make scrub noises that can be mistaken for clicking.

CV joint clicking symptoms

A CV joint transfers drive while the front wheels steer. When the outer joint wears, it often clicks most clearly during slow, tight turns, such as parking, reversing out of a driveway or turning at junctions.

  • 1Repeated clicking while turning left or right.
  • 2Noise worse on full lock.
  • 3Grease visible near the wheel, arch liner or suspension.
  • 4Clicking becomes louder over time.
  • 5Vibration or knocking if wear becomes severe.

What to check first

1. Notice when it clicks

Full-lock parking, reversing or low-speed turns are useful clues.

2. Identify the side

Clicks louder when turning one way can help locate the affected joint.

3. Look for grease

Grease splattered around the inner wheel area often points to CV boot failure.

4. Check wheel security

Do not ignore noises after recent tyre or wheel work.

5. Listen for heavier knocks

Knocking may suggest suspension, steering or mounting wear.

6. Book a front-end inspection

A ramp inspection can confirm CV, steering and suspension faults.

Can you drive with a clicking noise when turning?

If the click is mild and only just started, you may be able to drive carefully for a short time, but the vehicle should be inspected soon.

Avoid continuing to drive if the clicking is loud, vibration appears, steering feels loose, the car pulls badly, or there is visible grease from a split CV boot.

Typical UK repair costs

CV boot replacement

Often cheaper if caught before the joint wears out.

CV joint or driveshaft

Moderate cost depending on vehicle and parts design.

Suspension or steering joint

Costs vary by component and whether alignment is needed after repair.

Early inspection can reduce cost, especially if only the CV boot is damaged and the joint is still healthy.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car click on full lock?

A worn outer CV joint is one of the most common causes.

Can a split CV boot cause clicking?

Yes. If grease escapes, the CV joint can wear and start clicking.

Is clicking when turning dangerous?

It can become dangerous if linked to drivetrain, steering or suspension wear.

Can wheel bearings click when turning?

They more often hum or rumble, but wheel-related faults should still be checked.

Should I replace the whole driveshaft?

Sometimes, but diagnosis should confirm whether the boot, joint or shaft is needed.

Best next step?

Check for grease or split boots and book a front suspension/driveshaft inspection.
