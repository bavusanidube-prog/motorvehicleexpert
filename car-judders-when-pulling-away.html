Pull-away fault guide

Car Judders When Pulling Away

If your car shakes, shudders or feels uneven when moving off from a standstill, the cause may be clutch wear, engine mount movement, rough engine running, fuel faults or drivetrain issues. This guide explains common reasons and what UK drivers should check next.

Quick answer

A car that judders when pulling away is most commonly caused by clutch wear or clutch contamination, worn engine or gearbox mounts, rough engine running, low-speed fueling faults or drivetrain wear.

If the judder becomes severe, smells of clutch, or the car struggles to move off smoothly, have it checked soon.

Common causes of judder when moving off

Clutch wear

A worn clutch plate can grab unevenly as drive takes up.

Clutch contamination

Oil leaks onto the clutch can cause shuddering.

Engine mounts

Weak mounts allow excessive movement when pulling away.

Gearbox mounts

Mount wear can create vibration during take-up.

Engine misfire

Uneven low-speed running can feel like judder.

Fuel / throttle issue

Poor response at low revs can cause shaky pull-aways.

Dual mass flywheel wear

Some vehicles develop vibration as flywheels wear.

Driveshaft wear

Play in joints can be felt when first loading the drivetrain.

Cold running fault

Some cars judder more before warming up.

Manual or automatic?

Manual cars

Clutch, flywheel and mount faults are common causes.

Automatic cars

Torque converter, gearbox calibration or engine running faults may be involved.

When does the judder happen?

Only first gear

Often clutch related.

Only reverse

Clutch take-up issues can show strongly in reverse.

Cold only

Cold-running or mount stiffness may play a part.

Hill starts

Extra load can worsen clutch or mount faults.

With rough idle

Engine running faults become more likely.

Every take-off

Persistent mechanical wear is more likely.

When pull-away judder needs faster attention

  • !Burning clutch smell.
  • !Difficulty moving off on hills.
  • !Heavy shaking through the whole car.
  • !Loud knocks when taking drive.
  • !Judder worsening quickly.
  • !Warning lights or engine faults appear too.

What to check next

1. Notice when it occurs

Cold starts, hills, reverse or every pull-away all matter.

2. Check for clutch slip

Rising revs without speed increase suggests clutch wear.

3. Listen for knocks

Mount or drivetrain play can create noises.

4. Check idle quality

A rough idle may indicate engine misfire.

5. Avoid repeated abuse

Heavy slipping can worsen clutch damage.

6. Arrange inspection

A technician can road test and inspect mounts/clutch.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car judder pulling away?

Often clutch, mount or engine running related.

Can a worn clutch cause judder?

Yes. It is one of the most common causes.

Can engine mounts cause this?

Yes. Weak mounts can exaggerate movement.

Why is it worse on hills?

Extra load highlights clutch and mount faults.

Should I keep driving?

Mild cases may continue, but worsening judder should be checked.

Will diagnostics help?

Yes, especially if engine faults are also present.
