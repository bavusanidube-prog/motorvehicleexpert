Drivetrain fault guide

Car Revs But Won’t Accelerate

If the engine revs rise but the car does not gain speed properly, the issue is often not engine power itself. It usually points toward clutch slip, gearbox faults or another drivetrain problem preventing power from reaching the wheels correctly.

Quick answer

When revs increase but road speed does not match, the fault is commonly clutch slip on manual cars or transmission slip on automatics. Driveshaft, differential or reduced-power faults can also create similar symptoms.

Repeated slipping should not be ignored because it can quickly worsen and increase repair costs.

Common causes of revs rising without acceleration

Clutch slip

Very common on manual cars, especially under load or on hills.

Automatic gearbox slip

Revs flare while speed lags behind.

Worn transmission fluid

Low or degraded fluid can affect some automatics.

Driveshaft fault

Drive may not transfer properly to the wheels.

Differential issue

Less common, but possible on some vehicles.

Reduced-power mode

Sometimes feels like no acceleration rather than true slip.

Manual cars: is it clutch slip?

On manual cars, clutch slip is one of the most common explanations. Engine revs rise quickly but the vehicle does not accelerate in proportion.

  • ✓Worse in higher gears
  • ✓Worse uphill or with passengers
  • ✓Burning smell may appear
  • ✓Bite point may feel higher than normal

Automatic cars: possible transmission slip

Automatic cars may suffer from internal wear, fluid problems or control faults that allow revs to rise without matching drive.

Harsh shifts

May happen alongside slipping.

Delayed drive engagement

Selecting Drive or Reverse may feel slow.

Warning light

Transmission or engine warnings may appear.

Worse when hot

Some faults worsen after driving.

Helpful clues that narrow the cause

Only on hills

Load-related clutch or transmission slip is likely.

Only in one gear

May point to gearbox-specific issues.

Burning smell

Often linked to clutch overheating.

Noisy drivetrain

Could suggest shaft or differential faults.

Warning lights on

Electronic or engine faults may also be involved.

Sudden severe loss of drive

Needs urgent inspection.

What to do next

  • 1Notice whether revs rise faster than speed.
  • 2Check if it is worse uphill or in high gears.
  • 3Watch for warning lights or gearbox messages.
  • 4Avoid hard acceleration if slipping badly.
  • 5Arrange diagnosis promptly to limit damage.

Frequently asked questions

Can a worn clutch cause this?

Yes. It is one of the most common causes on manual cars.

Can an automatic gearbox slip?

Yes. Revs may rise while acceleration feels weak.

Should I keep driving?

Best avoided if slipping is severe or worsening.

Will it get worse?

Often yes, especially clutch or transmission faults.

Can engine faults feel similar?

Yes. Limp mode or misfires can mimic poor acceleration.

Best next step?

Get the drivetrain or transmission checked promptly.
