Used car buying guide

Used car inspection checklist UK

Buying a used car can save money, but hidden faults can be expensive. Use this practical UK used car inspection checklist to check condition, paperwork, warning signs and road behaviour before you buy.

Checks before the test drive

  • 1Inspect tyres for cracks, low tread, uneven wear or mismatched brands.
  • 2Check body panels for dents, rust, poor paint repairs or panel gaps.
  • 3Look underneath for oil, coolant or fuel leaks.
  • 4Check windows, mirrors, lights and locks work correctly.
  • 5Inspect interior wear against the stated mileage.
  • 6Switch ignition on and ensure warning lights illuminate then go out.

Important documents to check

  • ✓V5C logbook matches registration and seller details.
  • ✓Service history with invoices or stamped records.
  • ✓MOT history consistent with mileage and advisories.
  • ✓Spare keys included if advertised.
  • ✓VIN numbers match paperwork and vehicle plates.
  • ✓Outstanding finance / accident checks recommended.

Checks during the test drive

  • 1Listen for knocking, rattling or whining noises.
  • 2Check clutch bite point and smooth gear changes.
  • 3Watch for steering vibration or pulling to one side.
  • 4Test brakes for grinding, vibration or weak stopping power.
  • 5Watch engine temperature and dashboard warning lights.
  • 6Drive at town and faster road speeds if possible.

Mechanical red flags

Smoke from exhaust, overheating, poor idle, misfire, oil leaks or loud engine noise can indicate costly faults.

Paperwork red flags

Missing V5C, inconsistent mileage records, no history or seller reluctance can be warning signs.

Seller red flags

Pressure selling, refusal of test drive, meeting away from home address or vague answers should make you cautious.

How to inspect more confidently

1. View in daylight

Paint defects and dents are easier to spot.

2. Bring time

Do not rush because another buyer is mentioned.

3. Stay objective

Focus on condition, not excitement or appearance.

4. Walk away if unsure

There are always other cars available.

Best buyer mindset

A clean-looking used car is not always a good used car. Smart buying decisions come from checking evidence, condition and consistency.

If something feels wrong, paperwork is missing or faults are obvious, it is often better to walk away than force the purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Is 100k miles too much for a used car?

Not always. Service history and condition matter more than mileage alone.

Should I buy without a test drive?

Usually no. A test drive can reveal faults not visible while parked.

Can MOT history reveal problems?

Yes. Repeated advisories may show ongoing neglect.

Should I get an inspection?

For higher-value cars, an independent inspection can be worthwhile.
