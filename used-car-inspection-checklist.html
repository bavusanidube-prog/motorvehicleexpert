Used Car Inspection Checklist | Motor Vehicle Expert

A practical pre-purchase checklist to help buyers avoid obvious problems and make more confident decisions.

On this page

  • Before the test drive
  • During the test drive
  • Buyer mindset

Checks before the test drive

  • 1Inspect tyres for damage, uneven wear and poor quality matching.
  • 2Check body panels for gaps, mismatched paint or poor repair signs.
  • 3Look for fluid leaks under the engine and around the vehicle.
  • 4Check service history, receipts and MOT history where available.
  • 5Switch the ignition on and make sure warning lights behave normally.

Checks during the test drive

  • 1Listen for knocks, rattles, wheel bearing noise or suspension sounds.
  • 2Check clutch bite, gear change quality and pulling performance.
  • 3Watch for vibration through the steering or poor braking feel.
  • 4Check temperature behaviour and cabin warning lights.
  • 5Do not rush. A calm, methodical drive reveals more than a fast one.

Best buyer mindset

A clean-looking car is not always a good car. Good inspection habits are about condition, evidence and consistency. If anything feels off, it is usually better to walk away than force the purchase.
