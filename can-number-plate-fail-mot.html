MOT number plate guide

Can Number Plate Fail MOT?

Yes, a number plate can fail an MOT in the UK if it is missing, dirty, damaged, insecure, incorrectly spaced, difficult to read, the wrong colour, or not illuminated correctly by the rear number plate lights.

Quick answer

A number plate can fail an MOT if the registration cannot be read clearly, the plate is badly damaged, loose, incorrectly spaced, uses an illegal font, has the wrong background colour, or the rear plate lights do not illuminate it properly.

This is usually one of the easier MOT issues to fix. Cleaning the plate, replacing a failed bulb, securing the plate or fitting a new legal plate can often solve the problem before the test.

Common reasons number plates fail an MOT

Dirty or obscured plate

Mud, grime, covers or accessories can make the registration hard to read.

Cracked or damaged plate

Cracks, peeling, delamination or missing pieces can affect readability.

Incorrect spacing

Personalised plates with altered spacing are a common MOT problem.

Illegal font or styling

Novelty fonts, altered characters or decorative screws can make a plate illegal.

Wrong colour

Front plates should be white and rear plates should be yellow.

Loose or insecure plate

A number plate should be firmly attached and not likely to fall off.

Spacing, font and personalised plates

Personalised number plates can fail when letters or numbers are moved, squeezed together, spaced out, or styled to look like a word or name. Even if the plate looks readable to you, it can still fail if the layout is not legal.

  • !Do not move letters or numbers closer together.
  • !Do not use decorative screws to change how characters look.
  • !Do not use novelty fonts, italics or heavily stylised characters.
  • !Do not use tinted covers or smoked plates that reduce visibility.
  • !Make sure the plate is easy to read from a normal viewing distance.

Can number plate lights fail MOT?

Yes. Rear number plate lights are checked because the registration must be visible at night. If the bulb is out, the lamp is damaged, the wiring has failed, or the rear plate is not lit clearly, it can cause an MOT failure.

Easy check

Turn the lights on and check that the rear number plate is evenly illuminated.

Common fix

A failed number plate bulb is often quick and inexpensive to replace.

Watch for water

Moisture inside the lamp can damage bulbs, holders or wiring.

Check both sides

Some cars use more than one bulb, so make sure the whole plate is visible.

Pre-MOT number plate checks

  • 1Clean both front and rear number plates.
  • 2Check for cracks, peeling, delamination, fading or missing sections.
  • 3Make sure both plates are firmly attached.
  • 4Check that spacing, font and character layout are standard and legal.
  • 5Remove tinted covers, novelty screws or anything that makes characters harder to read.
  • 6Turn the lights on and check the rear number plate lamps work.
  • 7Replace damaged, faded or illegal plates before the MOT.

Should you replace a damaged number plate before MOT?

If the plate is cracked, peeling, unreadable, badly faded, incorrectly spaced or loose, replacing it before the MOT is usually the simplest option.

Number plates and number plate bulbs are normally cheaper to fix than many other MOT failures, so they are worth checking early.

Frequently asked questions

Can a dirty number plate fail MOT?

Yes, if dirt or grime makes the registration difficult to read.

Can a cracked number plate fail MOT?

Yes, especially if the crack affects the characters or makes the plate hard to read.

Can private plates fail MOT?

Yes. Private plates can fail if spacing, font, layout or visibility is not legal.

Can number plate lights fail MOT?

Yes. The rear registration plate must be properly illuminated.

Can tinted number plate covers fail MOT?

They can, especially if they make the registration harder to read.

Should I replace plates before MOT?

Yes, if they are damaged, illegal, faded, insecure or difficult to read.
