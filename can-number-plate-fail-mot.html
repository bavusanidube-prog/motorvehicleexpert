MOT number plate guide

Can Number Plate Fail MOT?

Yes, a number plate can fail an MOT in the UK if it is missing, dirty, cracked, faded, insecure, incorrectly spaced, hard to read, the wrong colour, illegally styled or not illuminated correctly by the rear number plate lights. From a mechanic’s point of view, the main question is simple: can the registration be read clearly and legally from the front and rear of the vehicle?

This is one of the easiest MOT failures to avoid. Many number plate problems come from dirt, loose fixings, faded plates, private plate spacing, damaged acrylic or failed rear plate bulbs.

Quick answer

Will a number plate fail an MOT?

A number plate can fail an MOT if the registration cannot be read clearly, the plate is cracked or badly faded, the characters are incorrectly spaced, the font is illegal, the plate is loose, or the rear number plate lights do not illuminate it properly.

Minor dirt may simply need cleaning, but a badly damaged, illegal or unreadable plate should be replaced before the MOT.

Mechanic view

Why number plates matter

The MOT is not only checking whether the plate exists. The plate must be secure, readable, correctly displayed and illuminated at the rear. If the registration is difficult to read because of damage, styling, dirt or poor lighting, it can become a fail item.

Failure points

Common reasons number plates fail an MOT

These are the faults to check before presenting the car for test.

Readability

Dirty or obscured plate

Mud, grime, snow, bike racks, tow bars, accessories or covers can make the registration hard to read.

Damage

Cracked or faded plate

Cracks, peeling, fading, delamination, missing pieces or damaged reflective backing can affect visibility.

Spacing

Incorrect spacing

Private plates often fail when letters or numbers are moved, squeezed together or spaced out to look like words.

Illegal font or styling

Novelty fonts, altered characters, decorative screws or unusual styling can make a plate illegal.

Wrong colour

The front plate should be white and the rear plate should be yellow. Incorrect colours can fail.

Loose number plate

The plate should be secure and not likely to fall off, flap around or detach while driving.

Private plates

Spacing, fonts and private number plates

Private number plates can fail when the characters are altered to make a name, word or phrase. Even if the plate looks readable to you, it can still be an MOT issue if the layout is not legal or the registration is not displayed correctly.

The safest approach before an MOT is simple: use standard spacing, standard font, clear characters and no tricks with screws, badges or covers.

Avoid these

Common private plate mistakes

  • !Moving characters closer together to spell a word.
  • !Using screws or caps to change how letters look.
  • !Using novelty fonts, italic text or stylised characters.
  • !Fitting tinted covers that reduce visibility.
  • !Using badges, borders or designs that make the plate unclear.
Plate lights

Can number plate lights fail an MOT?

The rear registration must be illuminated clearly when the lights are switched on.

Bulbs

Failed plate light bulb

If the rear number plate is not lit clearly at night, the vehicle can fail the MOT.

Lens

Broken or dirty lamp lens

A damaged, cloudy or dirty plate light lens can reduce the light reaching the plate.

Wiring

Moisture or wiring fault

Water ingress, corrosion or wiring damage can stop number plate lamps from working properly.

Easy check

Turn the sidelights on and check that the rear plate is clearly illuminated.

Common fix

A failed bulb is often quick and inexpensive to replace before the MOT.

Whole plate visible

The full registration should be readable, not just one side of the plate.

Pre-MOT checks

Pre-MOT number plate checks

These simple checks can prevent an avoidable MOT failure.

1

Clean both plates

Wipe the front and rear plates so the registration is clear and easy to read.

2

Check for cracks and fading

Look for damaged acrylic, peeling backing, faded characters or missing plate sections.

3

Check plate security

Make sure both plates are firmly attached and not loose, rattling or hanging off.

4

Check spacing and font

The characters should look standard, clear and properly spaced, especially on private plates.

5

Remove covers or obstructions

Remove anything that makes the registration harder to read, including tinted covers or accessories.

6

Check rear plate lights

Turn the lights on and check the rear number plate is illuminated clearly.

Repair advice

Should you replace a damaged number plate before MOT?

Yes, if the plate is cracked, faded, delaminated, unreadable, incorrectly spaced, loose or illegal. Replacing a bad plate before the MOT is usually easier than risking a failure.

Number plates and bulbs are normally inexpensive compared with many other MOT repairs, so this is one of the better faults to sort early.

One plate enough

Can one number plate fail the whole MOT?

Yes. A serious defect with either the front or rear number plate can fail the MOT. The rear plate also needs working illumination, so check the plate lights as well as the plate itself.

Do not assume the rear plate is fine just because the front plate looks good.

Real-world faults

Number plate problems drivers often miss

Small details can be the difference between a pass and a simple avoidable fail.

Rear plate light out

Many drivers never check the rear of the vehicle with the lights switched on.

Private plate spacing

A plate can look clever but still be illegal if the spacing is changed.

Decorative screws

Screws or caps can make characters look like different letters or numbers.

Cracked acrylic

A crack may seem small until it affects a letter or allows water into the plate.

Loose rear plate

Boot lids and tailgates can loosen plate fixings over time.

Tinted plate cover

Anything that reduces readability can become a problem at test time.

FAQs

Number plate MOT questions

Common questions about dirty plates, cracked plates, private plates, spacing and plate lights.

Can a number plate fail an MOT?

Yes. A plate can fail if it is missing, dirty, damaged, loose, incorrectly spaced, illegal or hard to read.

Can a dirty number plate fail MOT?

Yes, if dirt, mud or grime makes the registration difficult to read.

Can a cracked number plate fail MOT?

Yes, especially if the crack affects visibility, security or the appearance of the registration.

Can private plates fail MOT?

Yes, if the spacing, font, layout or styling is illegal or makes the registration hard to read.

Can number plate lights fail MOT?

Yes. The rear number plate must be illuminated clearly when the lights are on.

Can tinted covers fail MOT?

They can if they reduce readability or make the registration difficult to see clearly.

Can a loose number plate fail MOT?

Yes. A number plate should be securely fixed and not likely to detach.

Should I replace plates before MOT?

Yes, if they are damaged, faded, loose, illegal, incorrectly spaced or hard to read.
