MOT number plate guide

Can Number Plate Fail MOT?

Yes, a number plate can fail an MOT in the UK if it is missing, dirty, damaged, insecure, incorrectly spaced, hard to read, the wrong colour, or not illuminated correctly by the rear number plate lights.

Quick answer

A number plate can fail an MOT if the registration cannot be read clearly, the plate is cracked or badly faded, incorrectly spaced, uses an illegal font, is loose, or the rear plate lights do not illuminate it properly.

This is often one of the easiest MOT problems to fix. Cleaning the plate, replacing a bulb, securing the plate or fitting a legal replacement can solve it before the test.

Common reasons number plates fail an MOT

Dirty or obscured plate

Mud, grime, bike racks or accessories can hide the registration.

Cracked or damaged plate

Cracks, peeling, fading or missing pieces can affect readability.

Incorrect spacing

Altered spacing on private plates is a common issue.

Illegal font or styling

Novelty fonts, altered characters or decorative screws can fail.

Wrong colour

Front plates should be white and rear plates yellow.

Loose plate

The plate should be secure and not likely to detach.

Spacing, font and private plates

Private number plates can fail when letters or numbers are moved, squeezed together, spaced out or styled to spell words. Even if readable to you, it may still be illegal.

  • !Do not move characters closer together.
  • !Do not use screws to alter how letters look.
  • !Do not fit novelty fonts or stylised text.
  • !Do not use tinted covers that reduce visibility.
  • !Keep the registration easy to read clearly.

Can number plate lights fail MOT?

Yes. Rear number plate lights are checked because the registration must be visible at night. If bulbs fail, the lamp is broken, or the plate is not clearly lit, it can fail the MOT.

Easy check

Turn sidelights on and inspect rear plate illumination.

Common fix

Failed bulbs are often quick and inexpensive to replace.

Watch for moisture

Water ingress can damage bulbs or wiring.

Check whole plate

The full registration should be readable at night.

Pre-MOT number plate checks

  • 1Clean both front and rear plates.
  • 2Check for cracks, fading or peeling.
  • 3Make sure both plates are firmly attached.
  • 4Check spacing and font look standard.
  • 5Remove covers or anything obscuring characters.
  • 6Turn lights on and check rear plate lamps work.
  • 7Replace damaged or illegal plates before the MOT.

Should you replace a damaged number plate before MOT?

If the plate is cracked, unreadable, badly faded, incorrectly spaced or loose, replacing it before the MOT is usually the easiest solution.

Number plates and bulbs are normally inexpensive compared with many other MOT repairs.

Can one plate fail the whole MOT?

Yes. A serious defect with either the front or rear number plate can result in failure.
