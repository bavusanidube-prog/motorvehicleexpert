How much does clutch replacement cost in the UK?

Clutch replacement cost in the UK varies depending on the vehicle, labour time, parts quality and whether related components such as the dual-mass flywheel, slave cylinder or hydraulic system also need attention.

Typical clutch replacement cost in the UK

As a rough guide, many clutch replacements can cost from around £350 to £900+, depending on the vehicle and garage labour rate. Some small cars may be cheaper, while larger vehicles, performance cars, vans and cars with difficult gearbox access can cost more.

If a dual-mass flywheel also needs replacing, the total price can rise significantly because the flywheel part itself can be expensive and is usually replaced while the gearbox is already removed.

What affects clutch replacement cost?

  • 1Vehicle make and model: some cars are much harder to work on than others.
  • 2Labour time: gearbox removal is often the largest part of the job.
  • 3Parts quality: cheaper clutch kits may not offer the same durability as better-quality parts.
  • 4Dual-mass flywheel condition: a worn flywheel can add a large extra cost.
  • 5Hydraulic components: slave cylinders, master cylinders or concentric slave cylinders may need replacement.
  • 6Garage labour rate: prices vary by area, workshop type and specialist knowledge.

What should be included in a proper clutch quote?

  • ✓Clutch kit, usually including friction plate, pressure plate and release bearing.
  • ✓Labour for gearbox removal and refitting.
  • ✓Advice on flywheel condition where relevant.
  • ✓Hydraulic component advice, especially if the slave cylinder is inside the gearbox housing.
  • ✓Any gearbox oil, bolts, clips or related parts required.
  • ✓VAT and a clear total price.

Clutch kit only

Usually cheaper, but only suitable if the flywheel and hydraulic parts are in good condition.

Clutch and flywheel

Often more expensive, but sensible if the dual-mass flywheel is worn, noisy, loose or heat damaged.

Hydraulic parts

If the slave cylinder sits inside the gearbox, replacing it during clutch work can prevent repeat labour later.

Best advice before spending money

Make sure the fault really is clutch-related before approving replacement. Poor gear changes, judder, lack of power or pedal problems can sometimes be caused by hydraulic faults, gearbox issues, engine mounts, flywheel wear or engine performance problems.

A good garage should explain why the clutch is suspected, what parts are included, whether the flywheel is likely to need attention and whether additional costs may appear once the gearbox is removed.

Signs you may need a new clutch

  • ✓Engine revs rise without matching acceleration.
  • ✓Bite point feels unusually high.
  • ✓Burning smell after hills, traffic or heavy clutch use.
  • ✓Difficulty selecting gears.
  • ✓Judder when moving off.
  • ✓Car struggles to pull uphill or under load.

Frequently asked questions

Does every clutch job include a flywheel?

No. It depends on the vehicle design and flywheel condition. However, many modern diesels and higher-torque vehicles use dual-mass flywheels that may need checking carefully.

Can I keep driving with a worn clutch?

Only cautiously and for as little as possible if symptoms are worsening. A slipping clutch can deteriorate quickly and may eventually stop transferring power.

Should I choose the cheapest clutch quote?

Not automatically. Diagnosis, parts quality, warranty, labour details and whether the quote includes VAT all matter.

Why is clutch replacement labour expensive?

The gearbox often has to be removed to access the clutch, which can take several hours depending on the vehicle.

Can hydraulic faults feel like clutch failure?

Yes. Master cylinders, slave cylinders, fluid leaks or air in the system can affect pedal feel and gear engagement.
