Clutch Replacement Cost UK

Clutch replacement in the UK usually costs between £350 and £900+, depending on the vehicle, labour time, parts quality and whether the flywheel or hydraulic components also need replacing.

Typical clutch replacement cost in the UK

For many UK drivers, clutch replacement costs somewhere between £350 and £900+. Small hatchbacks are often cheaper, while larger vehicles, vans and premium models can cost more.

  • £Small car: £350–£600
  • £Family car: £500–£800
  • £Large SUV / van / premium car: £700–£1,200+
  • £Clutch + flywheel: £900–£1,500+

Prices vary by region, garage labour rates and how difficult the gearbox is to remove.

What affects clutch replacement cost?

  • 1Vehicle make and model: some cars take far longer to dismantle than others.
  • 2Labour time: removing the gearbox is often the biggest part of the bill.
  • 3Parts quality: premium clutch kits can cost more but may last longer.
  • 4Flywheel condition: a worn dual-mass flywheel can add significant extra cost.
  • 5Hydraulic parts: slave cylinders or master cylinders may also need attention.
  • 6Garage rates: labour charges vary across the UK.

How long does clutch replacement take?

Most clutch replacements take between 3 and 8 hours, depending on the vehicle.

Some small cars are relatively straightforward, while others require driveshaft removal, subframe movement or extra dismantling to access the gearbox.

This is why two garages may quote very different prices for the same repair.

Clutch kit only

Usually suitable when the flywheel is in good condition and there are no hydraulic issues.

Clutch and flywheel

If the dual-mass flywheel is noisy, loose or damaged, replacing it at the same time is often sensible.

Hydraulic components

Some vehicles use an internal slave cylinder. Replacing it during clutch work can avoid paying labour twice later.

Signs you may need a new clutch

  • ✓Engine revs rise without matching acceleration.
  • ✓High clutch bite point.
  • ✓Burning smell after traffic or hill starts.
  • ✓Difficulty selecting gears.
  • ✓Judder when pulling away.
  • ✓Loss of pulling power uphill.

Read more in our guide to clutch wear signs.

What should a clutch quote include?

  • ✓Clutch kit (friction plate, pressure plate and release bearing).
  • ✓Labour for gearbox removal and refitting.
  • ✓Advice on flywheel condition.
  • ✓Any hydraulic parts required.
  • ✓Gearbox oil or related consumables if needed.
  • ✓VAT and a clear final total.

Frequently asked questions

Can I drive with a slipping clutch?

Only for as little as possible. A slipping clutch can fail completely and leave the car unable to drive properly.

Does every clutch need a flywheel?

No. It depends on the vehicle design and flywheel condition.

Why is clutch labour expensive?

The gearbox usually has to be removed, which can take several hours.

How long should a clutch last?

Many clutches last well over 60,000 miles, but lifespan depends heavily on driving style and traffic conditions.

Should I choose the cheapest quote?

Not automatically. Check parts quality, warranty and whether VAT is included.
