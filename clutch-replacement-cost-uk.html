Clutch Replacement Cost UK

Clutch replacement in the UK usually costs between £350 and £900+. The final price depends on the car, labour time, clutch kit quality and whether the flywheel, slave cylinder or other related parts also need replacing.

Quick answer: how much is a clutch replacement?

A typical clutch replacement costs around £350 to £900+ in the UK. Smaller manual hatchbacks are usually at the lower end, while larger cars, vans, SUVs and premium models often cost more because they take longer to dismantle.

If the car also needs a dual-mass flywheel, the total can rise to around £900 to £1,500+. This is why it is important to ask exactly what is included before accepting a quote.

Typical clutch replacement cost in the UK

These are realistic guide prices for many UK vehicles. Exact costs vary by model, engine layout, parts brand, garage labour rate and whether extra parts are needed.

  • £Small car: usually around £350 to £600.
  • £Family car: usually around £500 to £800.
  • £Large SUV, van or premium car: often around £700 to £1,200+.
  • £Clutch and dual-mass flywheel: often around £900 to £1,500+.

What affects clutch replacement cost?

  • 1Vehicle make and model: some cars are much harder to access than others.
  • 2Labour time: removing and refitting the gearbox is usually the biggest part of the bill.
  • 3Clutch kit quality: better-known parts brands may cost more but can be worth it.
  • 4Flywheel condition: a worn dual-mass flywheel can add a large extra cost.
  • 5Hydraulic components: master cylinders, slave cylinders and clutch fluid can affect the quote.
  • 6Garage labour rate: prices vary between independent garages, specialists and main dealers.
  • 7Related damage: oil leaks, worn mounts or damaged gearbox parts can add to the final bill.

How long does clutch replacement take?

Most clutch replacements take between 3 and 8 hours. Straightforward small cars can be quicker, while larger vehicles and complicated layouts take longer.

The gearbox usually has to be removed to access the clutch. On some cars this may involve removing driveshafts, moving the subframe, disconnecting hydraulic parts or removing extra components around the engine bay.

This is why two garages can quote different prices for the same job. One may be quoting a basic clutch kit only, while another may include a slave cylinder, flywheel inspection, better parts or more labour time.

Clutch kit only

A clutch kit normally includes the friction plate, pressure plate and release bearing. This may be enough if the flywheel and hydraulics are in good condition.

Clutch and flywheel

If the dual-mass flywheel is worn, noisy, loose or heat damaged, replacing it with the clutch is often sensible because the gearbox is already removed.

Hydraulic components

Some cars use an internal slave cylinder. Replacing it during clutch work can prevent paying for the same labour again if it fails later.

How much does a flywheel add to the cost?

A dual-mass flywheel can add several hundred pounds to a clutch job. The extra cost depends on the vehicle and the price of the flywheel itself. Labour may not increase as much if it is replaced at the same time as the clutch, because the gearbox is already out.

Common flywheel warning signs include rattling at idle, vibration, rough clutch engagement, knocking when switching the engine off or judder when pulling away. If the car judders as you move off, read car judders when pulling away.

Signs you may need a new clutch

A clutch rarely fails without warning. Most drivers notice changes in the way the car pulls away, accelerates or changes gear before the clutch stops working properly.

  • ✓Engine revs rise without matching acceleration.
  • ✓High clutch bite point.
  • ✓Burning smell after traffic, hill starts or reversing.
  • ✓Difficulty selecting gears.
  • ✓Judder or shaking when pulling away.
  • ✓Loss of pulling power uphill.
  • ✓Clutch pedal feels heavy, soft or inconsistent.

For a full symptom guide, read signs your clutch is wearing out. If the engine revs but the car does not accelerate properly, see car revs but won’t accelerate.

Can you drive with a slipping clutch?

You should avoid driving with a slipping clutch except for a short journey to a garage. When a clutch slips, it creates heat and wears the friction material faster. The worse the slip becomes, the more likely the car is to lose drive completely.

Be especially careful if the car struggles uphill, smells of burning clutch or the revs flare under acceleration. For related symptoms, see car loses power uphill and car smells like burnt clutch.

What should a clutch quote include?

Before booking the repair, ask the garage what is included. A cheap quote may not be the best value if it excludes parts, VAT, hydraulic components or flywheel advice.

  • ✓Clutch kit, including friction plate, pressure plate and release bearing.
  • ✓Labour for gearbox removal and refitting.
  • ✓Flywheel inspection and advice.
  • ✓Slave cylinder or master cylinder replacement if required.
  • ✓Gearbox oil, bolts, seals or other consumables if needed.
  • ✓VAT included in the final total.
  • ✓Parts and labour warranty details.

Is it worth replacing the clutch on an older car?

It depends on the car’s value, condition and how long you plan to keep it. If the vehicle is otherwise reliable, a clutch replacement may be worthwhile. If it also needs major suspension, engine, MOT or corrosion work, compare the repair cost with the value of the car.

Frequently asked questions

Can I drive with a slipping clutch?

Only for as little as possible. A slipping clutch can quickly get worse and may leave the car unable to drive.

Does every clutch replacement need a flywheel?

No. It depends on the vehicle and the condition of the flywheel. However, if a dual-mass flywheel is worn, replacing it at the same time can save labour later.

Why is clutch labour expensive?

The gearbox usually has to be removed, which can take several hours. Access is much harder on some vehicles than others.

How long should a clutch last?

Many clutches last well over 60,000 miles, but lifespan depends heavily on driving style, traffic, towing, hills and how often the clutch is slipped.

Should I choose the cheapest clutch quote?

Not automatically. Check parts quality, warranty, VAT, whether the slave cylinder is included and whether the flywheel will be inspected.

Can a clutch fail suddenly?

Yes, although most clutches give warning signs first. Bad slipping, sudden pedal changes or difficulty selecting gears should be checked quickly.
