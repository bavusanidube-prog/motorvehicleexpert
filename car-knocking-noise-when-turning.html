Car Knocking Noise When Turning

A knocking noise when turning can come from worn suspension parts, CV joints, steering components, strut top mounts, wheel bearings or loose fittings. The timing of the noise helps narrow down the cause.

Quick answer

If your car knocks when turning, the most common causes are worn CV joints, suspension bushes, ball joints, strut top mounts, steering joints or wheel-related movement.

A repeated knock should not be ignored because steering and suspension faults can worsen and may affect safety, tyre wear and MOT results.

Common causes of knocking when turning

CV joint wear

Often causes clicking or knocking on full lock, especially while accelerating slowly.

Worn suspension bushes

Can knock as the car changes direction or loads one side.

Ball joint wear

May cause clunks, loose steering feel or uneven tyre wear.

Strut top mount

Can pop, creak or knock as the steering turns.

Track rod end wear

Can cause knocking, steering play or poor alignment.

Wheel bearing issue

Usually more of a hum, but turning can change the noise.

Loose wheel or fitting

A loose wheel, damaged nut or poor fitting can be dangerous and needs urgent checking.

Anti-roll bar link

Often knocks over bumps and may be more noticeable while turning.

Tyre scrub

Some low-speed scrubbing on full lock can sound unusual but may not be a fault.

Knocking noise on full lock

A knocking or clicking noise on full lock often points toward the outer CV joint, especially on front-wheel-drive cars. It may be louder when turning into a parking space or pulling away with the steering fully turned.

  • ✓Noise worse when accelerating while turning.
  • ✓Clicking or repetitive knocking from one front corner.
  • ✓Split CV boot or grease around the wheel area.

Low-speed knocking when parking or manoeuvring

Knocking at low speed can be caused by suspension joints, steering links, top mounts or anti-roll bar links. These parts move most when the car is turning, braking or crossing uneven ground.

Knock over bumps

Often points toward suspension links, bushes or ball joints.

Knock while steering stationary

Can suggest top mount, steering rack or power steering-related issues.

What to check next

  • 1Notice whether it happens on full lock, gentle turns, bumps or braking.
  • 2Check if the noise comes from the left or right side.
  • 3Look for split CV boots or grease around the wheel area.
  • 4Check for uneven tyre wear, steering vibration or pulling.
  • 5Do not ignore knocking that gets louder or more frequent.
  • 6Get the front suspension and steering inspected if the noise repeats.

Can knocking when turning affect MOT?

Yes, depending on the cause. Worn suspension joints, excessive play, broken springs, loose steering parts, wheel bearing play or unsafe wheel fittings can all create MOT problems.

Useful related pages: Can Suspension Fail MOT? and Can Wheel Bearing Fail MOT?

Can you drive with a knocking noise when turning?

If the noise is occasional and light, the car may still drive, but it should be checked soon. If the steering feels loose, the car pulls, the noise is loud, or the wheel area feels unstable, avoid driving until inspected.

A loose wheel or badly worn steering/suspension component can be dangerous.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car knock when turning left?

It may be a loaded suspension, CV joint or wheel component on one side. The direction can help a mechanic narrow it down.

Why does it knock on full lock?

Full-lock knocking often points toward CV joint wear, especially if it clicks repeatedly while moving.

Can suspension bushes knock?

Yes. Worn bushes can knock as the suspension moves and loads shift during cornering.

Is a knocking noise dangerous?

It can be. Steering, suspension and wheel faults should be inspected if the noise repeats.

Can wheel bearings knock when turning?

Wheel bearings more often hum or rumble, but turning can change the sound if a bearing is worn.

Should I get it checked before MOT?

Yes. Knocking can point to MOT-relevant steering, suspension or wheel defects.
