MOT Warning Light Guide

Can ABS Light Fail MOT?

Yes, an ABS warning light can fail an MOT in the UK if it stays on and shows a fault with the anti-lock braking system. The car may still have normal braking, but the ABS safety function may not work correctly in an emergency.

Quick answer

If the ABS warning light stays on after startup, comes on while driving, or remains active during the MOT test, the vehicle can fail.

The ABS light should normally come on briefly when the ignition is switched on, then go out after the system check. If it stays on, the system has detected a fault.

Why the ABS light can fail an MOT

ABS helps prevent the wheels locking under heavy braking. If the warning light is on, the MOT tester may treat it as evidence that the anti-lock braking system has a fault.

Safety system fault

The ABS may not work correctly during emergency braking.

Warning lamp active

An illuminated ABS warning light usually means the system has detected a fault.

Brake control issue

The car may still brake, but anti-lock control may be reduced or disabled.

Common reasons the ABS light comes on

  • 1Faulty wheel speed sensor.
  • 2Damaged, dirty or corroded ABS reluctor ring.
  • 3Broken, corroded or loose sensor wiring.
  • 4Low battery voltage or charging fault.
  • 5ABS pump, hydraulic unit or control module fault.
  • 6Brake system faults linked to stability or traction control systems.

Can you drive with the ABS light on?

In many cases, the normal brakes may still work, but the anti-lock braking function may not. This means the wheels may be more likely to lock during heavy braking, especially on wet, icy or loose surfaces.

  • !Drive cautiously if the ABS light is on.
  • !Leave more braking distance than usual.
  • !Do not ignore any brake warning light, fluid warning or poor brake feel.
  • !Arrange diagnosis before the MOT rather than clearing the light temporarily.

What to do before the MOT

1. Scan the ABS system

A normal engine code reader may not access ABS faults. Use a scanner that can read brake system codes.

2. Identify the affected wheel

Many ABS faults relate to one wheel speed sensor or wiring circuit.

3. Inspect wiring and rings

Damaged sensor wiring, corrosion or cracked reluctor rings are common causes.

4. Repair the fault properly

Simply clearing the warning light often does not fix the underlying problem.

ABS light and other warning lights

ABS faults can sometimes appear with traction control, stability control or brake warning lights. If several lights appear together, the issue may still be caused by one sensor or wiring fault, but proper diagnosis is needed.

If the brake warning light is also on, read brake warning light on while driving because that can be more urgent.

Frequently asked questions

If the ABS light comes on then goes off, is that normal?

Yes. It is normal for the ABS light to appear briefly during the startup check and then go out.

Will an ABS light always fail an MOT?

If the ABS warning light stays on and shows an active fault, it can fail the MOT.

Will my normal brakes still work with the ABS light on?

Often yes, but the anti-lock function may not work correctly. The car may be less stable during hard braking.

Can a wheel speed sensor cause the ABS light?

Yes. A faulty wheel speed sensor or damaged sensor wiring is one of the most common causes.

Can low battery voltage trigger the ABS light?

Yes. Low voltage or charging problems can sometimes trigger ABS and stability control warnings.

Should I clear the ABS light before the MOT?

Clearing the light without fixing the fault is not a proper repair. The warning may return and the underlying braking system fault may still be present.
