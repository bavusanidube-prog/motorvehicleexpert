A full car service in the UK usually costs around £180 to £350 for many everyday cars, but prices vary depending on vehicle size, oil specification, filters, labour rate, service schedule and whether you use a main dealer, specialist or independent garage.

Quick answer: how much is a full car service?

Most UK drivers can expect a full service to cost around £180 to £350 at many independent garages. Small petrol cars are often cheaper, while larger diesels, SUVs, hybrids, performance cars and premium models can cost more.

The cheapest quote is not always the best value. A proper full service should use the correct oil, suitable filters and include meaningful checks, not just a basic oil change with a quick look around.

Typical full car service cost in the UK

These are guide prices only. The final bill depends on the car, the garage, the oil specification, filters required and whether extra service items are due.

What is usually included in a full service?

A full service is more detailed than a basic oil change. The exact checklist varies by garage and manufacturer schedule, but it should cover the main maintenance, fluid, safety and wear items.

  • ✓Engine oil replacement using the correct grade and specification.
  • ✓Oil filter replacement.
  • ✓Air filter check or replacement depending on the schedule.
  • ✓Cabin or pollen filter check or replacement depending on the quote.
  • ✓Fluid level checks, including coolant, brake fluid, screenwash and power steering where applicable.
  • ✓Brake inspection, including visible pad, disc, pipe and fluid condition.
  • ✓Tyre tread, tyre pressure and visible tyre condition checks.
  • ✓Lights, wipers, horn, mirrors and washer checks.
  • ✓Battery and charging system checks where included.
  • ✓Visual inspection for leaks, wear, corrosion, damage and safety concerns.

What affects the cost of a full service?

  • 1Engine oil specification: some cars require expensive low-ash, fully synthetic or manufacturer-approved oil.
  • 2Oil quantity: larger engines often need more oil, which increases parts cost.
  • 3Filters included: oil, air, cabin, fuel and pollen filters can change the price significantly.
  • 4Garage labour rate: main dealers and specialists usually charge more than many independents.
  • 5Vehicle access: some cars take longer because filters, plugs or drain points are harder to reach.
  • 6Extra service items: spark plugs, brake fluid, fuel filters or gearbox oil may be due separately.
  • 7Location: labour rates vary across the UK.
  • 8Service history requirements: newer cars may need digital records, approved parts or manufacturer-specific checks.

Main dealer vs independent garage

Main dealer service

Often more expensive, but may be useful for newer cars, warranty records, software updates, digital service records and manufacturer-specific checks.

Independent garage service

Often cheaper and still good value if the garage uses correct parts, correct oil and follows the proper service schedule.

Specialist garage

A good specialist can be a strong option for premium, performance, hybrid, diesel or marque-specific vehicles.

Fast-fit service

Can be convenient, but check exactly what is included and whether it matches your vehicle’s service schedule.

Whichever you choose, ask for an itemised invoice and make sure the correct oil and filters are listed. Good service history can also help when selling a car later. See used car with no service history — is it worth it?

Full service vs interim service vs major service

Interim service

Usually a lighter service, often focused on oil, oil filter and key checks. Useful for high-mileage drivers between full services.

Full service

More detailed and normally includes a wider inspection and more service items. Often carried out yearly or at the mileage interval set by the manufacturer.

Major service

Usually more comprehensive and may include spark plugs, fuel filter, brake fluid or additional scheduled items depending on the car.

A cheap “full service” may not include the same work as a manufacturer-scheduled service. Always compare the checklist, not just the price.

Is a full service the same as an MOT?

No. An MOT checks whether the car meets minimum roadworthiness and emissions standards on the test date. A full service is maintenance work designed to reduce wear, replace service items and spot problems early.

A car can pass an MOT and still be overdue a service. It can also be serviced and still fail an MOT if there are defects with tyres, brakes, lights, suspension, emissions or safety items.

What to check before accepting a service quote

Service quotes can look similar but include very different work. Before booking, ask what is included and whether the price is final.

  • ✓Is the correct oil grade and manufacturer specification included?
  • ✓Which filters are included: oil, air, cabin, fuel or pollen?
  • ✓Is VAT included in the quoted price?
  • ✓Will you receive an itemised invoice and service stamp or digital record?
  • ✓Are brake, tyre, battery, lights and fluid checks included?
  • ✓Will the garage ask before carrying out extra repairs?
  • ✓Are genuine, OEM or aftermarket parts being used?
  • ✓Does the quote match the manufacturer service schedule?

Why service history matters

A proper service history helps prove that the car has been maintained. This matters for reliability, resale value and used car buying confidence.

  • ✓Keep itemised invoices, not just stamps.
  • ✓Check that oil grade, filters and mileage are recorded.
  • ✓Keep proof of major jobs such as cambelt, brake fluid, spark plugs or fuel filters.
  • ✓Compare service mileage with MOT history when buying a used car.
  • ✓Be cautious of cars with missing or vague service records.

When a cheap full service may be poor value

A low price is not automatically bad, but it should make you check what is actually included. A service that uses the wrong oil, skips key filters or does not inspect safety items properly may cost more later.

  • !The quote does not mention oil specification.
  • !Filters are not clearly listed.
  • !The garage will not provide an itemised invoice.
  • !The quote excludes VAT or adds unclear “environmental” charges later.
  • !The service does not follow the car’s mileage or age schedule.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a full car service cost in the UK?

Many full services cost around £180 to £350, but small cars can be cheaper and premium, diesel, hybrid or larger vehicles can cost more.

What does a full service include?

It usually includes oil and oil filter replacement, fluid checks, brake and tyre inspection, lights, wipers, battery checks and a general safety inspection. Filters and extra items vary by garage and service schedule.

Is a full service worth it?

Yes, if it is done properly. Regular servicing helps reduce wear, improves reliability and can protect resale value.

How often should I get a full service?

Many cars are serviced every 12 months or around 10,000 to 12,000 miles, but always follow the vehicle’s service schedule.

Can a car pass MOT without a service?

Yes. The MOT and service are different. Passing an MOT does not mean the car has had fresh oil, filters or maintenance work.

Should I use a main dealer or independent garage?

A main dealer may suit newer or warranty-sensitive cars. A good independent garage can be better value for many older vehicles, provided it uses the correct parts and oil.

Why is my service quote more expensive than expected?

The car may need specialist oil, extra filters, spark plugs, brake fluid, fuel filter replacement or other scheduled maintenance items.

Does a full service include brake pads?

Usually no. Brake inspection is normally included, but replacing brake pads or discs is usually charged separately.
