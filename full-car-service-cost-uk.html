Service cost guide

Full car service cost in the UK

A full service is one of the most important routine maintenance jobs for any car, but many drivers are unsure what it should cost and what is actually included. This guide explains typical UK full service pricing, the main factors that affect the bill and why the cheapest option is not always the best value.

Typical pricing

What does a full car service usually cost?

A full service price can vary a lot depending on the car, the garage and what is included. Smaller cars usually cost less than premium vehicles, and dealer servicing is often more expensive than using a reputable independent garage.

Smaller Cars

Lower-cost service range

Smaller petrol cars often sit at the lower end of the full-service price range, especially at independent garages.

Family Cars

Mid-range service cost

Family hatchbacks, estates and crossovers often cost more because parts, oil quantity and labour can vary.

Premium Vehicles

Higher-cost servicing

Premium, performance and larger diesel vehicles often cost more due to specialist oil, filters and labour rates.

What affects the bill?

Main factors that change service cost

  • 1Make, model, engine size and oil specification
  • 2Main dealer, specialist or independent garage labour rates
  • 3Whether filters and extra service items are included
  • 4Location in the UK and local labour pricing
  • 5Whether extra faults are found during inspection
What is usually included?

A full service often covers

1. Oil and filter

Fresh engine oil and an oil filter change are core parts of a proper full service.

2. Basic inspection checks

Many garages inspect tyres, brakes, fluid levels, battery condition, lights and visible leaks.

3. Air and cabin filters

These may be checked or replaced depending on the service package and the vehicle schedule.

4. General wear review

A service can highlight upcoming maintenance needs before they turn into larger repair bills.

Why price alone is not enough

Cheapest service vs best value

A low advertised service price may only cover the basics. It is worth checking what oil grade, which filters and how many inspection points are actually included before comparing quotes.

Ask what is included

One garage’s full service may be more detailed than another’s headline offer.
