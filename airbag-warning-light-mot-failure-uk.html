Airbag Warning Light MOT Failure UK

If the airbag warning light or SRS light stays on, it should not be ignored. It may indicate a fault in the supplementary restraint system, and an SRS malfunction warning can be classed as a major MOT defect in the UK.

Quick answer

Yes, an airbag or SRS warning light can fail an MOT if it is indicating a system malfunction. The official MOT inspection manual lists an SRS malfunction indicator lamp showing a system malfunction as a major defect. A passenger airbag disabled warning is treated differently and is not automatically a defect.

If the light comes on briefly at startup and then goes out, that is normally the system self-check. If it stays on, flashes, or returns while driving, the fault should be diagnosed before the MOT.

Why the airbag light matters for MOT

The airbag system is part of the vehicle’s safety equipment. It can include airbags, crash sensors, seatbelt pretensioners, control modules, wiring and seat occupancy sensors.

  • ✓An SRS light that indicates a malfunction can be a major MOT defect.
  • ✓A passenger airbag switched off warning is not the same as a system fault.
  • ✓Warning messages may help the tester decide whether a malfunction exists.
  • ✓If the light stays on, arrange diagnosis before the MOT date.

Common causes of an airbag warning light

Loose seat connector

Wiring under the front seats is a common cause, especially if seats have been moved or removed.

Weak battery voltage

Low voltage or battery replacement can trigger SRS faults on some vehicles.

Clock spring fault

The steering wheel ribbon cable can fail and affect the driver airbag circuit.

Seatbelt pretensioner fault

Pretensioners are part of the restraint system and can trigger the SRS light.

Crash sensor issue

Impact sensors, wiring or modules may store faults after knocks, water ingress or previous repairs.

Previous accident repairs

Poor-quality repairs can leave unresolved SRS faults or incorrect components fitted.

What to check before your MOT

  • 1Turn the ignition on and check whether the airbag light goes out after startup.
  • 2Do not unplug yellow SRS connectors while the battery is connected.
  • 3Check whether the issue started after moving seats, replacing a battery or repair work.
  • 4Book a proper diagnostic scan that can read SRS faults, not just engine codes.
  • 5Repair the actual cause rather than simply clearing the warning light.

Airbag systems can be dangerous to work on incorrectly. If you are unsure, use a qualified technician.

Possible repair costs in the UK

Diagnostic scan

Often around £30–£80 depending on garage and vehicle.

Loose connector repair

Can be relatively low cost if wiring access is simple.

Clock spring replacement

Often around £150–£400 depending on vehicle and parts.

Module, sensor or pretensioner

Costs vary widely and should be diagnosed properly first.

When to get it checked urgently

  • !The airbag light stays on constantly.
  • !The light appears after accident repairs.
  • !There are seatbelt warning problems as well.
  • !The horn, steering wheel buttons or airbag circuit also seem affected.
  • !Your MOT is due soon and the light has not been diagnosed.

Frequently asked questions

Can I clear the airbag light before MOT?

Only fixing the real fault is reliable. If the fault remains, the warning light can return.

Is it safe to drive with the airbag light on?

The car may drive normally, but the restraint system may not work correctly in a crash.

Can a low battery cause an airbag warning?

Yes, low voltage or battery work can trigger SRS fault codes on some vehicles.

Does passenger airbag off mean MOT failure?

Not automatically. A passenger airbag disabled warning is not the same as an SRS system malfunction.

Can an MOT tester diagnose the cause?

An MOT test checks whether the vehicle meets test standards. Diagnosis normally requires a separate scan or inspection.

Should I buy a used car with the airbag light on?

Be cautious. It could be a simple connector fault or evidence of previous accident or poor repair work.
