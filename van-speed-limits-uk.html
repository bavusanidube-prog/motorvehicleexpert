Van Speed Limits UK Explained

Many van drivers are surprised to learn that most goods vans have lower speed limits than cars on single and dual carriageways. The correct limit depends on the vehicle classification, weight, road type and whether you are towing.

Quick answer

Most goods vans up to 7.5 tonnes follow lower limits than cars on faster roads: usually 50 mph on single carriageways, 60 mph on dual carriageways and 70 mph on motorways, unless a lower signed limit applies.

Typical UK van speed limits by road type

For most goods vans not classed as car-derived vans or qualifying dual-purpose vehicles, these are the usual national speed limits when no lower signs are shown.

Built-up areas

30 mph in many built-up areas unless signs show otherwise.

Single carriageway

50 mph for many goods vans.

Dual carriageway

60 mph for many goods vans.

Motorway

70 mph for many goods vans up to 7.5 tonnes.

Local signs

A lower posted limit always overrides the national limit.

Smart motorways

Variable limits in red circles must be followed.

Car-derived vans and dual-purpose vehicles

A car-derived van is a goods vehicle based on a passenger car design and not more than 2 tonnes maximum laden weight. These vehicles can follow the same speed limits as cars. GOV.UK also says dual-purpose vehicles not more than 2.04 tonnes unladen may qualify for car limits. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Car-derived van

Usually based on a car body and limited to 2 tonnes maximum laden weight.

Dual-purpose vehicle

May qualify for car limits if it meets design and weight requirements.

Do not assume a small van is automatically car-derived. Some small vans are still goods vehicles and may be subject to lower van limits.

Why van type matters

Not every vehicle called a van is treated the same way. Classification matters more than the nickname people use for the vehicle.

  • ✓Car-derived vans may follow car speed limits if they meet the rules.
  • ✓Most panel vans follow goods vehicle speed limits.
  • ✓Converted vehicles may need checking carefully.
  • ✓Weight and design can affect classification.
  • ✓Fleet vehicles may also have company policies or speed limiters.

If unsure, check the V5C, manufacturer information, lease paperwork or fleet manager guidance.

What if you are towing with a van?

Towing can lower the maximum speed. Vehicles towing trailers are generally limited to 50 mph on single carriageways, 60 mph on dual carriageways and 60 mph on motorways, unless a lower limit applies.

When towing, allow more distance, avoid sudden manoeuvres and remember that heavy loads affect braking and stability.

Common mistakes van drivers make

  • !Assuming all vans follow car speed limits.
  • !Thinking a small van is automatically car-derived.
  • !Driving at 60 mph on a single carriageway when the van limit is 50 mph.
  • !Driving at 70 mph on a dual carriageway when the van limit is 60 mph.
  • !Forgetting that towing lowers motorway limits.
  • !Following traffic speed instead of the legal limit for the van.
  • !Missing roadworks, average speed cameras or smart motorway signs.

Do speed cameras enforce van speed limits?

Yes. Speed cameras can enforce the correct legal limit for the road and vehicle type. That includes lower limits that apply to many goods vans.

Average speed camera zones and smart motorways are especially important because the limit can change, and enforcement may cover a longer distance rather than a single camera point.

Best practical tips for van drivers

  • ✓Check your van classification before assuming car limits apply.
  • ✓Know whether your vehicle is a car-derived van, dual-purpose vehicle or goods van.
  • ✓Leave more braking distance when carrying tools, parcels, stock or equipment.
  • ✓Watch for lower roadwork, smart motorway and local speed limits.
  • ✓Do not rely on other traffic speed as a guide.
  • ✓Use a speed limiter where safe and appropriate.

Frequently asked questions

Do all vans have lower speed limits?

No. Car-derived vans and qualifying dual-purpose vehicles may follow car limits. Most other goods vans have lower limits on single and dual carriageways.

What is the van speed limit on a single carriageway?

For many goods vans, it is 50 mph unless a lower signed limit applies.

What is the van speed limit on a dual carriageway?

For many goods vans, it is 60 mph unless a lower signed limit applies.

What is the van speed limit on a motorway?

For many goods vans up to 7.5 tonnes, it is 70 mph, unless towing or a lower limit applies.

Can I use car speed limits in a work van?

Not automatically. Many work vans are goods vehicles and follow lower van limits.

How do I know my van category?

Check the V5C logbook, manufacturer information, lease paperwork or fleet manager guidance.

Can speed cameras tell I am driving a van?

Speed enforcement can take vehicle type into account, and you are responsible for following the correct limit for your vehicle.
