Van Speed Limits UK Explained

Many van drivers are surprised to learn that some vans have lower speed limits than cars. The correct limit depends on the van type, weight, classification and road type.

Quick answer

Many car-derived vans may follow car limits, but many goods vans have lower limits than cars on single and dual carriageways.

Always check the exact classification of your vehicle rather than assuming it follows car rules.

Typical van speed limits

Single carriageway

Often lower than car limits for many vans.

Dual carriageway

Often lower than car limits for many vans.

Motorway

May match car limits for many vans, depending on classification.

Why van limits can differ

  • ✓Vehicle design and load carrying purpose
  • ✓Higher stopping distances when loaded
  • ✓Different handling compared with cars
  • ✓Legal classification rules

Common mistakes drivers make

  • !Assuming all vans follow car limits
  • !Following traffic speed instead of legal limit
  • !Ignoring payload and stopping distance
  • !Not checking company fleet rules

Frequently asked questions

Do all vans have lower speed limits?

No. It depends on the exact vehicle type and classification.

Can I use car speed limits in a work van?

Not automatically. Many work vans have different limits.

How do I know my van category?

Check the V5C logbook, manufacturer details or fleet manager guidance.
