Car Battery Keeps Going Flat

If your car battery keeps going flat, the cause could be an old battery, short journeys, a faulty alternator, parasitic drain, cold weather, poor connections or something staying on while the car is parked.

Quick answer

If your car battery keeps going flat, start with the basics: battery age, driving pattern, alternator charging output, battery terminals and whether anything is draining power while parked.

A jump start may get the car moving, but it does not fix the reason the battery went flat. If it happens more than once, the cause needs checking.

Common reasons a car battery keeps going flat

Old battery

Batteries weaken with age and may no longer hold charge properly.

Short journeys

Frequent short trips may not give the alternator enough time to recharge the battery.

Alternator fault

If the alternator is not charging correctly, the battery will keep losing power.

Parasitic drain

Something may keep using electricity while the car is switched off.

Cold weather

Cold conditions reduce battery performance and make starting harder.

Poor connections

Loose terminals, corrosion or bad earth cables can cause charging and starting problems.

Dashcam or tracker

Accessories can drain the battery if wired incorrectly or left active too long.

Interior light left on

Boot lights, glovebox lights or cabin lights can flatten a battery overnight.

Alarm or module fault

Electrical modules that fail to sleep can drain power while parked.

Why does my car battery go flat overnight?

If the battery goes flat overnight, a parasitic drain is likely. This means something continues drawing electricity when the vehicle should be asleep.

  • 1Interior, boot or glovebox light staying on.
  • 2Dashcam parking mode draining too much power.
  • 3Faulty alarm, tracker or aftermarket accessory.
  • 4Control module failing to shut down.
  • 5Battery already too weak to survive normal parked drain.

Overnight battery drain should be tested properly rather than guessed. A garage can measure current draw when the car is locked and asleep.

New battery still going flat

If a new battery keeps going flat, the battery may not be the real cause. Common reasons include a charging fault, parasitic drain, incorrect battery type, poor coding on some modern cars or a wiring issue.

Alternator not charging

A new battery will still go flat if the charging system is weak.

Drain while parked

A new battery may last longer, but it can still be flattened by electrical drain.

Wrong battery type

Stop-start cars often need AGM or EFB batteries.

Poor battery registration

Some vehicles need the new battery registered to the car.

What to check first

  • 1Check battery age and condition.
  • 2Inspect terminals for looseness or corrosion.
  • 3Check whether the battery warning light appears while driving.
  • 4Think about journey pattern: lots of short trips can cause issues.
  • 5Look for lights or accessories staying on after locking the car.
  • 6Test alternator charging output if the battery keeps going flat.

Can you keep jump starting it?

Repeated jump starting is not a proper fix. It can leave you stranded again and may risk electrical issues if done incorrectly.

If the battery has gone flat more than once, test the battery and charging system before replacing parts blindly.

Battery keeps going flat after a few days parked

If the battery only goes flat after several days parked, the cause may be a weak battery, normal standby drain becoming too much, an alarm/tracker load, or short journeys not recharging the battery fully before parking.

Cars with many electronic systems can use a small amount of power when parked, but a healthy battery should normally cope for reasonable periods.

Typical UK repair costs

Battery replacement

Often around £80 to £250 depending on battery size and type.

Alternator replacement

Often £250 to £700+ depending on vehicle and access.

Drain diagnosis

Often £60 to £150+ depending on testing time.

Costs vary by vehicle, battery type and labour time. Diagnosis matters because replacing a battery will not fix an alternator fault or electrical drain.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car battery keep dying?

Common causes include an old battery, short journeys, alternator faults or parasitic drain.

Can an alternator drain a battery?

A faulty charging system can leave the battery undercharged. Some faults can also cause drain when parked.

Can short journeys flatten a battery?

Yes. Starting uses a lot of power and short trips may not replace it fully.

Why does it go flat in cold weather?

Cold weather reduces battery performance and increases the effort needed to start the engine.

Should I replace the battery first?

Not always. Test the battery and charging system first if the fault keeps returning.

Can a dashcam flatten the battery?

Yes, especially in parking mode if no proper voltage cut-off is fitted.
