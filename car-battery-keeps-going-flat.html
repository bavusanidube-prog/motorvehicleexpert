Battery guide

Car battery keeps going flat

If your car battery repeatedly goes flat, replacing the battery is not always the real solution. The cause may be charging faults, electrical drains, an ageing battery or driving habits that do not allow proper recharging.

Browse causes Browse diagnostics
Common causes

Why a battery may keep going flat

Repeated flat batteries usually mean the battery is weak, not being charged properly, or something is draining power while parked.

Battery

Old or failing battery

Batteries lose capacity over time and become less reliable in colder weather.

Read guide →
Charging

Alternator not charging correctly

If the battery is not replenished while driving, it may go flat repeatedly.

Read guide →
Drain

Parasitic battery drain

Electrical components staying on after shutdown can slowly drain the battery overnight.

Short Trips

Frequent short journeys

Repeated short trips may not give the charging system enough time to recover starting power used.

Connections

Loose or corroded terminals

Poor battery connections can reduce charging efficiency and cause starting trouble.

Cold Weather

Low temperatures exposing weakness

Winter conditions often reveal batteries that were already near the end of life.

Helpful clues

Signs that matter most

  • 1Battery goes flat overnight
  • 2Only happens after short journeys
  • 3Battery warning light appears
  • 4Slow cranking when starting
  • 5Problem worse in cold weather
What to do

Better next steps

1. Check battery age

Older batteries are often the simplest explanation.

2. Look for charging warnings

Battery warning lights can point toward alternator faults.

3. Notice timing of failure

Overnight drain and short-trip weakness usually suggest different causes.

4. Test battery and charging system

Proper testing is better than guessing and replacing parts blindly.
