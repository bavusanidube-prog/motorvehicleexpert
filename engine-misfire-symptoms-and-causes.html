Engine Misfire Symptoms and Causes

An engine misfire can make a car feel rough, shaky, hesitant, underpowered or uneven at idle. This guide explains common misfire symptoms, likely causes, warning signs and what UK drivers should check next.

Quick answer

A misfire happens when one or more cylinders do not burn fuel properly. Common signs include rough idle, shaking, hesitation, jerking, loss of power, poor fuel economy and an engine management light.

Common causes include spark plugs, ignition coils, fuel injectors, air leaks, compression problems, sensor faults or low voltage issues.

Common engine misfire symptoms

Rough idle

The engine feels uneven or shaky when stopped.

Hesitation

The car pauses or stumbles when accelerating.

Engine warning light

A misfire can trigger a steady or flashing engine management light.

Loss of power

The car may feel weak, flat or reluctant to accelerate.

Jerking under load

Misfires often show up when climbing hills or accelerating.

Poor cold running

Some faults are worst when the engine is cold.

Fuel smell

Unburnt fuel smell can appear if combustion is poor.

Hard starting

Bad misfires can make starting harder.

Bad fuel economy

The engine may use more fuel than normal.

Common causes of an engine misfire

Spark plugs

Worn spark plugs can cause weak or inconsistent ignition.

Ignition coils

A failing coil can cause one cylinder to misfire.

Fuel injector fault

Poor fuel delivery can make the engine run unevenly.

Air leak

Vacuum leaks can upset the air-fuel mixture.

Sensor fault

Airflow, oxygen, crank or cam sensors can affect running.

Low compression

Internal engine wear or valve problems can cause persistent misfires.

EGR fault

A sticking EGR valve can cause rough running.

Low voltage

Charging or battery problems can worsen engine running.

Neglected servicing

Old plugs, filters and poor maintenance can contribute to faults.

When an engine misfire is urgent

  • !The engine management light is flashing.
  • !The car shakes heavily or feels unsafe.
  • !There is severe power loss.
  • !You smell fuel or see smoke.
  • !The engine is overheating.
  • !The misfire gets worse quickly.

A flashing engine management light can mean the misfire may damage the catalytic converter, so avoid hard driving and arrange diagnosis quickly.

What to check next

1. Notice the pattern

Cold only, hot only, idle only or acceleration-only symptoms help narrow the cause.

2. Check warning lights

Fault codes can identify which cylinder or system is involved.

3. Avoid random parts

Misfires have several possible causes, so guessing can waste money.

4. Check service history

Old plugs, overdue servicing or neglected maintenance can contribute.

5. Confirm spark and fuel

A garage may check ignition, fuel delivery, compression and sensor data.

6. Do not ignore flashing lights

A flashing warning light should be treated as urgent.

Frequently asked questions

Can I drive with an engine misfire?

Not recommended if the car shakes badly, loses power or the engine light flashes.

Can a misfire damage the engine?

Yes, if ignored. It can also damage the catalytic converter.

Can bad spark plugs cause misfire?

Yes. Worn spark plugs are a common cause of misfires.

Can a misfire come and go?

Yes. Heat, moisture, load and failing parts can make symptoms intermittent.

Will a misfire show a fault code?

Often yes, but not always. Live data and proper testing may still be needed.

Is a flashing engine light serious?

Yes. It can indicate an active misfire and possible catalyst damage risk.
