Car Key Fob Not Working

If your car key fob will not lock, unlock or start the vehicle, the cause may be a flat key battery, lost synchronisation, worn buttons, water damage, signal interference, immobiliser recognition fault or a vehicle-side central locking problem.

Quick answer

The most common reason a car key fob stops working is a flat or weak battery inside the fob. If the range has reduced gradually, replacing the key battery is usually the best first step.

If both keys fail, the issue is more likely to be with the vehicle, central locking system, receiver, battery voltage or immobiliser system rather than one individual key.

Common reasons a car key fob stops working

Flat fob battery

Usually causes poor range first, then total remote failure.

Lost sync

Some keys need re-syncing after battery replacement or electrical faults.

Worn buttons

Buttons may stop clicking or only work when pressed hard.

Water damage

Moisture can damage the circuit board inside the key.

Signal interference

Remote locking may fail in certain locations due to interference.

Central locking fault

If the key sends a signal but locks do not move, the car may have a locking issue.

Weak car battery

Low vehicle voltage can affect body electronics and locking systems.

Receiver fault

If no remote keys work, the car’s receiver or control module may be involved.

Immobiliser issue

The car may unlock but fail to recognise the key for starting.

What still works?

Remote does nothing

Start with the fob battery, spare key and possible key damage.

Only range is poor

A weak fob battery is very likely.

Manual key works

The mechanical lock may be fine while the remote side has failed.

Both keys fail

Suspect vehicle battery, receiver, fuse, control module or central locking fault.

Unlocks but will not start

The immobiliser or key recognition system may be the issue.

One door does not lock

That points more towards a door actuator or central locking fault.

What to check first

  • 1Try the spare key if you have one.
  • 2Replace the key fob battery with the correct type.
  • 3Check whether the buttons feel broken, sticky or loose.
  • 4Try locking and unlocking in a different location in case of signal interference.
  • 5Check whether the manual key blade unlocks the door.
  • 6Check for dashboard key, immobiliser or battery warning messages.
  • 7If both keys fail, check the vehicle battery and central locking system.

Key fob unlocks but car will not start

If the fob unlocks the car but the engine will not start, the remote locking part may be working while the immobiliser or key recognition side has a fault.

Some vehicles need the key placed in a specific emergency start position when the fob battery is weak. Check the handbook, then investigate immobiliser warnings if the problem continues.

Key fob battery replacement tips

Use the correct battery

Check the old battery code before fitting a replacement.

Fit it the right way round

Incorrect polarity can stop the fob working.

Avoid damaging the case

Use care when opening the fob so clips and seals are not broken.

Re-sync if needed

Some cars need a simple key sync procedure after battery replacement.

Repair or replace the key fob?

If the fob works after a battery change, no further repair is usually needed. If buttons are damaged, the case is cracked, the circuit board is water damaged or the key has lost programming, a repair or replacement key may be needed.

Dealer replacement keys can be expensive, but automotive locksmiths may be able to repair, reprogram or replace keys depending on the vehicle.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my car key fob not working?

The most common cause is a flat fob battery, but lost sync, button damage, water damage or vehicle receiver faults can also cause problems.

Why does my key fob only work close to the car?

Reduced range usually means the fob battery is weak.

Why do both key fobs not work?

If both keys fail, suspect the vehicle battery, central locking system, receiver, fuse or control module.

Can I still unlock the car manually?

Usually yes, if the car has a hidden key blade or manual lock barrel.

Can a key fob stop the car starting?

Yes. The remote may unlock the car, but immobiliser recognition can still fail.

Should I replace the whole key?

Not always. Try the correct battery first, then consider repair, re-syncing or replacement if needed.
