Can a Windscreen Chip Fail MOT?

Yes, a windscreen chip or crack can fail an MOT in the UK if it significantly affects the driver’s view of the road. Damage in the driver’s main viewing area is judged more strictly than damage elsewhere on the screen.

Quick answer

A windscreen chip can fail an MOT if it is larger than 10mm in Zone A, or larger than 40mm elsewhere in the wiper-swept area, where it significantly affects the driver’s view of the road.

A small chip outside the driver’s main viewing area may still pass, but it can worsen later. Repairing chips early is often cheaper than replacing a cracked windscreen.

When a windscreen chip can fail an MOT

The MOT visibility section checks whether the driver has a clear view of the road. Windscreen damage is judged mainly by size, location and whether it obstructs or significantly affects visibility.

  • !Damage over 10mm in Zone A: may fail.
  • !Damage over 40mm elsewhere in the swept area: may fail.
  • !Spreading cracks: may fail if visibility is affected.
  • !Poor repairs: may fail if distortion affects vision.
  • !Other visibility faults: wipers, washers and obstructions also matter.

What is Zone A?

Zone A is the main area directly in front of the driver. It sits within the swept area of the wipers and is centred on the steering wheel. Damage here is judged more strictly because it can directly interfere with the driver’s forward view.

Zone A

Main driver viewing area. Damage over 10mm can be a problem.

Swept area

The part cleared by the wipers. Damage over 40mm can be a problem.

Outside swept area

Still worth repairing, but often judged differently.

What to check before your MOT

  • 1Look for chips, cracks or star breaks in the driver’s line of sight.
  • 2Check whether the damage sits inside the wiper-swept area.
  • 3See whether any crack has spread since it first appeared.
  • 4Make sure the wipers clear properly without smearing.
  • 5Fill the washer bottle and check spray pattern.
  • 6Remove stickers, sat-nav mounts or objects blocking vision.

Should you repair a windscreen chip before MOT?

Usually yes. Small chips can often be repaired quickly and cheaply if dealt with early. Once cracks spread, replacement is more likely.

Repair may be enough

Small chips that have not spread are often suitable for resin repair.

Replacement may be needed

Long cracks, multiple chips or damage in critical areas may need a new screen.

Do not delay

Temperature changes and potholes can quickly turn a chip into a crack.

Insurance may help

Many UK policies cover chip repair or reduced-cost windscreen replacement.

Other visibility issues that can fail an MOT

  • ✓Wipers not clearing the windscreen properly.
  • ✓Washer jets not working or empty washer bottle.
  • ✓Objects blocking the driver’s view.
  • ✓Severe tinting or discolouration affecting visibility.
  • ✓Loose mirrors or damaged mirror glass.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small windscreen chip fail MOT?

Yes, if it is in the driver’s main viewing area and large enough to affect visibility.

What size chip fails MOT?

Damage over 10mm in Zone A or over 40mm elsewhere in the swept area can be an MOT issue.

Can a repaired chip pass MOT?

Yes, if the repair does not distort the driver’s view.

Can a cracked windscreen fail MOT?

Yes. Cracks that obstruct vision or exceed accepted limits can fail.

Should I repair a chip before MOT?

Usually yes, especially if it is in front of the driver or spreading.

Can cold weather make cracks worse?

Yes. Temperature changes can cause chips to spread into larger cracks.
