Can a Windscreen Chip Fail MOT?

Yes, a windscreen chip or crack can fail an MOT in the UK if it significantly affects the driver’s view of the road. Damage in Zone A, the main area directly in front of the driver, is judged more strictly than damage elsewhere on the screen.

Quick answer

A windscreen chip can fail an MOT if it is more than 10mm in Zone A, or more than 40mm elsewhere in the wiper-swept area, where it significantly affects the driver’s view of the road.

A small chip outside the driver’s main viewing area may still pass, but it can worsen later. Repairing chips early is often cheaper than replacing a cracked windscreen.

When a windscreen chip can fail an MOT

The MOT visibility section checks whether the driver has a clear forward view. Windscreen damage is judged mainly by size, location and whether it obstructs vision.

  • !Damage over 10mm in Zone A: may fail because this is the driver’s main viewing area.
  • !Damage over 40mm elsewhere in the swept area: may fail if it affects the view through the wiper-cleared area.
  • !Spreading cracks: may fail if they obstruct vision or weaken the screen.
  • !Poor repairs: may fail if distortion, marks or damage still interfere with vision.
  • !Other visibility faults: wipers, washers, tinting, stickers and obstructions can also matter.

What is Zone A?

Zone A is the main area directly in front of the driver. It sits within the wiper-swept area, is centred on the steering wheel and is treated more strictly because damage here can directly interfere with the driver’s forward view.

Zone A

Main driver viewing area. Damage over 10mm can be an issue.

Wiper-swept area

The part cleared by the wipers. Damage over 40mm can be an issue.

Outside swept area

Still worth repairing, but judged differently from the main viewing area.

Windscreen chips vs cracks

Small chip

A small chip may pass if it is outside Zone A, not spreading and does not significantly affect the driver’s view.

Star break

A star-shaped chip can spread into cracks, especially with temperature changes, potholes and vibration.

Long crack

A crack is more concerning than a small chip because it can spread and may obstruct vision across the swept area.

Edge crack

Damage that reaches or starts near the edge of the screen can be harder to repair and may require replacement.

What to check before your MOT

  • 1Look for chips, cracks or star breaks in the driver’s line of sight.
  • 2Check whether the damage sits inside the wiper-swept area.
  • 3See whether any crack has spread since it first appeared.
  • 4Make sure the wipers clear properly without smearing.
  • 5Fill the washer bottle and check the spray pattern.
  • 6Remove stickers, sat-nav mounts or objects blocking vision.

Should you repair a windscreen chip before MOT?

Usually yes. Small chips can often be repaired quickly if dealt with early. Once cracks spread, replacement is more likely.

Repair may be enough

Small chips that have not spread are often suitable for resin repair.

Replacement may be needed

Long cracks, multiple chips or damage in critical areas may need a new screen.

Do not delay

Temperature changes and potholes can turn a chip into a crack.

Insurance may help

Many UK policies include windscreen repair or reduced-cost replacement cover.

Other visibility issues that can fail an MOT

  • ✓Wipers not clearing the windscreen properly.
  • ✓Washer jets not working or not spraying enough fluid.
  • ✓Objects blocking the driver’s view, such as mounts, stickers or decorations.
  • ✓Severe tinting or discolouration affecting visibility.
  • ✓Damaged or missing mandatory mirrors.

Standard windscreen

ADAS / camera screen

Higher cost if the windscreen has cameras or driver-assistance systems needing calibration.

Best pre-MOT advice

If the chip is in front of the driver, repair it before the MOT rather than hoping it passes. Even if it is small now, a spreading crack can quickly become a failure or require a full windscreen replacement.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small windscreen chip fail MOT?

Yes, if it is in the driver’s main viewing area and large enough to significantly affect visibility.

What size chip fails MOT?

Damage over 10mm in Zone A or over 40mm elsewhere in the swept area can be an MOT issue if it significantly affects the driver’s view.

Can a repaired chip pass MOT?

Yes, if the repair is flush, barely noticeable and does not distort or interfere with the driver’s view.

Can a cracked windscreen fail MOT?

Yes. Cracks that obstruct vision, spread through the swept area or exceed the relevant limits can fail.

Should I repair a chip before MOT?

Usually yes, especially if it is in front of the driver, spreading or likely to get worse.

Can cold weather make cracks worse?

Yes. Temperature changes can cause chips to spread into larger cracks.
