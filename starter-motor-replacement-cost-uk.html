Starter Motor Replacement Cost UK

Starter motor replacement in the UK usually costs between £180 and £500+ fitted, depending on the vehicle, access, labour time and whether a new, aftermarket or reconditioned starter motor is used.

Quick answer

A typical starter motor replacement costs around £180 to £500+ fitted in the UK. Small hatchbacks are often cheaper, while premium cars, larger engines and difficult-access engine bays can cost more.

Before replacing the starter motor, check the battery health, terminals, earth strap and charging system. A weak battery or poor connection can mimic starter motor failure.

Approximate starter motor replacement costs

  • £Small hatchback: £180–£280 fitted
  • £Family car: £250–£380 fitted
  • £Premium or difficult-access car: £350–£500+ fitted
  • £Extra wiring, battery or diagnostic work may increase the total

These are rough guide prices only. Labour rates, engine layout and part choice can change the final cost.

Symptoms of a failing starter motor

  • 1Single click when turning the key or pressing the start button.
  • 2Engine does not crank even though lights come on.
  • 3Intermittent starting problems.
  • 4Grinding noise during startup.
  • 5Car eventually starts after several attempts.
  • 6Starting problem gets worse over time.

If the car clicks but does not start, read the full starter motor clicking but not starting guide.

Starter motor or battery?

A weak battery and a bad starter motor can produce similar symptoms. That is why proper diagnosis matters before replacing parts.

Battery clues

Slow cranking, dim lights, rapid clicking, cold-start trouble or repeated flat battery.

Starter clues

Single click, no crank, intermittent engagement or grinding during startup.

Charging clues

Battery light while driving, dim lights when running or flat battery after driving.

If your battery keeps going flat, see car battery keeps going flat. If the battery warning light appears, see battery warning light meaning.

What affects starter motor replacement cost?

Vehicle make and model

Some cars use more expensive starter motors than others.

Labour access

Difficult engine bay access increases fitting time.

Part type

New, reconditioned and aftermarket units vary in price.

Diagnosis time

Testing may be needed to prove the starter is the real fault.

Extra wiring repairs

Bad cables, terminals or earth straps can add cost.

Garage labour rate

Prices vary by location and garage type.

What to check before replacing the starter motor

  • ✓Test the battery under load.
  • ✓Inspect battery terminals for looseness or corrosion.
  • ✓Check the earth strap and main starter cable.
  • ✓Confirm the alternator is charging correctly.
  • ✓Check whether the engine cranks, clicks or does nothing.
  • ✓Avoid replacing the starter without confirming the fault.

If the engine turns over but does not fire, the starter motor is usually not the main issue. Read car cranks but won’t start.

Can you drive with a bad starter motor?

If the engine is already running, the car may drive normally. The risk is that once you switch it off, it may not restart.

If starting is becoming unreliable, arrange diagnosis and repair before the starter fails completely.

Frequently asked questions

Can a starter motor fail suddenly?

Yes. Some starter motors fail gradually, while others stop working with very little warning.

Does clicking always mean a bad starter?

No. A weak battery, bad terminals, poor earth strap or relay issue can also cause clicking.

How long does starter motor replacement take?

Often 1 to 3 hours, but some cars take longer depending on access.

Should I replace the battery first?

Not without testing. Battery and charging faults should be ruled out first.

Is a reconditioned starter motor okay?

It can be, depending on supplier quality, warranty and vehicle value.
