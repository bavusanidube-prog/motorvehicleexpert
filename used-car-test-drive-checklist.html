Used Car Buying Guide

Used Car Test Drive Checklist UK

A used car can look clean in photos but reveal serious faults once driven. Use this UK test drive checklist to spot clutch slip, gearbox problems, brake issues, steering vibration, warning lights, overheating and expensive hidden faults before buying.

Before the test drive

Before driving, take a few minutes to check the car properly. Some faults are easier to spot before the engine is running or before the seller has warmed the car up.

  • 1Try to view the car from cold. A warm engine can hide starting or smoke problems.
  • 2Look underneath for oil, coolant or fuel leaks.
  • 3Check tyre tread, sidewall damage and uneven wear.
  • 4Turn the ignition on and check warning lights illuminate, then go out after starting.
  • 5Check the clutch, brake pedal and steering feel before moving off.
  • 6Confirm you are insured and allowed to test drive the vehicle.

During the test drive

A good test drive should include slow roads, stop-start traffic, braking, turning, reversing and, where safe and legal, a faster road. Do not just drive around the block.

Engine

The engine should pull smoothly without misfiring, hesitation, smoke or warning lights.

Clutch

The clutch should engage smoothly without slipping, juddering or smelling burnt.

Gearbox

Gear changes should feel clean, with no crunching, jumping out of gear or heavy resistance.

Brakes

The car should stop straight without grinding, vibration, pulling or a soft brake pedal.

Steering

The steering should feel stable, with no heavy vibration, wandering or pulling to one side.

Suspension

Listen for knocking, clunking or rattling over bumps and uneven roads.

Temperature

Watch the temperature gauge. Overheating can point to serious cooling system problems.

Noise

Listen for whining, humming, knocking, scraping or wheel bearing noise as speed changes.

Warning lights

No engine, ABS, airbag, oil, battery or brake warning lights should stay on.

Manual car test drive checks

  • ✓Check the clutch bite point is not unusually high.
  • ✓Accelerate gently in a higher gear to check for clutch slip.
  • ✓Make sure reverse selects cleanly without grinding.
  • ✓Check the car pulls away smoothly without judder.
  • ✓Watch for a burning clutch smell after hill starts or traffic.

If the clutch slips, judders or smells burnt, check our guides to clutch wear signs and clutch replacement cost UK.

Automatic car test drive checks

  • ✓Gear changes should be smooth, not harsh or delayed.
  • ✓Reverse should engage quickly without a heavy thump.
  • ✓The car should pull away cleanly without hesitation.
  • ✓There should be no gearbox warning lights or limp mode symptoms.
  • ✓Avoid cars with obvious slipping, flaring revs or delayed drive engagement.

Used car test drive red flags

  • !The seller refuses a test drive.
  • !The engine is already warm when you arrive and the seller cannot explain why.
  • !Warning lights stay on after the engine starts.
  • !There is smoke, overheating, coolant smell or burning oil smell.
  • !The clutch slips, gearbox crunches or the car struggles to pull.
  • !The brakes grind, vibrate or pull the car to one side.
  • !The seller rushes you, avoids questions or discourages an inspection.

Expensive faults a test drive can reveal

A test drive is not just about whether the car feels nice. It can help you avoid buying a car with expensive repair bills waiting around the corner.

  • £Clutch slip, judder or burnt clutch smell.
  • £Automatic gearbox hesitation, harsh changes or delayed reverse.
  • £Wheel bearing humming that gets louder with speed.
  • £Brake grinding, vibration or weak braking performance.
  • £Overheating, coolant loss or hot smells after driving.
  • £Suspension knocking, steering vibration or pulling to one side.

After the test drive

Check underneath again

Fresh oil, coolant or power steering leaks may appear after the engine is warm.

Use your nose

Burning clutch, burning oil, petrol, exhaust fumes or coolant smells should not be ignored.

Review calmly

Do not let the seller pressure you into deciding before you have checked everything.

Walk away if unsure

A bad used car can cost far more than paying slightly more for a better one.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a used car test drive be?

A test drive should be long enough to check starting, braking, steering, gearbox behaviour, slow-speed driving and a faster road if possible.

Should I test drive a used car from cold?

Yes, if possible. Cold starts can reveal smoke, rattles, weak batteries, misfires and other faults that may disappear when warm.

Can I buy a used car without a test drive?

It is usually not recommended unless there is strong buyer protection, a trusted inspection and a clear return policy.

Should warning lights stay on after starting?

No. Most warning lights should illuminate briefly during ignition checks and then go out after the engine starts.

Should I pay for an inspection before buying?

For expensive cars, high-mileage cars or anything you are unsure about, a professional inspection can be worthwhile.
