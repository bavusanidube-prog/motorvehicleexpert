Can Steering Rack Fail MOT?

Yes, a steering rack can fail an MOT in the UK if it has excessive play, is insecure, leaking seriously, damaged, corroded or affects safe steering control. Steering faults are safety-critical and should be checked before the test.

Quick answer

A steering rack can fail an MOT if it is loose, damaged, leaking excessively, has excessive free play, has worn inner joints or mountings, or makes the vehicle unsafe to steer.

Minor dampness may not always fail, but heavy leaks, knocking, wandering steering or obvious play should be inspected before the MOT.

When a steering rack can fail an MOT

Excessive play

Too much movement in the rack, joints or steering mechanism can fail.

Insecure mounting

Loose rack bolts, worn bushes or damaged brackets can make steering unsafe.

Serious fluid leak

Power steering fluid leaking heavily from the rack can cause MOT failure.

Damaged rack gaiter

Split gaiters can allow dirt and water into rack joints.

Corrosion or damage

A bent, cracked or badly corroded steering rack may be unsafe.

Heavy or jerky steering

Poor steering operation can indicate rack, pump, column or assistance faults.

Common symptoms of steering rack problems

  • !Knocking from the front: especially over bumps or when turning.
  • !Wandering steering: the car feels vague or does not track straight.
  • !Heavy steering: steering assistance may be weak or failing.
  • !Fluid leak: power steering fluid visible near the rack or gaiters.
  • !Uneven tyre wear: worn steering parts can affect alignment.
  • !Steering wheel play: free movement before the wheels respond.

Power steering rack leaks

Hydraulic steering racks can leak from seals, pipes, unions or gaiter areas. A small amount of old dampness is different from a fresh active leak.

Minor dampness

May not automatically fail if steering works correctly and there is no serious fluid loss.

Active leak

Fresh fluid dripping or heavy wetness around the rack is much more likely to be a failure issue.

Low fluid level

Can cause noisy, heavy or jerky steering and should be investigated.

Electric steering

Electric racks do not use hydraulic fluid, but motors, sensors or wiring can still fail.

Pre-MOT steering rack checks

  • 1Turn the steering from lock to lock and listen for knocks, grinding or whining.
  • 2Check whether the steering feels smooth, even and predictable.
  • 3Look underneath for power steering fluid leaks if safe to inspect.
  • 4Check rubber rack gaiters for splits, tears or grease/fluid around them.
  • 5Watch for uneven tyre wear, pulling or steering wheel off-centre.
  • 6Have any steering play or knocking checked on a ramp before the MOT.

Should you repair a steering rack before MOT?

Yes, if there is clear play, a serious leak, heavy steering, knocking or visible damage. Steering rack faults affect control of the vehicle and can become dangerous if ignored.

The repair may be as simple as replacing a gaiter or rack bush, but some faults require inner track rod replacement, pipe repair, rack reconditioning or complete rack replacement.

Related parts to check

Track rod ends

Worn outer joints can create play, knocking and MOT failure risk.

Inner tie rods

Inner rack joints can wear and cause vague steering or knocking.

Ball joints

Suspension ball joint wear can feel similar to steering rack problems.

Frequently asked questions

Will a leaking steering rack fail MOT?

It can if the leak is serious, active or affects steering operation or safety.

Can a split rack gaiter fail MOT?

Yes, especially if it allows contamination or exposes steering components to damage.

Can steering play fail MOT?

Yes. Excessive play in the steering system can fail because it affects control.

Is heavy steering an MOT issue?

It can be if the steering assistance or steering operation is defective.

Can I drive with a bad steering rack?

It depends on severity, but steering faults should be inspected urgently.

Best next step?

Check for leaks, gaiter damage, knocking and steering play before the MOT.
