Can Sidelights Fail MOT?

Yes, sidelights can fail an MOT in the UK if they do not work correctly, show the wrong colour, are insecure, badly damaged, obscured, too dim or affected by wiring faults. Sidelights are also known as position lamps and help make the vehicle visible.

Quick answer

A sidelight can fail an MOT if a required position lamp does not illuminate, is insecure, has a damaged lens, shows an incorrect colour, is badly obscured, flickers, or is not bright enough to be seen properly.

Many sidelight faults are simple to fix before the test. Failed bulbs, dirty lenses, poor connections and corroded bulb holders are common causes.

When sidelights can fail an MOT

Bulb not working

One or more required sidelights or position lamps fail to illuminate.

Wrong colour

Front and rear position lamps must show the correct colour for their location.

Dim or flickering light

Weak, intermittent or flickering lamps may indicate bulb, holder or wiring faults.

Damaged lens

Cracked, missing or faded lenses can affect visibility, colour or lamp security.

Loose lamp unit

Lamp units should be secure and not hanging loose or badly mounted.

Water ingress

Moisture inside the lamp can damage bulbs, LEDs, contacts or wiring.

Front sidelights and rear position lights

Drivers often call the front position lamps “sidelights”, but the MOT also considers rear position lights. Both help other road users see the vehicle when lighting conditions are poor.

Front sidelights

Usually show white light at the front and should work evenly on both sides.

Rear position lights

Usually show red light at the rear and should not be confused with brake lights.

Side marker lamps

Some larger or longer vehicles may have additional marker lamps that should work correctly where required.

Combined lamp units

On some cars, sidelights share a lamp cluster with headlights, indicators or daytime running lights.

Common causes of sidelight faults

  • 1Failed bulb: common on older vehicles with replaceable bulbs.
  • 2Corroded bulb holder: moisture can create poor contact.
  • 3Blown fuse: may stop one circuit or several lamps working.
  • 4Damaged wiring: broken wires can cause intermittent or no operation.
  • 5LED unit failure: some modern lights may need a complete lamp unit.
  • 6Wrong bulb fitted: incorrect bulbs can cause poor brightness or incorrect colour.

Pre-MOT sidelight checks

  • 1Turn the sidelights or parking lights on.
  • 2Walk around the vehicle and check every position lamp.
  • 3Check front sidelights are similar brightness and colour.
  • 4Check rear position lights are working and showing red.
  • 5Inspect lenses for cracks, dirt, fading, moisture or missing pieces.
  • 6Tap the lamp gently to check for flickering or loose connections.
  • 7Replace weak or failed bulbs before the MOT.
  • 8Investigate dashboard bulb warnings if your car displays them.

Sidelight repairs are usually simple

Many sidelight faults are caused by a failed bulb, dirty contact, corroded holder or water inside the lamp. These are often inexpensive compared with many other MOT failures.

If a new bulb does not fix the issue, check the fuse, bulb holder, earth connection, wiring and lamp unit. For modern LED lamps, the repair may involve replacing the complete light unit.

Sidelights vs headlights

Sidelights are not the same as dipped beam headlights. Sidelights help make the vehicle visible, while headlights illuminate the road ahead. Both can affect the MOT result and should be checked separately.

Frequently asked questions

Will one sidelight out fail MOT?

Yes, if a required sidelight or position lamp is not working correctly.

Are sidelights the same as parking lights?

Many drivers use the terms together, but the exact function can vary by vehicle.

Is it usually just a bulb?

Often yes, but fuse, wiring, earth or bulb holder faults can also be responsible.

Can moisture cause failure?

Yes. Water ingress can damage bulbs, LEDs, holders or wiring.

Do LED sidelights fail too?

Yes, LED sidelights can fail, flicker or require a replacement lamp unit.

Should I check all lights before MOT?

Yes. A full exterior light check is one of the easiest ways to avoid an MOT failure.
