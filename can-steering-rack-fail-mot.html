Can Steering Rack Fail MOT?

Yes, a steering rack can fail an MOT if it has excessive play, fluid leaks, damaged gaiters, insecure mountings or any defect that affects steering control or safety.

Quick answer

If the steering rack is loose, leaking badly, worn internally or has split gaiters exposing components, it may fail the MOT.

Steering defects are treated seriously because they directly affect control of the vehicle.

Common MOT failure reasons

Excessive play

Free play or looseness in steering movement.

Fluid leaks

Hydraulic power steering leaks from rack seals.

Split gaiters

Damaged rack boots allowing dirt inside.

Loose mountings

Rack not securely attached to vehicle.

Heavy steering

Faults causing restricted or unsafe steering.

Unsafe condition

Any issue reducing steering safety.

Signs a steering rack may be worn

  • !Knocking through steering.
  • !Fluid under front of car.
  • !Steering feels vague.
  • !Heavy or inconsistent steering.
  • !Visible split gaiters.

Should you repair before MOT?

Yes. Steering faults can quickly lead to MOT failure and may be unsafe to ignore.

Sometimes only outer components need repair, but internal rack wear may require replacement or refurbishment.
