Ownership guide

Car central locking not working

If your car central locking stops locking or unlocking one or more doors, the cause may be a key fob issue, blown fuse, actuator fault or low battery voltage. This guide explains common reasons and sensible next steps.

Browse causes Browse guides
Common causes

Why central locking may stop working

Central locking systems rely on keys, control modules, actuators and stable electrical power.

Key Fob

Flat key fob battery

A weak remote battery is one of the most common causes.

Read guide →
Battery

Weak car battery

Low vehicle voltage can affect locks and body electronics.

Read guide →
Fuse

Blown fuse

Some locking systems lose function entirely after fuse failure.

Actuator

Door lock actuator fault

If only one door fails, the actuator inside that door is a common cause.

Wiring

Door wiring damage

Repeated opening and closing can damage loom wires near hinges.

Module

Control module fault

Less common, but body control systems can fail or glitch.

Helpful clues

Signs that matter most

  • 1All doors affected
  • 2Only one door affected
  • 3Works with key but not remote
  • 4Problem started after flat battery
  • 5Locks click but do not move
What to do

Better next steps

1. Try spare key

Useful for separating remote faults from vehicle faults.

2. Check battery voltage

Weak batteries can trigger many convenience-system faults.

3. Identify one-door or all-door issue

This helps narrow actuator versus system-wide faults.

4. Repair if recurring

Security and access faults are worth fixing promptly.
