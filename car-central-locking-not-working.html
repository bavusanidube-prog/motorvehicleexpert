Car Central Locking Not Working

If your car central locking stops locking or unlocking one or more doors, the cause may be a flat key fob battery, weak car battery, blown fuse, failed actuator or wiring issue. This guide explains common causes and sensible next steps.

Quick answer

If central locking has stopped working completely, start by checking the key fob battery and car battery voltage. If only one door is affected, a failed lock actuator is a common cause.

Because locking faults affect security and access, recurring issues are worth fixing promptly.

Common reasons central locking stops working

Flat key fob battery

A weak remote battery is one of the most common causes.

Weak car battery

Low voltage can upset locks and body electronics.

Blown fuse

A failed fuse can disable locking circuits completely.

Door actuator fault

If one door will not lock or unlock, the actuator may have failed.

Door wiring damage

Repeated door movement can damage wires near hinges.

Control module glitch

Less common, but body control systems can fail or lose communication.

Helpful clues that narrow it down

  • 1All doors affected: battery, fuse or system fault more likely.
  • 2Only one door affected: actuator or door wiring likely.
  • 3Works with key but not remote: key fob issue possible.
  • 4Locks click but do not move: weak actuator or mechanism sticking.
  • 5Started after flat battery: voltage-related reset or battery weakness possible.
  • 6Intermittent problem: wiring or failing actuator may be developing.

What to check first

1. Try the spare key

Useful for separating remote faults from vehicle faults.

2. Replace key fob battery

Often a quick and inexpensive first step.

3. Check car battery health

Weak batteries can cause multiple electrical faults.

4. Test each door

Identify whether one door or all doors are affected.

5. Listen for clicking noises

Sounds without movement may indicate actuator failure.

6. Book diagnosis if recurring

Security and convenience faults rarely fix themselves.

One door vs all doors fault

One door not working

Usually points to that door’s actuator, latch or wiring loom.

All doors not working

More likely linked to battery voltage, fuse, key fob or control module issues.

Can you still drive?

Usually yes, but locking faults can leave the car insecure or make access difficult.

If doors fail to latch securely or cannot be opened properly, get the fault inspected urgently.

Typical UK repair costs

Key fob battery

Usually low cost and quick to replace.

Actuator repair

Moderate cost depending on door access and vehicle type.

Module or wiring repair

Can vary widely depending on diagnosis time required.

Accurate diagnosis often saves money compared with replacing random parts.

Frequently asked questions

Why has my central locking stopped working?

Flat key fob batteries, weak car batteries, fuses and actuator faults are common causes.

Why does only one door fail?

Usually a failed actuator, latch problem or broken wiring in that door.

Can a weak battery cause locking faults?

Yes. Low voltage often causes body-electrical issues.

Can I lock the car manually?

Often yes, depending on the vehicle and which part has failed.

Will it fix itself?

Usually no. Intermittent faults often become more frequent.

Best first step?

Try the spare key, replace the fob battery and check the car battery.
