National Speed Limit Sign Meaning UK

The national speed limit sign is a white circle with a black diagonal stripe. It means the national speed limit applies, but the actual speed depends on the road type and vehicle you are driving.

Quick answer

For cars in the UK, the national speed limit usually means 60 mph on a single carriageway and 70 mph on a dual carriageway or motorway.

It does not always mean 60 mph, and it does not always mean the same speed for vans or vehicles towing trailers.

What it means for cars

Single carriageway

Usually 60 mph for cars unless signs show lower.

Dual carriageway

Usually 70 mph for cars unless signs show lower.

Motorway

Usually 70 mph unless variable or lower signs apply.

What it means for vans

Many vans have lower national speed limits than cars depending on weight and classification. Drivers should check the exact rules for their vehicle.

  • ✓Some vans have lower single carriageway limits
  • ✓Some vans have lower dual carriageway limits
  • ✓Do not assume van limits match car limits

Frequently asked questions

Does the national speed limit sign mean 70 mph?

Only on certain roads such as dual carriageways for cars. It depends on road type.

Can the limit be lower than national speed limit?

Yes. Local posted signs override the standard national limit.

Do vans use the same limit?

Not always. Many vans have different limits.
