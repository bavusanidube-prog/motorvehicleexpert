Simple rule

What warning light colour usually means

  • 1Red often means stop and check urgently, especially for brakes, oil pressure, overheating or charging faults.
  • 2Amber or yellow usually means a fault needs attention soon, even if the car still drives.
  • 3Green or blue usually shows a system is active rather than faulty.
  • 4A flashing warning light is usually more urgent than a steady one.
  • 5The way the car feels matters as much as the light itself.
Better approach

What to do when a warning light appears

1. Check the colour

Red warnings usually need faster action than amber warnings.

2. Notice symptoms

Power loss, overheating, smoke, brake changes or steering changes make the issue more urgent.

3. Avoid guessing

Replacing random parts can waste money if the real fault has not been confirmed.

4. Get diagnosis

Fault-code reading and inspection are usually the quickest route to a useful answer.

FAQ

Common warning light questions

Can I keep driving with a warning light on?

It depends on the light and symptoms. Red, flashing, brake, oil pressure, overheating or steering warnings need more urgent attention.

Does a warning light always mean an expensive repair?

No. Some faults are simple, but diagnosis is needed before guessing at parts.

Can a warning light fail an MOT?

Yes, some lights can affect MOT results, especially airbag, ABS, engine/emissions and safety-system warnings.
