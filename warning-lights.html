Simple rule

What warning light colour usually means

  • 1Red often means stop and check urgently, especially for braking, oil pressure or overheating issues.
  • 2Amber or yellow often means a fault needs attention soon, even if the car still drives.
  • 3Green or blue usually shows a system is active rather than faulty.
  • 4A flashing warning is often more urgent than a steady one.
  • 5The way the car feels still matters just as much as the light itself.
A better approach

How to respond to a warning light more sensibly

1. Notice the symptom

Pay attention to power loss, rough running, overheating, odd noises or changes in braking or steering feel.

2. Judge urgency

Some warnings can wait briefly, while others suggest you should stop or avoid driving further.

3. Avoid guessing

Replacing random parts because of a warning light often wastes money and may not fix the real issue.

4. Confirm the fault

Proper fault-code reading and inspection are usually the quickest route to a useful answer.
