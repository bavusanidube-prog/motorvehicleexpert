Can Headlights Fail MOT?

Yes, headlights can fail an MOT in the UK if they do not work correctly, are badly aimed, insecure, seriously damaged, the wrong colour, or likely to dazzle other road users. Headlight faults are one of the easiest MOT issues to spot before the test.

Quick answer

A headlight can fail an MOT if a required dipped beam or main beam does not work, the beam pattern is incorrect, the aim is too high or too low, the lamp is insecure, the lens is badly damaged, or the light output is the wrong colour.

Many failures are simple to avoid. Check both headlights, both beam settings, both colours and the condition of the lenses before the MOT.

Common reasons headlights fail an MOT

  • !Bulb not working: a failed dipped beam or main beam can fail the test.
  • !Incorrect beam aim: headlights pointing too high, too low or too far to one side can fail.
  • !Poor beam pattern: the beam must be clear enough for the tester to assess.
  • !Damaged lens: a cracked, missing, loose or badly deteriorated lens can cause failure.
  • !Insecure headlamp: a loose or badly mounted headlight unit may fail.
  • !Wrong colour: unsuitable or heavily tinted light output can be a problem.
  • !Dazzle risk: badly fitted bulbs or poor conversions can glare at other drivers.

Headlight beam aim problems

Headlight aim is one of the most common lighting-related MOT issues. A lamp can work but still fail if the beam is not aimed correctly or the pattern is not acceptable.

Too high

Can dazzle oncoming drivers and fail the beam alignment check.

Too low

May not illuminate the road properly and can fail if the beam aim is outside limits.

Uneven beams

One headlight sitting higher than the other may point to adjustment, bulb seating or mounting problems.

Can condensation or cloudy headlights fail an MOT?

Light misting inside a headlamp is not always an MOT failure by itself. However, heavy condensation, standing water, poor light output, electrical faults or a distorted beam pattern can become a problem.

Light misting

May be acceptable if the light still works correctly and the beam pattern is clear.

Heavy water inside

Can reduce light output, damage electrics and affect the beam pattern.

Cloudy plastic lenses

Can fail if they significantly reduce brightness or distort the beam.

Cracked lenses

May fail if the lamp is damaged, insecure, lets in water or affects light output.

Can LED or HID headlight conversions fail?

They can. Aftermarket bulbs or conversions may cause MOT problems if the beam pattern is wrong, the light output dazzles, the colour is unsuitable, or the lamp unit is not appropriate for the bulb type.

If your headlights became brighter but now scatter light, look uneven or annoy other drivers, have the beam checked before the MOT.

Pre-MOT headlight checks

  • 1Turn on sidelights, dipped beam and main beam.
  • 2Check both left and right headlights work.
  • 3Check both sides are similar brightness and colour.
  • 4Inspect lenses for cracks, water, cloudiness or missing parts.
  • 5Make sure the headlight units are secure and not loose.
  • 6Shine the lights against a wall to spot obviously uneven aim.
  • 7Replace failed bulbs before the MOT.
  • 8Get beam alignment adjusted if the light pattern looks wrong.

Typical UK repair costs

Bulb replacement

Often low cost, although access can be awkward on some vehicles.

Beam alignment

Usually modest if the lamp units and adjusters are not damaged.

Headlamp unit

Can be expensive on modern LED, xenon or adaptive headlight systems.

If a headlight has water inside or the adjuster is broken, replacing a bulb alone may not fix the MOT problem.

Easy MOT win

Headlights are quick to check and often cheap to fix compared with many MOT failures. A five-minute light check before your booking can save a failed test, retest and wasted time.

Frequently asked questions

Can one headlight out fail MOT?

Yes. If a required headlight does not work, the vehicle can fail.

Can misaligned headlights fail MOT?

Yes. Incorrect beam aim is a common reason for MOT failure.

Can cloudy headlights fail MOT?

Yes, if lens condition seriously reduces light output or affects the beam pattern.

Can LED bulb conversions fail MOT?

Yes, especially if they create glare, poor beam pattern or unsuitable light output.

Will headlight condensation fail MOT?

Not always. Severe moisture that affects operation, brightness or beam pattern can be a problem.

Should I check headlights before MOT?

Yes. It is one of the simplest failures to prevent before the test.
