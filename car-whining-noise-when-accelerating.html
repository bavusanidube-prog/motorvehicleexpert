Noise guide

Car whining noise when accelerating

A whining noise during acceleration can come from several systems depending on where the sound comes from and when it happens. Common causes include bearings, belts, transmission components, turbo systems and steering-related faults.

Browse common causes Browse diagnostics
Common causes

Why a car may whine when accelerating

The sound often changes with engine speed, road speed or load, which helps narrow the likely source.

Bearings

Wheel bearing or rotating component

Some bearings create a rising whine that changes as speed increases.

Read guide →
Belts

Belt or pulley noise

Drive belts and pulleys can whine or squeal under load.

Read guide →
Transmission

Gearbox or driveline wear

Some whining noises become more noticeable under acceleration through the drivetrain.

Turbo

Turbocharger related noise

Turbocharged cars may develop whistles or whining sounds if faults are present.

Steering

Power steering pump noise

Some steering systems whine more during load or turning while moving.

Read guide →
Tyres

Tyre tread noise mistaken for whine

Road noise from tyres can sometimes sound like mechanical whining.

Take notice

Signs that help narrow it down

  • 1Noise rises with engine revs
  • 2Noise rises with road speed
  • 3Noise only under acceleration
  • 4Noise changes while turning
  • 5Warning lights or vibration appear too
What drivers should do

Better next steps

1. Notice when the sound happens

Engine speed, gear, road speed and steering angle all matter.

2. Do not guess at parts

Many different systems can create similar whining sounds.

3. Check for warning lights

Related warnings may point toward steering, charging or engine faults.

4. Get it inspected if worsening

Persistent noises are easier and cheaper to fix earlier.
