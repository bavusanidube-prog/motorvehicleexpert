Will a Traction Control Light Fail an MOT?

A traction control, ESC or stability control warning light can affect the MOT if it stays on and indicates a system fault. Because these systems often share sensors with ABS, it is best to diagnose the warning before test day.

Quick answer

Yes, a traction control warning light can fail an MOT if the vehicle has traction control, ESC or stability control fitted and the warning light shows a fault. If the light stays on after startup, get it checked before the test.

The warning may be caused by something simple such as a wheel speed sensor, but it can also point to ABS, steering angle or stability control faults.

How the traction control light can affect an MOT

During an MOT, warning lamps for safety systems can be checked. If the traction control or stability control warning remains on, shows a fault, or does not behave as expected, it may be treated as a failure where the system is fitted.

Light comes on then goes out

Usually normal self-check behaviour when starting the car.

Light stays on

Usually means a fault is stored or the system is disabled.

Light flashes while driving

May mean the system is actively controlling wheel slip.

Why the warning light matters

Safety system

Traction control helps reduce wheelspin and maintain grip.

Linked to ABS

Many traction systems share wheel sensors and control modules with ABS.

Reduced assistance

Stability or traction support may be limited until repaired.

Stored fault codes

A diagnostic scan usually identifies the fault area.

Bad weather risk

Loss of assistance matters more on wet, icy or loose surfaces.

Pre-test fix recommended

Fixing it before the MOT avoids an avoidable failure.

Common causes of a traction control light

  • 1Wheel speed sensor fault: one of the most common causes.
  • 2ABS reluctor ring damage: cracked, dirty or corroded rings can confuse the system.
  • 3ABS system issue: traction control often relies on ABS components.
  • 4Steering angle sensor fault: the system may not know where the steering is pointing.
  • 5Low battery voltage: weak voltage can trigger multiple warning lights.
  • 6Tyre size or pressure mismatch: different rolling speeds can confuse traction control.
  • 7ABS module or wiring fault: less common, but can be more expensive.

Can you drive with the traction control light on?

If the car drives normally and there are no brake, steering or ABS symptoms, careful short-distance driving may be possible. However, traction and stability assistance may be reduced.

Be especially cautious in rain, snow, ice, mud or when accelerating out of junctions. If ABS or brake warning lights are also on, treat it as more urgent.

  • ✓Drive smoothly and avoid harsh acceleration.
  • ✓Leave more braking distance in poor weather.
  • ✓Do not ignore additional ABS or brake warning lights.
  • ✓Book diagnosis if the light stays on or returns after reset.

How to fix it before an MOT

Start with a diagnostic scan rather than guessing. The stored fault code can point to a specific wheel sensor, steering angle sensor, ABS fault, voltage issue or communication problem.

Sensor or wiring fault

A wheel speed sensor, wiring connector or reluctor ring may need cleaning, testing or replacement.

Voltage issue

Battery or alternator problems can cause traction, ABS and ESC warnings.

Tyre issue

Incorrect tyre sizes or pressures should be corrected before deeper diagnosis.

Calibration needed

Some steering angle or stability systems need calibration after repair or alignment work.

Do not rely on clearing the light only. If the fault remains, the warning will usually come back and may still fail the MOT.

Frequently asked questions

Will a traction control light fail an MOT?

It can. If the light stays on and indicates a fault with a fitted traction control, ESC or stability system, it may fail.

Is traction control the same as ABS?

No, but they are closely linked. Traction control often uses ABS sensors and braking control to reduce wheelspin.

Can low battery voltage cause a traction control light?

Yes. Low battery voltage or charging faults can trigger traction control, ABS and ESC warnings.

Can different tyre sizes cause the light?

Yes. Mismatched tyre sizes or pressures can confuse wheel speed readings and trigger traction control warnings.

Can I reset the light before MOT?

Resetting without fixing the cause is not a proper repair. If the system still has a fault, the light may return.

What should I check first?

Check tyre pressures, look for other warning lights and read diagnostic fault codes to identify the affected system.
