Smart Motorway Speed Limits Guide UK

Smart motorways use overhead signs, variable speed limits, lane signals, Red X closures and cameras to manage traffic, congestion, breakdowns, incidents and roadworks. If a speed limit is shown inside a red circle, it is mandatory and you must follow it.

Quick answer

On a smart motorway, a speed limit shown in a red circle is mandatory. If the signs show 50, 60 or another limit, you must follow that limit until the signs change or the restriction ends.

A Red X means the lane is closed. You must not drive in that lane. There may be a broken-down vehicle, collision, debris, road workers or emergency services ahead.

What is a smart motorway?

A smart motorway uses technology to monitor traffic and manage how the motorway is used. Signs and signals can lower speed limits, close lanes, warn of hazards and help traffic flow more smoothly.

Some smart motorways use the hard shoulder as a running lane at certain times or permanently. This makes paying attention to overhead signs even more important.

What common smart motorway signs mean

Speed in a red circle

Mandatory variable speed limit. Do not exceed the displayed speed.

Red X

Lane closed. Move out safely and do not use that lane.

Amber warning signs

Hazard, queue, incident, weather warning or road condition ahead.

Lane arrows

Move safely in the direction shown. A lane may be closing ahead.

END

The displayed restriction has ended, but only increase speed when safe.

National speed limit sign

Normal national motorway limits apply unless another restriction is shown.

Why smart motorway speed limits change

Smart motorway limits are changed to manage risk and traffic flow. A lower limit may feel frustrating, but it is often used to reduce sudden braking, protect people ahead or control congestion.

  • ✓Heavy traffic or queues ahead.
  • ✓Breakdowns, collisions or debris in the road.
  • ✓Roadworks or lane closures.
  • ✓Poor weather, standing water, fog or reduced visibility.
  • ✓Emergency services, recovery operators or road workers ahead.
  • ✓To smooth traffic flow and reduce stop-start braking.

Red X lane closure rules

A Red X means the lane is closed. You must stay out of that lane because there may be a serious hazard ahead or people working in or near the carriageway.

  • !Do not drive in a lane showing a Red X.
  • !Move safely into an open lane as soon as you can.
  • !Do not wait until the last second to move over.
  • !Do not move back into the lane until signs show it has reopened.
  • !Leave space for other drivers to merge safely.

Ignoring a Red X is illegal and can lead to penalties. More importantly, it can put broken-down drivers, recovery workers, emergency services and road workers in danger.

Smart motorway cameras

Smart motorway cameras may enforce variable speed limits and lane closure rules. Treat cameras as active and follow the displayed limit. Do not assume a camera is off because there is no visible flash.

When no variable limit is displayed, the normal motorway speed limit may apply for cars, but cameras can still enforce speeding offences. Always drive to the signs, road conditions and traffic ahead.

What to do if you break down on a smart motorway

If possible, leave the motorway at the next exit or service area. If that is not possible, try to reach an emergency area, hard shoulder where available, or another place of relative safety.

  • ✓Put your hazard lights on.
  • ✓Move left if it is safe to do so.
  • ✓Use an emergency area if you can reach one safely.
  • ✓Exit the vehicle on the left if it is safe and wait behind a barrier.
  • ✓If you cannot leave the vehicle safely, keep your seatbelt on and call for help.
  • ✓Call 999 if you are stopped in a live lane or in immediate danger.

Important smart motorway driving rules

  • ✓Obey displayed speed limits in red circles.
  • ✓Never drive in a Red X lane.
  • ✓Keep a safe following distance, especially in queues.
  • ✓Watch gantries ahead, not just the vehicle in front.
  • ✓Do not accelerate until restrictions end and it is safe.
  • ✓Adjust speed for rain, fog, spray, darkness and heavy traffic.

Frequently asked questions

Are smart motorway limits enforceable?

Yes. Mandatory variable speed limits shown in a red circle can be enforced.

Can smart motorway limits change quickly?

Yes. They may change because of traffic flow, incidents, lane closures, roadworks, weather or safety concerns.

What if one lane shows a Red X?

That lane is closed and must not be used until signs show it has reopened.

Does no sign mean 70 mph?

For cars, the normal motorway speed limit is usually 70 mph when no lower restriction is displayed, but always check roadworks, gantries, lane signs and conditions.

Can smart motorway cameras catch speeding?

Yes. Cameras can be used to enforce variable speed limits and other speed limits on smart motorways.

Can you use the hard shoulder on a smart motorway?

Only when signs show it is open as a running lane, or in an emergency where it is available and safe. Never assume the hard shoulder is open.
