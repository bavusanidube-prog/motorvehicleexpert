Smart Motorway Speed Limits Guide UK

Smart motorways use overhead signs, cameras and traffic systems to manage congestion, reduce stop-start traffic and improve flow. Speed limits can change depending on live conditions.

Quick answer

If a speed limit is displayed above your lane on a smart motorway, drivers should obey that limit. Limits may reduce temporarily for congestion, incidents, roadworks or weather.

Do not assume the national speed limit applies when lower overhead signs are active.

What common signs mean

50 / 60 signs

Temporary mandatory speed limit displayed overhead.

Red X

Lane closed. Do not drive in that lane.

Amber warnings

Hazard or queue warning ahead.

No sign shown

Usual motorway rules may apply unless otherwise signed.

Why limits change

  • ✓Heavy traffic congestion
  • ✓Breakdowns or collisions
  • ✓Roadworks
  • ✓Poor weather or visibility
  • ✓To smooth traffic flow

Important driver rules

  • !Obey displayed speed limits
  • !Never drive in a lane with a red X
  • !Keep safe distances in slower traffic
  • !Stay alert for changing signs

Frequently asked questions

Are smart motorway limits enforceable?

Yes, displayed limits can be enforced.

Can limits change quickly?

Yes, they may change with live traffic conditions.

What if one lane shows a red X?

That lane is closed and should not be used.
