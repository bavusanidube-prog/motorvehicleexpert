Service Light On After Service

If your service light is still on after a service, the most likely reason is simple: the service reminder was not reset correctly. In many cases it does not mean a mechanical fault, but you should still check that the right work was done and that no other warning lights are showing.

Quick answer

A service light is usually a maintenance reminder, not the same as an engine fault light. If the car has just been serviced and drives normally, the reminder may simply need resetting through the dashboard menu or with diagnostic equipment.

However, do not ignore other warning lights. If the engine management light, oil light, brake warning, battery warning or any red warning message is also on, treat that separately.

Common causes of a service light staying on

  • 1Service reminder not reset: the most common reason after a recent service.
  • 2Wrong reset procedure used: some vehicles need exact steps or diagnostic equipment.
  • 3Separate inspection item due: oil service, inspection, brake fluid or emissions checks may have separate reminders.
  • 4Battery disconnection or low voltage: can confuse service intervals or trigger warnings.
  • 5Service was incomplete: the car may still be due a scheduled item not included in a basic service.
  • 6Fault light mistaken for service light: engine, oil, brake or battery warnings are different and more urgent.

What to check first

  • ✓Check the service invoice to see exactly what was done.
  • ✓Check whether the message says “service due”, “inspection due”, “oil service” or something more serious.
  • ✓Look for other warning lights on the dashboard.
  • ✓Check the oil level if the service included an oil change.
  • ✓Ask the garage whether they reset the service interval.
  • ✓Confirm the next service mileage and date are showing correctly.

How is the service light reset?

The reset method depends on the car. Some vehicles use a dashboard menu, steering wheel controls or a button sequence. Others need a diagnostic tool connected to the car.

Dashboard reset

Many cars allow reset through the instrument cluster or infotainment menu.

Diagnostic reset

Some cars require professional diagnostic equipment to reset the correct interval.

Oil and inspection reset

Certain cars have separate resets for oil service and general inspection.

Digital service record

Some newer cars may also need the digital service history updated.

If the garage serviced the car, ask them to reset it and confirm the next service interval. This should usually be quick.

Service light or fault warning light?

A service reminder is normally about scheduled maintenance. A fault warning means the car has detected a problem. The two are easy to confuse, especially if the dashboard message is vague.

  • ✓Service due: usually maintenance reminder.
  • ✓Oil service due: usually oil change interval reminder.
  • !Engine management light: diagnostic fault or emissions issue.
  • !Oil pressure light: urgent lubrication warning.
  • !Brake warning light: safety-related warning that needs attention.
  • !Battery warning light: charging system issue.

For fault warnings, see engine management light guide, oil light comes on then goes off and battery warning light meaning.

When to go back to the garage

Return to the garage that carried out the service if the service reminder was not reset, the wrong interval is displayed, the oil level looks wrong, the car runs badly, or new warning lights appeared immediately after the service.

A good garage should explain what was done, what was reset and whether any additional service items are still due.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my service light still on after a service?

Most commonly, the service reminder was not reset. It may also be the wrong reset, a separate inspection reminder or a fault light being confused with a service light.

Is it safe to drive with the service light on?

If it is only a service reminder and the car drives normally, it is usually not urgent. If other warning lights are on, get the car checked.

Can I reset the service light myself?

Sometimes. Many cars allow a dashboard reset, but others need diagnostic equipment. Only reset it if the correct service work has been completed.

Should the garage reset the service light?

Yes. If they carried out the service, they should normally reset the correct service reminder and confirm the next due date or mileage.

Why does my car still say oil service due?

Some cars have separate oil service and inspection reminders. The garage may have reset one but not the other.

Is a service light the same as engine management light?

No. A service light is usually a maintenance reminder. An engine management light usually means a fault has been detected.
