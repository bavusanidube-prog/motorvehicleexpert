How Early Can You Renew an MOT?

In the UK, you can renew your MOT up to one month minus a day before your current MOT expires and still keep the same renewal date for next year. You can test earlier than that, but your next expiry date will normally move forward.

Quick answer

You can renew an MOT up to one month minus a day before it runs out and keep the same expiry date for the following year.

For example, if your MOT expires on 15 May, the earliest date you can test and keep the same renewal date is 16 April. This is the official rule used in Great Britain.

Always check your exact MOT expiry date first. If you test too early, your next MOT date may be based on the new test date instead of your old expiry date.

The MOT one month minus a day rule explained

The rule means you do not have to wait until the final day before booking your MOT. You can have the test done during the final month and still keep your normal yearly renewal pattern.

This is useful because it gives you time to deal with repairs if the car fails. It also helps you avoid last-minute garage shortages or accidentally driving with an expired MOT.

What happens if you renew your MOT too early?

You are allowed to get an MOT earlier than one month minus a day before expiry. However, if the car passes, the new MOT expiry date will normally be based on the new test date, not your old expiry date.

That means you may lose some of the remaining time on your current MOT. It is not a fine or penalty, but it can shorten your renewal cycle.

Example: if your MOT expires on 15 May but you test on 14 April, your next expiry date would normally become 13 April the following year.

What if your car fails an early MOT?

If your car fails an MOT before the old certificate has expired, the situation depends on the defects. If the car has a dangerous defect, you should not drive it until it has been repaired.

Even if your old MOT still has time left, the vehicle must remain roadworthy. Driving a car with dangerous defects can lead to fines, points and safety risks.

Frequently asked questions

How early can I renew my MOT and keep the same date?

You can renew up to one month minus a day before the current MOT expires and keep the same renewal date for the following year.

Can I get an MOT more than one month early?

Yes, but your next expiry date will usually change to one year minus a day from the date the car passed the early test.

Is there an MOT grace period after expiry?

No. Once the MOT expires, you usually cannot drive the car except to a pre-booked MOT test or eligible repair appointment, and the car must still be insured and roadworthy.

Can I drive if my MOT has expired but I have booked a test?

You can usually drive directly to a pre-booked MOT test, provided the vehicle is insured and safe to drive.

Should I service my car before the MOT?

It can help, especially if the car has warning lights, worn tyres, brake issues, poor wipers or other obvious defects.

What if my MOT fails before the old certificate expires?

You must not drive the car if it has dangerous defects. Even with time left on the old MOT, the car must still be roadworthy.
