Car Radio Not Working

If your car radio will not turn on, has no sound, asks for a code or keeps resetting, the cause may be a blown fuse, weak battery, wiring fault, radio lock, speaker issue or internal head unit failure.

Quick answer

The most common reasons a car radio stops working are a blown fuse, low battery voltage, a lost security code after battery disconnection, loose wiring, a poor earth, speaker or amplifier faults, or failure inside the radio unit itself.

Start with simple checks first: battery condition, fuses, whether the radio asks for a code, and whether the problem is no power or no sound.

Common reasons a car radio may stop working

Blown fuse

A common reason the radio suddenly loses power.

Weak battery voltage

Low voltage can cause infotainment resets, shutdowns or odd behaviour.

Security code required

Some radios lock after battery disconnection or replacement.

Loose wiring

A poor power feed or earth can cause intermittent radio operation.

Speaker or amplifier fault

The radio may power on but produce no sound.

Head unit failure

Older or damaged radios can fail internally.

Radio has no power at all

If the screen is blank and no buttons respond, the first checks are usually the radio fuse, vehicle battery voltage, ignition feed and earth connection. Some vehicles have more than one fuse linked to the radio or infotainment system.

  • 1Check whether other electrical items also behave strangely.
  • 2Inspect the relevant radio or infotainment fuse.
  • 3Think about whether the fault started after a battery change.
  • 4Check for signs of low battery or charging system trouble.

If several electrical items are affected, read Battery Warning Light Meaning and Alternator Not Charging Battery Signs.

Radio turns on but there is no sound

If the radio powers up but there is no sound, the fault may be audio-related rather than power-related. Possible causes include muted settings, Bluetooth routing, speaker wiring, amplifier faults or a failed head unit output.

Settings issue

Check mute, balance, fader and audio source settings.

Bluetooth source

The car may be connected to a phone but playing from the wrong source.

Speaker wiring

Loose or damaged wiring can affect one speaker or the whole system.

Amplifier fault

Some cars use separate amplifiers that can fail or lose power.

Radio asks for a security code

Some factory radios need a security code after the battery has been disconnected, replaced or gone completely flat. The code may be in the owner’s manual, service book, radio card or manufacturer records.

  • ✓Check vehicle paperwork for a radio code card.
  • ✓Check the handbook or service documents.
  • ✓Contact the dealer or manufacturer if proof of ownership is needed.
  • ✓Avoid repeated wrong attempts, as some systems lock temporarily.

What to check next

1. Identify the exact symptom

No power, no sound, code lock and resetting faults point in different directions.

2. Check battery health

Low voltage can cause strange infotainment faults and memory loss.

3. Check the fuse

Use the handbook or fuse box diagram to find the correct fuse.

4. Look for recent work

Battery replacement, jump starts or stereo fitting can trigger radio issues.

5. Check settings

Mute, source, balance and Bluetooth routing can mimic faults.

6. Get electrical diagnosis

Modern infotainment faults may need proper testing or coding.

Can a faulty radio drain the battery?

Yes, it can happen. If the radio or infotainment system does not shut down correctly, it may contribute to battery drain. This is more likely if the radio stays lit, keeps making noises after locking the car, or the battery keeps going flat overnight.

For repeated flat battery symptoms, read Car Battery Keeps Going Flat and How To Check Car Battery Health.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my car radio completely dead?

A blown fuse, lost power feed, poor earth or failed head unit are common causes.

Why does my radio turn on but has no sound?

Check mute, audio source, speaker wiring, amplifier power and balance settings.

Why is my radio asking for a code?

The battery may have been disconnected or gone flat, triggering the security lock.

Can a weak battery affect the radio?

Yes. Low voltage can cause resets, shutdowns or lost settings.

Can a radio drain the battery?

Yes, if it stays awake or has a fault that draws current after shutdown.

Best first check?

Check the fuse, battery condition and whether a radio code is required.
