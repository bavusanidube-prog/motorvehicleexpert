Can Power Steering Leak Fail MOT?

Yes, a power steering leak can fail an MOT in the UK if it affects steering operation, causes excessive fluid loss, contaminates components or creates a safety risk. Steering defects are treated seriously because they can affect vehicle control.

Quick answer

Minor sweating or light dampness may not always fail immediately, but an active fluid leak, low reservoir level, heavy steering or noisy steering pump can lead to failure.

If the steering feels heavy, jerky, noisy or inconsistent, inspect it before the MOT.

When a power steering leak can fail MOT

Active fluid leak

Fresh steering fluid leaking from hoses, rack or pump.

Low fluid level

Fluid loss affecting normal steering assistance.

Heavy steering

Reduced assistance or complete loss of assistance.

Noisy pump

Whining noises can indicate low fluid or pump wear.

Contamination risk

Fluid leaking onto brakes, belts or other components.

Unsafe steering operation

Any steering defect affecting control is serious.

Common causes of power steering leaks

Hoses and pipes

Rubber hoses can crack or joints can seep with age.

Rack seals

Leaks from steering rack seals are common on older cars.

Pump seals

The pump shaft or housing seals can leak fluid.

Reservoir joints

Loose caps or seals may allow seepage.

Pipe corrosion

Metal lines can rust and start leaking.

Poor previous repair

Loose clips or badly fitted parts may leak again.

What to check before the MOT

  • 1Look under the car for fresh red, amber or clear fluid spots.
  • 2Check the power steering fluid level if your car uses hydraulic steering.
  • 3Turn the wheel at low speed and listen for whining noises.
  • 4Check if steering feels heavy or jerky.
  • 5Inspect visible hoses and joints for wetness.
  • 6Repair active leaks before the test.

How to fix a power steering leak

Do not rely on repeatedly topping up the fluid. Find the leak source and repair it properly. Common fixes include replacing hoses, seals, clips or the steering rack.

After repair, the fluid level should be corrected and air bled from the system if required.

Electric power steering vs hydraulic systems

Many newer cars use electric power steering and do not use hydraulic fluid. Those vehicles cannot leak steering fluid, but electrical faults or warning lights can still cause steering-related MOT issues.

Frequently asked questions

Can a small power steering leak fail MOT?

Minor seepage may not always fail, but active leaks can.

Will low steering fluid fail MOT?

It can if steering operation is affected.

Can heavy steering fail MOT?

Yes, especially if steering assistance is impaired.

Is whining from the pump serious?

Often yes, as it may indicate low fluid or pump wear.

Can I just top it up?

Only as a short-term measure. The leak still needs repair.

Should I fix it before MOT?

Yes, steering faults are best repaired before testing.
