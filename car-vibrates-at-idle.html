Idle vibration guide

Car Vibrates At Idle

If your car vibrates while stopped in traffic or idling on the driveway, the cause may be rough engine running, worn engine mounts, misfire, low idle speed or another drivability fault. This guide explains the most common causes and what to check next.

Quick answer

A car that vibrates at idle often has either an engine running issue or a mounting issue. Common causes include engine misfire, worn engine mounts, dirty throttle body, air leaks, low idle speed or charging-related problems.

If the vibration disappears when driving, the fault is often most noticeable only at idle because engine speed is lowest.

Common causes of vibration at idle

Engine misfire

One of the most common reasons for shaking at idle.

Worn engine mounts

Mounts absorb engine movement. When worn, vibration transfers into the cabin.

Dirty throttle body

Can upset idle airflow and cause unstable engine speed.

Vacuum leak

Air leaks can cause rough idle and uneven running.

Low idle speed

If idle RPM drops too low, the engine may feel rough.

Ignition fault

Spark plugs or coils can cause misfire vibration.

Fuel injector issue

Poor fuel delivery can make idle uneven.

Charging problem

Low voltage can worsen idle quality on some cars.

Diesel system issue

Injectors or emissions faults can feel like vibration at idle.

Signs of rough idle vs normal vibration

Normal slight vibration

Some engines naturally produce a small amount of vibration when idling.

Likely fault vibration

Shaking seats, steering wheel vibration, uneven RPM or engine stumbling usually suggest a problem.

Only with air conditioning on

Extra load may reveal a weak idle control or mount issue.

Comes and goes

Intermittent faults can point to ignition, sensors or air leaks.

When idle vibration needs faster attention

  • !Engine management light appears.
  • !The engine shakes heavily.
  • !Stalling or near-stalling occurs.
  • !Strong fuel smell or smoke appears.
  • !Vibration becomes worse quickly.
  • !Power loss happens while driving too.

What to check next

1. Watch engine RPM

Fluctuating idle speed often points to running faults.

2. Note hot or cold only

Cold-only symptoms may narrow the cause.

3. Check warning lights

Stored fault codes can save time.

4. Listen for misfire

Uneven engine rhythm can indicate cylinder misfire.

5. Check service history

Old spark plugs or overdue servicing can contribute.

6. Inspect mounts

Worn mounts may show excess engine movement.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car shake at idle but drive fine?

Idle speed is lowest, so misfires and mount issues are most noticeable there.

Can bad spark plugs cause idle vibration?

Yes. Worn plugs can cause misfires and rough running.

Can engine mounts cause vibration?

Yes. Failed mounts allow vibration into the cabin.

Is vibration at idle serious?

Sometimes minor, sometimes a misfire or engine fault needing attention.

Can low battery voltage cause rough idle?

It can contribute on some modern vehicles.

Should I ignore it if it is slight?

Monitor it, but worsening vibration should be checked.
