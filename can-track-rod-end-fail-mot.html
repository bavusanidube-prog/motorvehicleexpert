Can Track Rod End Fail MOT?

Yes, a worn or loose track rod end can fail an MOT. Track rod ends are steering components, so excessive play or damage can affect control and road safety.

Quick answer

Track rod ends connect steering rack movement to the front wheels. If worn, steering can feel loose or imprecise.

MOT testers check for wear, looseness and security.

When a track rod end can fail MOT

Excessive play

Movement beyond limits.

Split boot

Rubber cover damaged.

Loose fixing

Insecure steering joint.

Knocking wear

Wear may cause noise.

Corrosion

Heavy rust damage.

Dangerous looseness

Serious steering risk.

Common symptoms

Loose steering, knocking sounds, uneven tyre wear, vague handling and poor alignment are common signs.

What to check before MOT

1. Steering feel

Notice looseness or wandering.

2. Listen for knocks

Especially over bumps.

3. Inspect tyres

Uneven wear may be linked.

4. Get inspection

A garage can confirm play safely.
