Can Track Rod End Fail MOT?

Yes, a track rod end can fail an MOT in the UK if it has excessive play, a split dust boot, looseness, damage or any defect that affects steering safety. Track rod ends are critical steering joints linking the steering rack to the wheels.

Quick answer

A track rod end can fail an MOT if the joint is worn, loose, damaged or has excessive movement. Steering components must be secure and operate safely.

If your car knocks when steering, wanders on the road, wears tyres unevenly or feels vague through the steering wheel, it should be checked before the MOT.

When a track rod end can fail an MOT

Excessive play

Movement in the joint beyond safe limits can fail.

Loose fixing

Loose lock nuts or insecure fixing points are unsafe.

Split dust boot

A damaged rubber boot can allow dirt and water into the joint.

Worn ball joint

Internal wear can reduce steering precision and safety.

Corrosion or damage

Bent, cracked or heavily corroded parts may fail.

Unsafe steering response

Any defect affecting control of the vehicle is serious.

Common symptoms of worn track rod ends

  • !Knocking when turning: often at low speed or parking.
  • !Vague steering: delayed response from steering wheel input.
  • !Uneven tyre wear: especially inner or outer shoulder wear.
  • !Steering wheel vibration: worn joints can contribute to instability.
  • !Pulling left or right: worn steering joints may affect alignment.
  • !Clunk over bumps: front-end looseness may be noticeable.

Can a split track rod end boot fail MOT?

Yes, it can. The rubber dust boot protects the joint grease and keeps dirt and moisture out. If it splits, the joint can wear quickly and may already be contaminated.

Minor split

May depend on condition and whether the joint is affected.

Severe split

More likely to be an MOT issue, especially if grease has escaped.

Loose joint

If movement is present as well as a split boot, failure risk rises.

Best action

Replace worn parts early rather than waiting for steering wear to worsen.

Pre-MOT track rod end checks

  • 1Listen for knocks while turning the steering at low speed.
  • 2Check front tyres for uneven shoulder wear.
  • 3Notice if the car wanders or needs constant correction.
  • 4Inspect visible rubber boots for tears if safe to do so.
  • 5Check steering wheel sits straight when driving normally.
  • 6Have front-end play checked professionally before the MOT.

Should you replace track rod ends before MOT?

Yes, if there is play, knocking, split boots or vague steering. Track rod ends are relatively common wear items and directly affect steering safety.

After replacement, wheel alignment is usually recommended to ensure straight steering and proper tyre wear.

Typical UK repair costs

Single track rod end

Often moderate cost depending on vehicle and labour time.

Both sides

May be sensible if both joints are similarly worn.

Alignment afterwards

Usually recommended after steering component replacement.

Track rod end vs steering rack fault

Track rod end wear can feel similar to steering rack play, lower arm wear or ball joint faults. Correct diagnosis helps avoid replacing the wrong parts.

Frequently asked questions

Will a worn track rod end fail MOT?

Yes, if there is excessive play or unsafe wear.

Can a split dust boot fail MOT?

It can, especially if the joint is contaminated or worn.

Can track rod ends cause tyre wear?

Yes, worn joints can affect wheel alignment.

Do I need wheel alignment after replacement?

Usually yes, it is strongly recommended.

Can I drive with a bad track rod end?

It depends on severity, but steering faults should be repaired quickly.

Best next step?

Have any steering knock, looseness or uneven tyre wear inspected before MOT.
