Average Speed Cameras: How They Work UK

Average speed cameras calculate your speed over a measured distance, rather than at one single point. They are commonly used in roadworks, tunnels, smart motorway sections, dual carriageways and other routes where steady traffic speeds improve safety.

Quick answer

Average speed cameras record a vehicle at two or more points, compare the time taken to travel between those points, and calculate the average speed over that section.

That means braking only when you see a camera is not enough. The safest approach is to stay within the posted speed limit for the whole monitored zone.

How average speed cameras work

1. First camera records your vehicle

The system captures your number plate and the time you pass the first camera point.

2. Another camera records it again

Your vehicle is identified again further along the route.

3. Travel time is compared

The system compares your travel time over a known distance.

4. Average speed is calculated

If your average speed is above the limit, enforcement may follow.

Where average speed cameras are commonly used

  • ✓Motorway roadworks and temporary speed limit zones.
  • ✓Smart motorways and controlled motorway sections.
  • ✓Long dual carriageways and A-roads.
  • ✓Tunnels, bridges and controlled corridors.
  • ✓Urban routes with repeated speeding or safety concerns.

Common average speed camera myths

Changing lanes avoids them

No. You should not treat lane changing as a way to avoid average speed enforcement.

They only work in daylight

No. Many systems are designed to work day and night.

They always flash

No. You may not see any visible flash even if the system records vehicles.

Slowing at the camera is enough

No. Your average across the whole measured section is what matters.

Fines, points and enforcement

Average speed cameras can enforce speed limits like other camera systems. If your average speed is recorded above the limit, you may receive a notice and could face a fixed penalty, penalty points, a speed awareness course offer or further action depending on the circumstances.

For more detail, read the Speeding Fine UK Explained guide.

How to drive through average speed zones

  • 1Check the posted speed limit as you enter the zone.
  • 2Keep a steady speed instead of braking late.
  • 3Leave a safe gap from the vehicle ahead.
  • 4Watch for temporary limits in roadworks.
  • 5Do not rely only on sat nav alerts.
  • 6Remember that vans and larger vehicles may have different limits on some roads.

Why average speed cameras are different from fixed cameras

A fixed speed camera usually measures speed at or near one point. An average speed system measures your speed across a longer section, so speeding briefly and then slowing later can still leave your average too high.

They are often used where smoother, steadier speeds are more important than catching a single moment of speeding.

Frequently asked questions

Do average speed cameras flash?

Not always. Many systems do not rely on a visible flash.

Can average speed cameras work at night?

Yes. Many systems are designed to operate day and night.

Are average speed cameras only in roadworks?

No. They can also be used on permanent routes, tunnels and controlled corridors.

Can changing lanes avoid them?

No. Changing lanes should not be treated as a way to avoid enforcement.

Do they measure every vehicle?

They can monitor vehicles travelling through the camera zone.

What is the safest approach?

Stay within the posted limit for the whole monitored section.
