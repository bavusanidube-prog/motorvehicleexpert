Average Speed Cameras: How They Work UK

Average speed cameras calculate your average speed between two or more camera points. They are commonly used on roadworks, major routes and areas where steady traffic speeds improve safety.

Quick answer

Instead of measuring one instant speed at a single point, these systems compare the time taken to travel between camera locations and calculate average speed over that distance.

This encourages drivers to maintain a steady legal speed rather than braking only at one camera.

How average speed cameras work

1. Camera records vehicle

Number plate captured at first point.

2. Second camera records again

Vehicle identified further along route.

3. Time compared

Travel time used with known distance.

4. Average speed calculated

If above limit, enforcement may follow.

Where they are commonly used

  • ✓Motorway roadworks
  • ✓Long dual carriageways
  • ✓Urban routes with safety concerns
  • ✓Tunnels or controlled corridors

Common myths and mistakes

  • !Speeding then slowing down later can still trigger average enforcement
  • !Changing lanes does not automatically avoid systems

Frequently asked questions

Do average speed cameras flash?

Not always. Many systems do not rely on visible flashes.

Can they work at night?

Yes, many systems operate day and night.

Are they only in roadworks?

No. They are also used on permanent routes in some areas.
