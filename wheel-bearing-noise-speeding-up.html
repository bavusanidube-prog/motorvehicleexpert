Wheel Bearing Noise When Speeding Up

A worn wheel bearing often makes a humming, droning, rumbling or grinding noise that gets louder as road speed increases. The noise may also change when cornering because the load shifts from one side of the car to the other.

Quick answer

Wheel bearing noise usually gets louder with speed because the bearing rotates faster under load. A worn bearing can create a steady humming, droning or rumbling sound that rises with road speed rather than engine revs.

If the noise is loud, grinding, getting worse, causing vibration or linked with ABS warnings, get it inspected quickly. A failing bearing can become a safety issue if ignored.

Signs of a failing wheel bearing

Humming noise

Often starts quietly and becomes louder as road speed increases.

Droning or rumbling

Can sound like tyre roar, especially at steady cruising speeds.

Grinding sound

A rough metallic sound can suggest more advanced bearing wear.

Changes when cornering

Noise may get louder or quieter as weight shifts left or right.

Vibration

A badly worn bearing can cause steering wheel, floor or seat vibration.

ABS warning light

Some hub assemblies include ABS sensors or magnetic encoder rings.

Why does the noise get louder when speeding up?

A wheel bearing supports the wheel and allows it to rotate smoothly. When the bearing wears, the internal surfaces can become rough or loose. As speed increases, the bearing rotates faster and the damaged surfaces create more noise.

A key clue is that bearing noise usually follows road speed, not engine speed. If you lift off the accelerator and the noise continues at the same pitch while the car rolls, the fault may be wheel, tyre or bearing related rather than engine related.

Noise changes when turning left or right

Wheel bearing noise often changes when cornering because weight transfers across the car. For example, turning left loads the right side of the vehicle more, while turning right loads the left side more.

Louder turning left

Can sometimes point towards a bearing on the right side, because that side is loaded more.

Louder turning right

Can sometimes point towards a bearing on the left side, because that side is loaded more.

This is only a clue, not a final diagnosis. Tyres, brakes, suspension and wheel bearings should be checked properly.

Other faults that can sound like a wheel bearing

  • ✓Uneven tyre wear: sawtooth or cupped tyres can create a droning noise.
  • ✓Tyre damage: bulges, flat spots or internal damage can cause noise and vibration.
  • ✓Brake problems: dragging pads, stuck calipers or worn discs can create rubbing or grinding.
  • ✓CV joint issues: usually clicking when turning, but can sometimes be confused with wheel-end noise.
  • ✓Differential or gearbox noise: can drone with speed, especially on some drivetrains.
  • ✓Wheel alignment issues: can cause tyre wear that later creates noise.

If you also feel vibration, read steering wheel shaking while driving and car shakes at high speed.

Can you keep driving with wheel bearing noise?

Mild early bearing noise may allow short careful trips to a garage, but it should not be ignored. Wheel bearings can worsen over time and may affect wheel stability, braking, ABS operation or hub components.

  • !Loud grinding or metallic noise should be checked urgently.
  • !Vibration, wobble or steering shake means inspect sooner.
  • !Noise that worsens quickly should not be ignored.
  • !ABS warning lights may suggest hub or sensor-related issues.
  • !Avoid long journeys and motorway speeds until inspected.

Wheel bearing replacement cost in the UK

Wheel bearing replacement cost depends on the vehicle, bearing design, labour time and whether the bearing is pressed into the hub or replaced as a complete hub assembly.

Can a bad wheel bearing fail an MOT?

Yes. A wheel bearing can fail an MOT if there is excessive play, roughness, noise, insecurity or a safety concern. Even if the noise is only early-stage, it is worth fixing before it worsens.

Frequently asked questions

Can tyres sound like a bad wheel bearing?

Yes. Uneven tyre wear, tyre roar or damaged tyres can sound very similar to a wheel bearing, especially at higher speeds.

Does wheel bearing noise always change when turning?

Often, but not always. Some bearing noises remain steady and only get louder with road speed.

Should both wheel bearings be replaced at the same time?

Usually only the faulty bearing is replaced unless both sides are worn or the garage finds play or noise on both.

Can a wheel bearing cause steering vibration?

Yes, especially if the bearing is badly worn, but tyres, wheel balance, brakes and suspension can also cause vibration.

Can a wheel bearing affect ABS?

Yes. Some hub and bearing assemblies include ABS sensors or magnetic rings. Wear or damage can trigger ABS-related warnings.

How quickly should I fix wheel bearing noise?

Arrange inspection soon. If the noise is loud, grinding, worsening or causing vibration, treat it as urgent.
