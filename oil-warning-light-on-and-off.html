Oil Warning Light On and Off

An oil warning light that comes on and off can indicate low oil level, unstable oil pressure, a faulty sensor or a more serious lubrication problem. Because lack of oil pressure can damage an engine quickly, this warning should be treated seriously.

Quick answer

If the oil warning light flickers or comes on intermittently, stop and investigate as soon as it is safe. It may be something simple such as low oil level, but it can also mean dangerously low oil pressure.

Continuing to drive with genuine low oil pressure can cause severe engine damage. If the light stays on, the engine sounds noisy, or the oil level is very low, switch off the engine and arrange help.

Common reasons the oil warning light comes on and off

Low oil level

Low oil in the sump is one of the most common reasons.

Oil pressure drop

Pressure may fall when idling, cornering or when the engine is hot.

Faulty oil pressure switch

A bad sender or sensor can trigger false warnings.

Wrong oil grade

Incorrect oil viscosity can affect pressure behaviour.

Worn engine internals

Internal wear can reduce pressure, especially when warm.

Wiring fault

Damaged wiring or connectors may cause intermittent lights.

Blocked oil pickup

Restricted oil flow can create pressure warnings.

Oil pump issue

A weak pump may not maintain safe pressure.

Oil leak

Leaks can reduce oil level enough to trigger warnings.

When you should stop driving

  • !The oil light stays on continuously.
  • !There is engine knocking, rattling or harsh noise.
  • !The engine overheats or smells hot.
  • !The warning appears together with poor running.
  • !Oil level is very low or there is visible leakage.
  • !The light comes back after topping up oil.

If any of these happen, stop safely and switch off the engine. Do not keep driving to “see if it clears”.

What to check first

1. Check oil level

Use the dipstick or approved electronic level check if fitted. Follow the handbook procedure.

2. Notice when it happens

Only at idle, only when hot, during cornering or after braking can be useful clues.

3. Look for leaks

Check under the vehicle and around the engine bay for fresh oil.

4. Listen to the engine

Knocking, rattling or harsh mechanical noise makes the warning more urgent.

5. Check service history

Wrong oil, overdue servicing or poor maintenance can contribute to oil problems.

6. Do not guess

Oil pressure testing may be needed to confirm whether the warning is real.

Why intermittent oil warnings matter

Some drivers ignore a flickering oil light because it goes away. That can be risky because low oil pressure may first appear only when the engine is hot, idling, braking or cornering.

Treat repeated warnings seriously even if the engine seems normal. A sensor fault is possible, but real oil pressure loss should be ruled out first.

Frequently asked questions

Can low oil cause a flickering light?

Yes, low oil level is a common cause of intermittent oil warnings.

Can I keep driving?

Not recommended until the cause is checked. Stop if the light stays on or the engine sounds noisy.

Could it just be the sensor?

Yes, but real pressure problems must be ruled out first.

Why only at idle?

Oil pressure is often lowest at hot idle, so weak pressure may show there first.

Will an oil change fix it?

Sometimes if oil is wrong or overdue, but not if the engine has a pressure fault.

Best next step?

Check the oil level, avoid driving if the warning continues, and get oil pressure tested properly.
