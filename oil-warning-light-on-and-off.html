Oil Warning Light On and Off

An oil warning light that comes on and off can indicate low oil level, unstable oil pressure, a faulty sensor or a more serious lubrication problem. Because lack of oil pressure can damage an engine quickly, this warning should be taken seriously.

Quick answer

If the oil warning light flickers or comes on intermittently, stop and investigate as soon as it is safe. It may be something simple such as low oil level, but it can also mean dangerously low oil pressure.

Continuing to drive with genuine low oil pressure can cause severe engine damage.

Common reasons the oil warning light comes on and off

Low oil level

Low oil in the sump is one of the most common reasons.

Oil pressure drop

Pressure may fall when idling or when hot.

Faulty oil pressure switch

A bad sender or sensor can trigger false warnings.

Wrong oil grade

Incorrect oil viscosity can affect pressure behaviour.

Worn engine internals

Internal wear can reduce pressure, especially when warm.

Wiring fault

Damaged wiring or connectors may cause intermittent lights.

When you should stop driving

  • !The oil light stays on continuously.
  • !There is engine knocking, rattling or harsh noise.
  • !The engine overheats or smells hot.
  • !The warning appears together with poor running.
  • !Oil level is very low or there is visible leakage.

If any of these happen, stop safely and switch off the engine.

What to check first

1. Check oil level

Use the dipstick or approved electronic level check if fitted.

2. Notice when it happens

Only at idle, only when hot, or during cornering can be useful clues.

3. Look for leaks

Check under the vehicle and around the engine bay.

4. Do not guess

Pressure testing may be needed to confirm whether the fault is real.

Why intermittent warnings matter

Some drivers ignore a flickering oil light because it goes away. That can be risky because low oil pressure may first appear only when the engine is hot or idling.

Treat repeated warnings seriously even if the engine seems normal.

Frequently asked questions

Can low oil cause flickering light?

Yes, low oil level is a common cause.

Can I keep driving?

Not recommended until the cause is checked.

Could it just be the sensor?

Yes, but real pressure problems must be ruled out first.

Why only at idle?

Oil pressure is often lowest at hot idle.

Will an oil change fix it?

Sometimes if oil is wrong or overdue, but not always.

Best next step?

Check level and get the pressure tested properly.
