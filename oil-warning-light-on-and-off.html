Oil warning light guide

Oil warning light on and off

If the oil warning light comes on and then goes off, it should never be ignored. It can be caused by low oil level, low oil pressure, a faulty oil pressure sensor, oil pump problems, blocked oil flow, incorrect oil or engine wear.

Quick answer: can you keep driving?

If the red oil warning light comes on while driving, stop safely and check the oil level before continuing. The warning may be intermittent, but genuine low oil pressure can damage an engine very quickly.

If the oil light comes on repeatedly, stays on, appears with engine noise, or returns after topping up, do not keep driving. The car needs proper inspection.

Why the oil light comes on then disappears

  • 1Low engine oil level: oil may move away from the pickup during cornering, braking or hills.
  • 2Low oil pressure at idle: pressure may drop when the engine is hot and running slowly.
  • 3Faulty oil pressure sensor: an intermittent sensor can trigger false warnings.
  • 4Blocked oil pickup or sludge: poor maintenance can restrict oil flow.
  • 5Wrong oil grade: incorrect oil can affect pressure and flow.
  • 6Worn oil pump: weak oil circulation may cause pressure loss.
  • 7Engine wear: worn internal parts can reduce oil pressure, especially when hot.

When the oil light appears matters

Light at idle

If the oil light appears at idle when the engine is hot, low oil pressure, oil grade issues or engine wear may be possible.

Light on corners

If it appears during cornering, braking or hills, check the oil level immediately.

Light with noise

If the light appears with knocking, tapping or rattling, stop driving and arrange urgent inspection.

Immediate checks to make

1. Stop safely

Pull over and switch the engine off if the red oil warning light appears.

2. Check oil level

Use the dipstick on level ground after allowing the engine to settle.

3. Look for leaks

Check under the car and around the engine bay for fresh oil leaks.

4. Listen for noise

Knocking, tapping or rattling may suggest poor lubrication.

5. Use correct oil

Only top up with oil that meets the correct specification for your vehicle.

6. Test oil pressure

If the level is correct but the light returns, actual oil pressure should be tested.

Stop driving immediately if:

  • !The oil warning light stays on constantly.
  • !The engine sounds noisy, knocking, rattling or tapping.
  • !The oil level is very low or below the minimum mark.
  • !The light returns after topping up oil.
  • !Performance changes suddenly or the engine feels rough.

Possible repair costs in the UK

Oil top-up

Usually low cost, depending on oil type and amount needed.

Oil pressure sensor

Often around £60 to £180 depending on access and vehicle type.

Oil pump or engine repair

Can be much higher, especially if low oil pressure has caused damage.

Costs vary widely, so diagnosis matters. Do not assume the cheapest fault until oil level and oil pressure have been checked properly.

Good maintenance advice

Regular servicing helps reduce oil-related problems. Old oil, wrong oil, blocked filters and poor maintenance can all contribute to lubrication issues.

A regular oil and filter change is one of the most important maintenance jobs on most vehicles. If the oil warning light keeps returning, the cause should be found rather than simply resetting the warning.

Frequently asked questions

Can low oil cause the light to flicker?

Yes. Low oil level can cause flickering during braking, cornering, hills or when the engine is hot.

Can I just reset the oil warning light?

No. Find the cause first. Resetting a warning without checking oil level and pressure can risk engine damage.

Can wrong oil grade trigger the oil light?

Sometimes. Incorrect oil viscosity or specification can affect oil pressure and flow.

Is an oil warning light serious?

Yes, potentially. If it is a genuine oil pressure problem, engine damage can happen quickly.

Will an oil warning light fail an MOT?

It depends on the fault and vehicle condition. Visible leaks, engine problems or related warning issues may matter. See our common MOT failure reasons guide.
