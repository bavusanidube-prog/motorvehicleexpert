Charging system cost guide

Alternator Replacement Cost UK

Alternator replacement in the UK commonly costs around £250 to £700+ fitted, depending on the vehicle, part type, engine layout and labour time. A failed alternator can stop the battery charging and eventually leave the car unable to run.

Quick answer

A typical alternator replacement can cost around £250 to £700+ fitted in the UK. Smaller mainstream cars are often cheaper, while premium cars, stop-start systems and awkward engine bays can cost more.

Before replacing the alternator, confirm the battery, drive belt, wiring and earth connections are not the real cause.

Approximate alternator replacement costs

Small hatchback

Often around £250 to £400 fitted.

Family car / SUV

Often around £350 to £550 fitted.

Premium / complex vehicle

Can be £500 to £700+ fitted.

Prices vary by region, garage labour rate, parts choice and how difficult the alternator is to access.

Warning signs of a failing alternator

  • 1Battery warning light on the dashboard.
  • 2Dim or flickering headlights.
  • 3Repeated flat battery after driving.
  • 4Slow cranking or no-start after a short stop.
  • 5Electrical glitches, warning messages or low-voltage behaviour.
  • 6Whining noise or burning smell from belt area.

What affects alternator replacement price?

Vehicle model

Some cars have alternators that are quick to access, while others require extra strip-down.

Part type

New, reconditioned, aftermarket and genuine manufacturer parts can vary widely in price.

Labour time

Awkward access increases fitted cost.

Extra repairs

Belts, tensioners, batteries, wiring or pulleys may need attention too.

Is it safe to drive with a bad alternator?

Sometimes the car may still drive for a short period, but once the battery charge drops too far, the engine can cut out or fail to restart.

  • !You may get stranded unexpectedly.
  • !Lights, steering assistance and electronics may be affected.
  • !Repeated jump-starting does not fix charging failure.
  • !Diagnosis and repair should be arranged quickly.

What to check before replacing the alternator

1. Test the battery

A weak battery can mimic alternator problems.

2. Check charging voltage

Confirm whether the alternator is actually charging correctly.

3. Inspect the belt

A loose or broken auxiliary belt can stop the alternator working.

4. Check wiring and earths

Poor connections can cause charging faults without alternator failure.

Frequently asked questions

Can a bad alternator drain a battery?

Yes. If the alternator stops charging properly, the battery will gradually run flat.

Should I replace the battery too?

Sometimes, especially if the battery has been deeply discharged or is already weak.

How long does alternator replacement take?

Often 1 to 3 hours, but some vehicles take longer.

Can the warning light mean something else?

Yes. Wiring faults, belt issues and battery faults can also trigger charging warnings.

Can I fit a used alternator?

It may be cheaper, but reliability and warranty can be weaker than new or reconditioned parts.

Is diagnosis worth paying for?

Yes. It can prevent replacing an alternator when the real fault is battery, belt or wiring related.
