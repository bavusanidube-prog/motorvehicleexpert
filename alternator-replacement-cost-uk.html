Charging System Cost Guide

Alternator Replacement Cost UK

Alternator replacement in the UK usually costs between £250 and £700+ fitted, depending on the vehicle, part type, engine layout and labour time. A failing alternator can stop the battery charging and eventually leave the car unable to run or restart.

Quick answer

A typical alternator replacement costs around £250 to £700+ fitted in the UK. Small mainstream cars are often at the lower end, while premium cars, stop-start systems and awkward engine bays can cost more.

Before replacing the alternator, confirm the battery, auxiliary belt, wiring and earth connections are not causing the charging problem.

Approximate alternator replacement costs

  • £Small hatchback: £250–£400 fitted
  • £Family car or SUV: £350–£550 fitted
  • £Premium or complex vehicle: £500–£700+ fitted
  • £Extra battery, belt or tensioner work may increase the total

Prices vary by region, garage labour rate, parts choice and how difficult the alternator is to access.

Warning signs of a failing alternator

  • 1Battery warning light on the dashboard.
  • 2Dim or flickering headlights.
  • 3Repeated flat battery after driving.
  • 4Slow cranking or no-start after a short stop.
  • 5Electrical glitches, warning messages or low-voltage behaviour.
  • 6Whining noise or burning smell from the belt area.

For symptoms in more detail, read alternator not charging battery signs and battery warning light meaning.

What affects alternator replacement price?

Vehicle model

Some alternators are quick to access, while others need extra parts removed first.

Part type

New, reconditioned, aftermarket and genuine parts can vary widely in price.

Labour time

Awkward access increases the fitted cost.

Extra repairs

Belts, tensioners, batteries, wiring or pulleys may need attention too.

Is it safe to drive with a bad alternator?

Sometimes the car may still drive for a short period, but once the battery charge drops too far, the engine can cut out or fail to restart.

  • !You may get stranded unexpectedly.
  • !Lights, steering assistance and electronics may be affected.
  • !Repeated jump-starting does not fix charging failure.
  • !Diagnosis and repair should be arranged quickly.

What to check before replacing the alternator

  • 1Test the battery, because a weak battery can mimic alternator problems.
  • 2Check charging voltage to confirm whether the alternator is charging correctly.
  • 3Inspect the auxiliary belt, because a loose or broken belt can stop charging.
  • 4Check wiring, fuses and earth connections for poor contact or corrosion.
  • 5Check for unusual pulley, bearing or belt noises before fitting parts.

Frequently asked questions

Can a bad alternator drain a battery?

Yes. If the alternator stops charging properly, the battery will gradually run flat while the car is being used.

Should I replace the battery too?

Sometimes. If the battery has been deeply discharged several times or is already weak, it may need replacing as well.

How long does alternator replacement take?

Often 1 to 3 hours, but some vehicles take longer due to difficult access.

Can the battery warning light mean something else?

Yes. Wiring faults, belt issues, battery problems and poor earth connections can also trigger charging warnings.

Is diagnosis worth paying for?

Yes. Diagnosis can prevent replacing the alternator when the real fault is the battery, belt, wiring or another charging system issue.
