Car Jerks When Changing Gear

If your car jerks, lurches or feels harsh when changing gear, the cause may be clutch wear, poor clutch control, worn engine or gearbox mounts, gearbox faults, throttle response problems, misfires or low-speed drivability issues.

Quick answer

A car jerking during gear changes usually means drive is being interrupted and reapplied too harshly. In manual cars, clutch wear, clutch technique, mounts or drivetrain play are common. In automatic cars, harsh shifting may point to gearbox control, fluid, mounts or engine running issues.

If the jerking is new, getting worse, comes with warning lights, slipping, grinding, burning smells or rough running, it should be checked before it causes further damage.

Manual car jerks when changing gear

In a manual car, gear-change jerkiness often happens when the clutch does not engage smoothly, the engine response is uneven, or the drivetrain moves too much as power is reapplied.

Clutch wear

A worn clutch can make take-up harsh, grabby or inconsistent.

Clutch control

Releasing the clutch too quickly can cause a sharp lurch.

Engine mounts

Weak mounts allow excessive drivetrain movement during shifts.

Clutch hydraulics

Master or slave cylinder faults can affect smooth engagement.

Misfire

Rough engine running can make shifts feel snatchy.

Low-speed drivability

Throttle delay or hesitation can make reapplying power feel abrupt.

Automatic car jerks when changing gear

In an automatic, jerking may feel like a harsh shift, delayed engagement or sudden thump between gears. This can come from transmission behaviour, engine running faults or worn mounts.

  • 1Low or degraded transmission fluid: can affect shift quality on some vehicles.
  • 2Gearbox adaptation issue: some automatic gearboxes adapt to wear and driving style.
  • 3Dual-clutch behaviour: jerking at low speed can be clutch or mechatronic related.
  • 4Engine misfire: poor engine power delivery can feel like gearbox jerk.
  • 5Worn mounts: can make normal shifts feel much harsher.
  • 6Warning light: transmission or engine codes should be diagnosed.

Common causes of jerking during gear changes

Worn clutch

Can cause judder, slipping, grabbing or harsh take-up.

Engine or gearbox mount

Allows excessive movement when drive is taken up.

Throttle response issue

Delayed or uneven throttle response can make shifts feel abrupt.

Misfire or rough running

Uneven power delivery can feel like drivetrain jerk.

Gearbox fault

Harsh, delayed or clunky shifts may need transmission diagnosis.

Driveshaft or drivetrain play

Wear in joints or mountings can create clunks and lurches.

Symptoms to note

  • 1Jerks only between certain gears.
  • 2Feels worse in first, second or reverse.
  • 3Happens more when cold or in traffic.
  • 4Comes with burning clutch smell.
  • 5Engine revs rise but speed does not match.
  • 6There is a clunk when lifting off or reapplying throttle.
  • 7Warning lights, limp mode or rough idle appear too.

What to check first

1. Identify when it happens

Upshifts, downshifts, cold starts, traffic or hill starts all give useful clues.

2. Check clutch behaviour

Look for slipping, judder, high bite point or burning smells in manual cars.

3. Listen for clunks

Clunks can point to worn mounts, driveshafts or drivetrain play.

4. Notice engine smoothness

Rough idle, hesitation or misfire can make gear changes feel jerky.

5. Check warning lights

Engine or gearbox warning lights should be scanned for fault codes.

6. Compare warm and cold

Cold-only jerking may point to engine management, clutch or gearbox behaviour.

Can you keep driving?

If the jerking is mild and clearly related to clutch technique, you may be able to continue carefully. However, if it is worsening, accompanied by slipping, grinding, burning smell, gearbox warning lights, misfire or heavy clunking, arrange inspection soon.

Do not ignore harsh shifts that feel mechanical. Clutch, gearbox and mount faults can become more expensive if left too long.

Repair advice

Avoid assuming the clutch or gearbox is definitely at fault without diagnosis. Engine running faults, mounts and throttle response issues can feel very similar from the driver’s seat.

  • 1Check clutch condition and bite point on manual cars.
  • 2Inspect engine and gearbox mounts for excessive movement.
  • 3Scan for engine or gearbox fault codes if warning lights are present.
  • 4Check for misfires, hesitation or poor throttle response.
  • 5For automatics, ask for gearbox-specific diagnosis before replacing parts.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car jerk when I change gear?

Common causes include clutch wear, poor clutch take-up, worn mounts, gearbox faults, misfires or throttle response issues.

Is gear-change jerking always the clutch?

No. Engine mounts, misfires, throttle delay and gearbox problems can feel similar.

Why does it jerk in first and second gear?

Low gears multiply torque, so clutch, mount and throttle issues often feel stronger there.

Can an automatic jerk between gears?

Yes. Harsh shifts may come from transmission fluid, adaptations, dual-clutch wear, mounts or engine faults.

Can worn engine mounts cause jerking?

Yes. Weak mounts let the drivetrain move sharply when power is reapplied.

When should I get it checked?

If it is new, worsening, clunking, slipping, showing warning lights or causing unsafe driving.
