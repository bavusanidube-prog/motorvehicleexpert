Can Fog Lights Fail MOT?

Yes, fog lights can fail an MOT in the UK, especially if a required rear fog light does not work, shows the wrong colour, is insecure, badly damaged or cannot be switched on correctly. Front fog lights are treated differently, but damaged or unsafe front fog lamps can still cause problems.

Quick answer

A required rear fog light can fail an MOT if it does not work, is missing, insecure, obscured, badly damaged, shows the wrong colour, or has a faulty switch or warning indicator.

Front fog lights are not always required in the same way, but they can still cause MOT issues if they are damaged, insecure, show the wrong colour, dazzle, or create a safety defect.

Rear fog light MOT rules

Rear fog lights are important because they help other drivers see your car in very poor visibility. For many vehicles, at least one required rear fog lamp must work correctly and show a steady red light.

Must work

A required rear fog light should illuminate when switched on.

Must show red

Rear fog lamps should show a steady red light to the rear.

Must be secure

The lamp should not be loose, hanging off or badly mounted.

Must be visible

The lamp should not be obscured, badly faded or hidden by damage.

Switch must work

The fog light switch should operate correctly from the driving position.

Tell-tale should work

The dashboard indicator or switch warning light may also be checked.

Front fog light MOT rules

Front fog lights are not always mandatory MOT items in the same way as rear fog lights. A front fog light bulb being out may not automatically fail every vehicle.

However, front fog lights can still be an issue if they are insecure, damaged, badly modified, show an unsuitable colour, dazzle other road users or leave sharp edges after damage.

Common reasons fog lights can fail MOT

1. Rear fog light not working

A failed bulb, LED unit, fuse, switch or wiring fault can stop the rear fog lamp working.

2. Wrong colour

Rear fog lights should be red. Front fog lights should not show an unsuitable colour.

3. Broken or missing lens

Lens damage can affect colour, brightness, water resistance or lamp security.

4. Loose lamp unit

A fog lamp that is insecure or hanging loose can fail.

5. Faulty switch or tell-tale

A required rear fog lamp must be controllable and show the correct warning indication.

6. Wiring or earth fault

Corrosion, water ingress, damaged wiring or bad earths can cause intermittent operation.

What to check before the MOT

  • 1Turn on the ignition and required lights if your vehicle needs them for fog lamp operation.
  • 2Switch on the rear fog light and confirm it illuminates red.
  • 3Check the fog light dashboard indicator or switch tell-tale works.
  • 4Inspect the lens for cracks, missing pieces, water ingress or fading.
  • 5Make sure the fog lamp is secure and not loose.
  • 6Check front fog lights if fitted, especially if the units are damaged or modified.

Fog light repairs are often simple

Many fog light faults are caused by a failed bulb, blown fuse, corroded connector, bad earth or faulty switch. These can often be repaired before the MOT without major cost.

Modern LED lamp units can be more expensive because the whole unit may need replacing if the LED section fails.

Rear fog light vs front fog light

Rear fog lights help drivers behind see your vehicle in thick fog or very poor visibility. Front fog lights help illuminate the road close to the vehicle, but they are not fitted or required on every car.

This is why rear fog light faults are usually more important for MOT purposes than a front fog light bulb being out.

Frequently asked questions

Will a rear fog light fail MOT?

Yes, if a required rear fog light does not work correctly or is defective.

Will a front fog light fail MOT?

Not always, but damaged, insecure, unsafe or badly modified front fog lights can still cause issues.

Can a cracked fog light lens fail MOT?

Yes, especially if the damage affects colour, light output, security or visibility.

Can a blown rear fog bulb fail MOT?

Yes, if it means the required rear fog lamp does not work.

What colour should a rear fog light be?

A rear fog light should show a steady red light to the rear.

Should I fix fog lights before MOT?

Yes. Lighting faults are usually worth checking and fixing before the test.
