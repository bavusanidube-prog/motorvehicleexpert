Can a Fog Light Fail MOT?

Yes, a fog light can fail MOT depending on which light is faulty, whether it is required on your vehicle, and whether the lamp, switch or warning indicator works correctly. This guide explains UK front and rear fog light MOT rules.

Quick answer

Rear fog lights are usually more important for MOT purposes than front fog lights. If a required rear fog light does not work, has lens damage, incorrect operation or warning indicator faults, it may fail.

Front fog lights are not always test-critical in the same way unless fitted in a way that affects lighting rules or creates a defect.

Rear fog light MOT rules

Must operate properly

A required rear fog light should switch on and function correctly.

Visible red light

The lamp should emit the correct colour and be bright enough to see.

Secure fitting

Loose or damaged lamp units may cause issues.

Lens condition

Cracked or missing lenses can fail if light output is affected.

Switch / tell-tale

Dashboard indicator or switch operation may also matter.

Correct side / position

The required rear fog light must be properly positioned.

Front fog light MOT rules

Front fog lights are not always mandatory MOT items in the same way as rear fog lights. A failed front fog bulb alone may not automatically fail every vehicle.

However, damaged units, insecure lamps, sharp edges or lighting modifications can still create problems.

Common reasons fog lights can fail MOT

1. Rear fog light not working

One of the most common MOT issues.

2. Broken lens

Cracks, missing pieces or white light showing incorrectly.

3. Loose light unit

Lamp insecure or poorly attached.

4. Faulty switch

Cannot activate fog light properly.

5. Warning light issue

Tell-tale indicator may not function as intended.

6. Wiring fault

Corrosion, damaged wiring or bad earth.

What to check before MOT

Turn on sidelights or headlights as required, then activate the fog light switch and confirm the rear fog lamp works.

Check the lens for cracks, water ingress and secure mounting. If unsure, ask a garage to inspect before the MOT.

Frequently asked questions

Will rear fog light fail MOT?

Yes, if a required rear fog light does not work or is defective.

Will front fog light fail MOT?

Not always, but damaged or unsafe units can still cause issues.

Can cracked fog light lens fail MOT?

Yes, especially if output or colour is affected.

Can blown bulb fail MOT?

A rear fog bulb can, depending on the vehicle requirements.

What colour should rear fog light be?

Rear fog lights should display red light.

Should I fix it first?

Yes, lighting faults are often simple and worth fixing before the test.
