Vehicle smell guide

Car smells like rotten eggs

A rotten egg smell from a car is often linked to sulphur compounds and should not be ignored. It may point to catalytic converter problems, battery issues, fuel-system faults or an engine that is not running correctly.

Common causes

Why a car may smell like rotten eggs

This smell usually comes from sulphur gases being produced or not properly processed within the vehicle system.

Exhaust

Catalytic converter problem

A failing or overloaded catalytic converter is one of the most common causes.

Read related guide →
Battery

Battery releasing sulphur smell

A damaged or overcharging battery can sometimes smell like rotten eggs.

Read guide →
Engine Fault

Poor combustion or misfire

Running faults can overload the exhaust system and create unusual smells.

Read guide →
Fuel System

Fuel mixture issues

Too much fuel or related faults can contribute to exhaust smell problems.

Charging

Alternator overcharging battery

Charging-system faults can damage batteries and create strong smells.

Read guide →
Age

Older high-mileage components

Age-related wear can increase the chance of emissions-system issues.

Take it seriously

Warning signs that matter most

  • 1Engine management light appears
  • 2Battery warning light appears
  • 3Loss of power or hesitation
  • 4Strong smell inside cabin
  • 5Smell becomes frequent or stronger
What drivers should do

Better next steps

1. Notice where smell comes from

Rear exhaust area, engine bay or cabin can help narrow likely causes.

2. Check warning lights

Dashboard warnings can point toward charging or engine faults.

3. Avoid ignoring it

Persistent sulphur smells usually need diagnosis.

4. Get it inspected

Prompt checks may prevent more expensive repairs later.
