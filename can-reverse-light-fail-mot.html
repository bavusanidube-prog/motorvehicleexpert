Can Reverse Lights Fail MOT?

Yes, reverse lights can fail an MOT on vehicles where reversing lamps are required to be tested. They should illuminate when reverse gear is selected, show the correct white light, be secure, and switch off when reverse is no longer selected.

Quick answer

A reverse light can fail an MOT if it does not illuminate when reverse is selected, shows the wrong colour, stays on constantly, flickers, has a damaged lens, is insecure or has a wiring fault.

The fault is often simple, such as a bulb, fuse, connector or reverse light switch.

When reverse lights can fail an MOT

Light not working

The reversing lamp does not illuminate when reverse gear is selected.

Wrong colour

Reverse lights should normally show white light to the rear.

Damaged lens

Cracked, missing or faded lenses can affect visibility or colour.

Loose lamp unit

Rear lamp units should be secure and not hanging loose.

Stays on constantly

Reverse lights should not stay on when the vehicle is not in reverse.

Intermittent fault

Flickering or unreliable operation can point to wiring or switch issues.

Common causes of reverse light faults

  • 1Blown bulb: common on vehicles with replaceable bulbs.
  • 2Reverse light switch fault: often fitted on or near the gearbox.
  • 3Damaged wiring: wires near the boot lid or rear lamp can break or corrode.
  • 4Poor earth connection: can cause dim, flickering or strange rear light behaviour.
  • 5Water ingress: moisture inside the lamp can damage contacts.
  • 6LED lamp unit fault: some modern cars may need a complete lamp unit.

What to check before the MOT

1. Select reverse safely

With the car secure, ignition on and engine off if suitable, check whether the lamp illuminates.

2. Use a helper

Ask someone to stand behind the car while reverse is selected safely.

3. Check the colour

The reverse light should show a clear white light, not red, amber or heavily discoloured light.

4. Inspect the lens

Look for cracks, missing pieces, water ingress or loose rear lamp sections.

5. Check it switches off

Reverse lights should go out when reverse gear is deselected.

6. Investigate repeat faults

If bulbs keep failing, check wiring, holders, water ingress and earth points.

Reverse light repairs are often simple

Many reverse light faults are quick to repair if the cause is a failed bulb, dirty connector, blown fuse or poor bulb holder contact.

If the bulb is fine but the light never comes on, the reverse light switch or wiring may need diagnosis.

Manual and automatic cars

On manual cars, the reverse light is often triggered by a gearbox switch. On automatic cars, the signal may be linked to the gear selector, transmission range switch or body control module.

If the light stays on in the wrong gear, get it checked before the MOT.

Frequently asked questions

Will one reverse light out fail MOT?

It can if that vehicle requires the reverse light to operate correctly.

What colour should reverse lights be?

Reverse lights should normally show white light.

Can reverse lights stuck on fail?

Yes, if they operate incorrectly or confuse other road users.

Is it usually just a bulb?

Often yes, but switches, wiring, fuses and earth faults can also cause problems.

Do LED reverse lights fail too?

Yes. LED units can fail, although the repair may involve replacing a full lamp unit.

Should I fix it before MOT?

Yes. Lighting faults are usually worth checking before the test.
