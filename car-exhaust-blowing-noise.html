Car Exhaust Blowing Noise

If your car sounds louder than normal, hisses, chuffs or makes a blowing noise from underneath, the cause is often an exhaust leak, failed flexi pipe, loose joint, damaged silencer or manifold gasket leak.

Quick answer

An exhaust blowing noise usually means exhaust gases are escaping before they should. Small leaks often become louder under acceleration and may get worse as the exhaust heats up, moves or corrodes further.

Exhaust leaks should be checked promptly, especially if you smell fumes inside the car, hear rattling underneath, or your MOT is due soon.

Common causes of an exhaust blowing noise

Exhaust hole

Rust holes in pipes or boxes can create a blowing, chuffing or roaring sound.

Broken flexi pipe

The flexible section near the engine commonly fails and can sound loud under load.

Loose clamp or joint

Poor sealing between sections can let gases escape and make a hissing noise.

Damaged silencer

A failed back box or centre silencer can make the car much louder than normal.

Broken hanger

A loose exhaust can strain joints, knock underneath and create leaks.

Manifold gasket leak

Leaks near the engine can sound sharper, especially when cold or accelerating.

What the exhaust noise can tell you

Blowing from front

May point to the manifold, gasket, downpipe or flexi section.

Blowing from middle

Often linked to pipe joints, clamps, centre sections or corrosion.

Loud from rear

A rear silencer or back box fault is common.

Rattle plus blowing

A broken mount, loose heat shield or loose exhaust section may also be involved.

What to check first

  • 1Notice whether the noise is front, middle or rear of the car.
  • 2Check if it gets louder under acceleration.
  • 3Listen when cold and again once warm.
  • 4Look for a hanging tailpipe or loose exhaust section.
  • 5Take exhaust smells inside the cabin seriously.
  • 6Check old MOT advisories for exhaust corrosion or mounting defects.

Can you drive with a blowing exhaust?

A small leak may not stop the car moving, but it can get worse quickly and may affect emissions, noise, fuel economy and safety. If fumes enter the cabin, avoid driving and get the car inspected urgently.

A loose or hanging exhaust can detach or strike the road, so do not ignore rattling or visible movement underneath.

Can an exhaust blowing noise fail an MOT?

Yes. An exhaust can fail an MOT if it is leaking, insecure, excessively noisy, missing required emissions equipment, or causing emissions problems.

For more detail, read Can an Exhaust Fail MOT?, Can Exhaust Mount Fail MOT? and Common MOT Failure Reasons UK.

Typical UK repair costs

Clamp or gasket

Often lower cost if the pipework is still solid.

Flexi pipe repair

Usually moderate cost depending on access and whether welding is needed.

Silencer or section

Costs vary by vehicle, exhaust layout and parts availability.

If the exhaust is badly corroded in several places, replacing a complete section may be more sensible than patching one small hole.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my exhaust making a blowing noise?

Usually because exhaust gases are leaking from a hole, failed joint, flexi pipe or gasket.

Is a blowing exhaust dangerous?

It can be, especially if fumes enter the cabin or the exhaust is loose.

Will a blowing exhaust fail MOT?

Yes, it can fail if leaking, insecure, too noisy or affecting emissions.

Why is it louder when accelerating?

Exhaust gas flow and engine load increase, making leaks easier to hear.

Can a flexi pipe cause blowing?

Yes. A failed flexi section is a common front exhaust leak point.

Should I repair it before MOT?

Yes. Exhaust leaks and insecure mounts are best fixed before the test.
