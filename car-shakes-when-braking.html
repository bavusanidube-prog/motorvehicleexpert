Brake vibration guide

Car Shakes When Braking

If your steering wheel, brake pedal or whole car shakes when braking, the cause is often brake disc surface variation, worn pads, a sticking caliper, tyre issues or worn suspension parts. Brake vibration should be checked because it can affect braking confidence and control.

Quick answer

A car shaking when braking is commonly caused by uneven brake discs, pad deposits, worn brake pads, a sticking caliper, suspension wear, tyre problems or wheel balance issues.

If the shake is strong, the car pulls to one side, the brake pedal pulses heavily or there is grinding noise, arrange inspection soon.

Common causes of shaking when braking

Brake disc variation

Uneven disc surfaces can cause vibration when pads clamp down.

Pad deposits

Uneven pad material can feel similar to warped discs.

Worn brake pads

Low or uneven pads can create vibration, noise and poor feel.

Sticking caliper

Uneven braking force may cause shake, heat and pulling.

Suspension wear

Worn bushes or joints can move under braking load.

Tyre or wheel issue

Existing vibration can become more noticeable when slowing down.

Where you feel the shake matters

Steering wheel shakes

Often points toward front brake discs, front tyres or front suspension.

Brake pedal pulses

Often linked to disc surface variation or braking imbalance.

Whole car vibrates

Rear brakes, tyres, wheels or multiple faults may be involved.

Car pulls while braking

May suggest brake imbalance, sticking caliper or suspension movement.

When brake shake needs faster attention

  • !The brake pedal pulses heavily.
  • !The steering wheel shakes strongly at speed.
  • !The car pulls left or right when braking.
  • !You hear grinding, scraping or knocking noises.
  • !One wheel smells hot or feels much hotter than others.
  • !Brake warning lights appear.

What to check next

1. Notice when it happens

Only braking, only high speed, or all the time are different clues.

2. Listen for brake noise

Grinding or scraping can point to worn pads or damaged discs.

3. Check tyre condition

Bulges, uneven wear and poor pressure can worsen vibration.

4. Check for pulling

Pulling under braking can suggest imbalance or caliper issues.

5. Avoid heavy braking if severe

Drive gently and arrange inspection if the car feels unstable.

6. Inspect brakes and suspension

A garage can check discs, pads, calipers, bushes and joints.

Typical UK repair costs

Brake inspection

Often low cost, sometimes included with repair quotes.

Discs and pads

Usually moderate cost, depending on vehicle and axle.

Caliper or suspension repair

Can cost more if parts are seized, worn or difficult to access.

Frequently asked questions

Does shaking always mean warped discs?

No. Pad deposits, suspension wear, tyres and calipers can feel similar.

Can tyres cause shaking when braking?

Yes. Tyre or wheel faults can become more obvious under braking load.

Should pads be changed with discs?

Usually yes, so the new braking surfaces bed in correctly.

Is brake vibration dangerous?

It can be if severe, worsening or joined by pulling, noise or poor pedal feel.

Can suspension cause brake shake?

Yes. Worn bushes or joints can move when braking forces load the front end.

Best first step?

Inspect brakes, tyres and front suspension before replacing parts blindly.
