Do You Need an MOT to Insure a Car?

You can often buy or renew car insurance without an MOT, but that does not mean you can legally drive the car. If the vehicle needs an MOT and the certificate has expired, it may affect road use, insurance claims, vehicle tax and the way your insurer deals with an accident.

Quick answer

Insurance and MOT are separate things. In many cases, you may still be able to arrange insurance for a car without a current MOT. However, if the car legally requires an MOT, you normally cannot use it on the road unless it has a valid certificate or you are driving to a pre-booked MOT test or eligible repair appointment.

An expired MOT does not always automatically cancel an insurance policy, but it can cause serious problems. Insurers usually expect the car to be roadworthy, legally used and properly maintained.

Can you insure a car without an MOT?

Yes, it can be possible to insure a car without an MOT. For example, you may be buying a car that is off the road, arranging cover before an MOT test, or renewing a policy before the car is back in use.

The important point is that insurance does not replace the need for an MOT. If the vehicle is old enough to need an MOT, it must have one before normal road use.

New insurance policy

Some insurers may allow cover to start without an MOT, especially if the car is not being used normally yet.

Insurance renewal

A policy may renew separately from MOT status, but you still need to meet the policy conditions.

Roadworthiness

The car must still be safe and roadworthy. An MOT is not a substitute for maintenance.

Policy wording

Some insurers specifically require a valid MOT where one is legally needed.

Honest answers

Answer insurer questions accurately when taking out or renewing cover.

Off-road vehicles

Cars kept off road may still need insurance for fire, theft or storage, depending on your situation.

Can you drive a car without MOT if it is insured?

Usually, no. If your MOT has expired, you cannot normally drive or park the vehicle on a public road. Being insured does not make the car legal to use without an MOT.

The usual exceptions are driving to or from a pre-booked MOT test, or to or from a place of repair where eligible. You should be able to prove the appointment if stopped, and the car must still be insured and safe enough to drive.

If you are unsure how long your MOT lasts, read how long is an MOT valid?

Can no MOT affect an insurance claim?

Yes. An expired MOT can make an insurance claim more difficult, especially if the car was being driven when it should not have been, or if a defect contributed to the accident.

  • 1Insurer checks: the insurer may check MOT history after an accident.
  • 2Roadworthiness: unsafe defects can weaken your position in a claim.
  • 3Policy conditions: some policies require the vehicle to have a valid MOT where legally required.
  • 4Vehicle value: a car without MOT may be valued lower if it is written off.
  • 5Legal use: driving illegally can create extra problems beyond the insurance claim.

The safest approach is simple: keep the MOT valid, repair dangerous defects quickly and keep service or repair records where possible.

MOT, insurance and car tax: how they link together

MOT, insurance and vehicle tax are separate requirements, but they often depend on each other in practice. To tax a car that legally needs an MOT, you normally need a valid MOT certificate. You also need valid insurance to use the car on the road.

Insurance

Protects against insured risks, but the car must meet policy conditions.

MOT

Confirms the car met minimum safety and environmental standards on the test date.

Vehicle tax

Usually cannot be renewed if the MOT has expired on a car that needs one.

What to do if your MOT has expired

If your MOT has expired, do not keep using the car as normal. Book an MOT test as soon as possible and avoid unnecessary journeys until the vehicle is legal again.

  • ✓Check the MOT expiry date online or on your certificate.
  • ✓Book an MOT test before driving the car.
  • ✓Make sure the vehicle is insured before the journey.
  • ✓Only drive to a pre-booked MOT test or eligible repair appointment.
  • ✓Fix known safety defects before driving.
  • ✓Tax the car once it has passed, if tax is also due.

If the MOT has failed, read MOT retest rules UK and common MOT failure reasons.

What if you are buying a car with no MOT?

A car with no MOT can sometimes be insured, but it should not be driven normally on the road until it has a valid MOT, unless a legal exception applies. If you are buying the car, think carefully about recovery, transport, repairs and the risk of hidden faults.

Before buying, check its MOT history, inspect the car properly and budget for repairs. These guides may help: buying a car with failed MOT history, used car inspection checklist and how to check MOT history before buying a car.

Frequently asked questions

Do you need an MOT before getting insurance?

Not always. Some insurers may still provide cover without a current MOT, but if the vehicle legally needs an MOT, you should not use it on the road as normal until it has one.

Does no MOT automatically void insurance?

Not in every possible situation, but it can create serious claim problems. Many insurers require the car to be roadworthy, legally used and properly maintained.

Can I drive to an MOT test without MOT?

Yes, if the MOT test is pre-booked, the car is insured and the vehicle is safe enough to drive. You should go directly to the appointment and keep proof of the booking.

Can I park a car on the road without MOT?

No, if the vehicle legally needs an MOT. A car without a valid MOT should not normally be driven or parked on a public road.

Can I tax a car without MOT?

Usually not if the vehicle legally requires an MOT. You normally need a valid MOT before taxing it.

What happens if I have an accident with no MOT?

The insurer may investigate the car’s condition, MOT status and whether the vehicle was being used legally. A claim may be reduced, disputed or refused depending on the circumstances and policy wording.
