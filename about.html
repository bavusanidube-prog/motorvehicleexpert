About the creator

Hi, I’m Bavusani Otis Dube

I created Motor Vehicle Expert to make car problems easier to understand for everyday UK drivers. My background includes automotive training, vehicle systems knowledge and practical experience around vehicle faults, servicing, MOT-related issues and repair decisions.

The aim of this website is simple: explain common vehicle problems in a way drivers can actually use. Whether your car has a warning light, a strange noise, a smell, a starting problem, overheating, coolant loss, braking symptoms or an MOT concern, the guides are written to help you understand what may be happening and what to check next.

The information is written in plain English with a mechanic-style approach. The pages focus on real symptoms, likely causes, safety, MOT implications, repair-cost awareness and sensible next steps before spending money.

How the guides are written

Mechanic-Style Advice Without Overcomplicating It

Many car websites explain faults in a way that feels too technical. Motor Vehicle Expert is different. The pages are written around real driving situations: the battery light comes on, the brake pedal feels soft, the car overheats in traffic, the engine management light flashes, or the car shakes when braking.

Each guide aims to explain what the symptom may mean, how serious it could be, what checks make sense first, and which related faults may need professional diagnosis.

  • ✓Plain wording for everyday drivers.
  • ✓Safety-first advice for serious warning signs.
  • ✓Strong internal links to related fault guides.
  • ✓UK-focused advice around MOT, repairs and ownership.
Important note

General Guidance, Not A Final Diagnosis

The information on this website is designed to help drivers understand possible causes and next checks. It does not replace a physical inspection, proper diagnostic scan, qualified mechanic, MOT tester or legal advice.

If your vehicle has serious symptoms such as brake failure, oil pressure warning, overheating, smoke, coolant loss, fuel smell, steering difficulty or unsafe handling, stop safely and get professional help.

Trust and safety

How To Use The Information Safely

Car symptoms can have more than one cause. A warning light, noise, smell or vibration may point to a simple issue or something more serious depending on the vehicle, mileage, service history and driving conditions.

Use the guides to understand likely causes and sensible checks, but do not ignore serious symptoms such as oil warning lights, brake warning lights, overheating, smoke, fuel smells, coolant loss or unsafe braking.

If the vehicle feels unsafe, stop driving and arrange professional help.
