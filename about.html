About the creator

Hi, I’m Bavusani Otis Dube

I created Motor Vehicle Expert to make car problems easier to understand for everyday UK drivers. My background includes automotive training, vehicle systems knowledge, MOT-related training and long-term experience in the automotive and vehicle manufacturing industry.

The aim of this site is simple: explain common vehicle problems in a way drivers can actually use. Whether your car has a warning light, a noise, a smell, a starting problem, an overheating fault or an MOT concern, the guides are written to help you understand what may be happening and what to check next.

The information is written in plain English with a mechanic-style approach, focusing on symptoms, likely causes, safety, MOT implications and sensible next steps.

Purpose

Why Motor Vehicle Expert Exists

Most drivers do not need complicated workshop language. They need to know what a symptom might mean, how serious it could be, whether it may affect safety or MOT, and when to get the car checked professionally.

Motor Vehicle Expert is designed for real situations: a dashboard warning light comes on, the car starts making a noise, the heater stops working, the MOT is due, a repair quote feels expensive, or you are checking a used car before buying.

The information on this website is general guidance only. It is not a replacement for professional inspection, diagnosis, repair, MOT testing or legal advice.

Trust and safety

How To Use The Information Safely

Car symptoms can have more than one cause. A warning light, noise, smell or vibration may point to a simple issue or something more serious depending on the vehicle, mileage, service history and driving conditions.

Use the guides to understand likely causes and sensible checks, but do not ignore serious symptoms such as oil warning lights, brake warning lights, overheating, smoke, fuel smells, coolant loss or unsafe braking.

If the vehicle feels unsafe, stop driving and arrange professional help.
