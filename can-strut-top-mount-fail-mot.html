Can Strut Top Mount Fail MOT?

Yes, a strut top mount can fail an MOT in the UK if it is excessively worn, loose, insecure, damaged or causing unsafe suspension movement. Top mounts help secure the suspension strut and can affect steering feel, ride comfort, tyre wear and vehicle safety.

Quick answer

A strut top mount can fail an MOT if it has excessive play, loose fixings, badly worn rubber, a damaged bearing, visible movement or any defect that affects suspension security.

Minor rubber wear may receive an advisory, but serious looseness, knocking or insecure mounting should be repaired before the test.

When a strut top mount can fail an MOT

Excessive play

Too much movement in the top mount can affect suspension control and steering stability.

Loose fixing

Loose nuts, bolts or mounting hardware can make the suspension insecure.

Split rubber

Badly deteriorated rubber can reduce support and allow abnormal movement.

Worn bearing

A rough or seized top mount bearing can affect steering rotation on front strut setups.

Corrosion or damage

Rust, cracks or impact damage around the mounting area can become an MOT issue.

Unsafe suspension movement

Any defect that affects suspension security or vehicle control can fail.

Common symptoms of worn strut top mounts

  • !Clunking over bumps: often heard from the front suspension area.
  • !Creaking when steering: can point to a worn or dry top mount bearing.
  • !Knocking at low speed: especially over speed bumps, potholes or uneven roads.
  • !Heavy or uneven steering: the steering may feel rough, notchy or reluctant to self-centre.
  • !Visible movement: the strut top may move more than expected when steering or bouncing the car.
  • !Uneven tyre wear: suspension movement can affect alignment and tyre contact.

Strut top mount bearings

On many front suspension designs, the strut top mount includes a bearing that allows the strut to rotate as you steer. If this bearing wears, dries out or seizes, it can cause creaking, spring twang, notchy steering or clunks.

Worn bearing

May cause creaking, grinding or rough steering movement.

Seized bearing

Can make the spring twist and release suddenly when steering.

Rubber mount wear

Can allow the strut to move excessively over bumps.

Mounting corrosion

Rust around the turret or mounting area should be checked carefully.

Pre-MOT strut top mount checks

  • 1Listen for clunks, knocks or creaks over bumps.
  • 2Turn the steering from lock to lock and listen for grinding, twanging or creaking.
  • 3Check whether the steering feels smooth and returns normally.
  • 4Inspect visible top mount areas under the bonnet for rust, movement or loose fixings.
  • 5Look for uneven tyre wear that could suggest suspension or alignment issues.
  • 6Ask a garage to inspect suspension play on a ramp if you hear knocking.

Should you replace strut top mounts before MOT?

Yes, if there is clear knocking, excessive movement, rough steering or visible damage. Strut top mounts are suspension safety components, and faults can worsen over time.

Top mounts are often replaced when fitting new shock absorbers or struts because much of the same labour is involved. If one side is worn, the other side should also be checked.

Typical UK repair costs

Top mount replacement

Often moderate cost, depending on access and suspension design.

Top mount and bearing

Commonly replaced together if the bearing is noisy or rough.

Strut and mount together

Can cost more, but may be sensible if the shock absorber is also worn or leaking.

After suspension work, wheel alignment may be recommended, especially if the car pulls, the steering wheel sits off-centre or tyres are wearing unevenly.

Top mount faults can mimic other suspension problems

A knocking strut top mount can sound similar to worn drop links, ball joints, lower arms, shock absorbers or track rod ends. A proper inspection is important before replacing parts.

Frequently asked questions

Can noisy top mounts fail MOT?

Yes, if the noise is linked to excessive wear, looseness or unsafe suspension movement.

Are strut top mounts checked on MOT?

Yes, they can be checked as part of suspension condition and security.

Do top mounts affect steering?

Yes, especially on front strut suspension where the mount includes a bearing.

Can worn rubber be an advisory?

Minor deterioration may be an advisory, but serious movement or damage can fail.

Should top mounts be replaced in pairs?

Not always, but many garages recommend checking both sides for even wear and balanced suspension behaviour.

Best next step before MOT?

Have knocking, creaking, visible movement or rough steering inspected before the test.
