Can You Drive to an MOT Without Tax?

Many drivers ask whether they can drive directly to a pre-booked MOT test without tax. UK rules can depend on circumstances, so it is important to understand the risks and keep the journey strictly for the appointment.

Quick answer

Driving to a genuine pre-booked MOT appointment is commonly discussed as a limited exception scenario, but you should ensure the vehicle is roadworthy and the trip is direct to the test centre.

If unsure, verify current official guidance before travelling.

General rules to understand

Pre-booked test

Keep evidence of the booking.

Direct journey

Travel straight to the appointment.

Roadworthy car

Unsafe vehicles should not be driven.

Insurance needed

Ensure valid cover remains in place.

No extra trips

Avoid unrelated stops.

Proof matters

Save confirmation emails or texts.

Risks to avoid

  • 1Driving an unsafe vehicle
  • 2No genuine MOT booking
  • 3Using the car for other journeys
  • 4No insurance in place
  • 5Poor documentation if stopped

Best practice before travelling

Check tyres, brakes, lights and fluid levels. If the car has dangerous faults, use recovery or transport instead of driving it.

Keep the booking confirmation with you.
