UK MOT travel guide

Can You Drive to an MOT Without Tax?

Yes, in limited circumstances you can usually drive an untaxed vehicle to a pre-arranged MOT test. But this is not permission to use the car normally. The journey must be for the MOT, the vehicle must be insured, and it must be roadworthy enough to drive safely.

GOV.UK says that if the MOT has run out, you cannot drive or park the vehicle on the road except to or from repair, or to a pre-arranged MOT test. GOV.UK also says you cannot renew vehicle tax if the MOT has expired, and you should tax the vehicle once it has passed.

Quick answer

Can you drive an untaxed car to an MOT?

Usually yes, but only for a genuine pre-booked MOT journey, with valid insurance and a vehicle that is safe enough to drive.

The safest approach is: book the MOT first, keep proof of the booking, make sure the vehicle is insured, drive directly to the test centre, and do not use the vehicle for unrelated journeys.

Important

This is not normal road use

The MOT journey exception is narrow. It does not mean you can drive around for errands, commuting, shopping or testing the car on the road.

If the car is untaxed or MOT-expired, keep the journey directly connected to the MOT or pre-arranged repair.

Basic rule

The basic rule: pre-booked MOT only

Before driving, make sure the MOT appointment is real and already booked.

1

Book the MOT first

Have a genuine appointment before driving the vehicle.

2

Drive directly

Do not use the trip for shopping, commuting, school runs or errands.

3

Keep proof

Save the booking email, text message or garage confirmation.

Use a sensible route

Avoid unnecessary detours or long unrelated journeys.

Do not park on road

An untaxed or MOT-expired vehicle should not be left parked on public roads.

Tax after it passes

Once it has a valid MOT, arrange vehicle tax before normal road use.

Insurance

Insurance still matters

The MOT appointment exception does not remove the need for valid insurance. If the car is not insured, do not drive it to the test.

It is also sensible to check your policy position if the vehicle has no MOT, because some insurers may have conditions around roadworthiness and expired MOT use.

Roadworthy

The car must still be safe

The vehicle must be safe enough to use on the road. If it has dangerous defects such as bald tyres, failed brakes, serious steering faults, broken lights or severe structural damage, arrange recovery or transport instead.

The MOT booking does not make a dangerous vehicle safe to drive.

SORN

Can you drive a SORN car to an MOT?

A SORN vehicle may be driven to a pre-arranged MOT test in limited circumstances, but it still needs insurance and it must be roadworthy enough for the journey.

Do not use a SORN vehicle for normal road use. Keep the journey strictly connected to the MOT or repair appointment.

Practical tip

Keep the booking details handy

If you are stopped or questioned, clear booking proof helps show the journey is genuinely connected to a pre-arranged MOT.

Keep the garage name, appointment time, address and confirmation message available.

Risks to avoid

1

2

3

4

5

6

Forgetting tax afterwards

Once the car passes, tax it before using it normally.

After test

What happens after the MOT?

The next step depends on whether the vehicle passes, fails or has dangerous defects.

Pass

If it passes

Tax the vehicle before using it normally on the road.

Fail

If it fails

Dangerous

If dangerous defects are found

Do not drive it until repaired. Arrange repair or recovery.

Vehicle tax

Can you tax a car without an MOT?

Usually no. If the car needs an MOT, you normally need a valid MOT before you can tax it.

That is why the usual order is: book MOT, drive only to the test if allowed, pass MOT, then tax the vehicle.

Real-world advice

Do not leave it until the last minute

If the car has been off the road, book the MOT early and check the basics before travelling. Tyres, lights, brakes, wipers and warning lights are common avoidable problems.

If you already know the car has serious defects, transport it instead of driving.
