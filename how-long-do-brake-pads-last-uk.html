Brake maintenance guide

How long do brake pads last in the UK?

Brake pad life varies widely, but many drivers see roughly 20,000 to 50,000 miles depending on driving style, vehicle weight, traffic, road type, brake quality and maintenance. Some pads wear sooner, while gentle motorway driving can make them last longer.

Typical brake pad lifespan

As a general guide, brake pads can last around 20,000 to 50,000 miles, but this is only an estimate. City driving, heavy braking and larger vehicles can wear pads much faster.

Brake pads should be judged by actual condition, not mileage alone. A visual brake inspection during servicing is the best way to know how much pad material remains.

What affects brake pad life?

  • 1Driving style: harsh braking wears pads faster than smooth, planned braking.
  • 2Urban driving: stop-start traffic uses the brakes more often than motorway driving.
  • 3Vehicle weight: heavier cars, SUVs and vans put more demand on the braking system.
  • 4Brake pad quality: cheaper pads may wear faster or create more noise and dust.
  • 5Brake disc condition: scored, rusty or uneven discs can shorten pad life.
  • 6Sticking calipers: a seized or dragging brake can wear one pad much faster than the others.

Signs your brake pads may be worn

  • ✓Squealing, scraping or grinding noises when braking.
  • ✓Brake warning light or pad wear sensor message on some vehicles.
  • ✓Longer stopping distance or weaker braking feel.
  • ✓Vibration, judder or pulsing through the brake pedal.
  • ✓Very thin pad material visible through the wheel.
  • ✓Car pulling to one side when braking.

Front brake pads

Front pads usually wear faster because the front brakes do more work when the car slows down.

Rear brake pads

Rear pads may last longer, but electronic handbrakes, seized calipers or short trips can still cause wear.

Discs should be checked too

Pads and discs work together. New pads fitted to badly worn discs may not perform correctly.

Can worn brake pads fail an MOT?

Yes. Brake pads can fail an MOT if they are excessively worn, unsafe, contaminated, damaged or causing poor braking performance.

Brakes may also fail due to imbalance, fluid leaks, damaged discs, binding calipers or poor parking brake performance.

Best brake maintenance habits

1. Check during servicing

Ask for pad thickness, disc condition and brake fluid condition to be inspected.

2. Do not ignore noise

Grinding can mean the pad material is gone and metal is contacting the disc.

3. Replace axle pairs

Brake pads are normally replaced in pairs across the same axle.

4. Investigate uneven wear

One pad wearing faster can suggest a caliper, slider or brake hardware issue.

Frequently asked questions

Can brake pads last only 20,000 miles?

Yes. Heavy urban use, hard braking, automatic cars, heavy vehicles and poor-quality pads can all reduce lifespan.

Can brake pads last over 50,000 miles?

Yes. Gentle motorway driving and lighter vehicles can allow pads to last much longer.

Should brake discs be replaced with pads?

Not always, but discs should be checked for thickness, scoring, corrosion, cracks and vibration.

Is grinding brakes serious?

Yes. Grinding can mean the pads are badly worn and the braking system should be inspected urgently.

How often should brake pads be checked?

They should be checked during routine servicing and whenever you notice noise, vibration, poor braking or warning lights.
