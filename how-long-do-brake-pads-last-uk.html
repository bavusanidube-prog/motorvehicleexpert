Brake Maintenance Guide

How Long Do Brake Pads Last in the UK?

Brake pads usually last between 20,000 and 50,000 miles, although some wear out sooner and others last much longer. Lifespan depends on driving style, traffic, vehicle weight, road conditions and the quality of the brake pads fitted.

Typical brake pad lifespan

For many UK drivers, brake pads last somewhere between 20,000 and 50,000 miles. This is only a guide, not a fixed rule.

Cars used mainly in town traffic often wear pads faster because of repeated stop-start braking. Vehicles driven mostly on motorways may see much longer pad life.

Mileage alone should never be the only measure. Regular inspections are the safest way to check how much friction material remains.

What affects brake pad life?

  • 1Driving style: harsh braking wears pads faster than smooth, planned braking.
  • 2Urban driving: town traffic uses the brakes more often than motorway driving.
  • 3Vehicle weight: heavier cars, SUVs and vans place more load on the brakes.
  • 4Pad quality: better quality pads may last longer and produce less dust or noise.
  • 5Brake disc condition: worn or uneven discs can shorten pad life.
  • 6Sticking calipers: dragging brakes can wear one side much faster.

Signs your brake pads may be worn

  • ✓Squealing, scraping or grinding noises when braking.
  • ✓Longer stopping distances.
  • ✓Brake warning light or pad wear warning on some vehicles.
  • ✓Vibration or judder when braking.
  • ✓Very thin pads visible through the wheel.
  • ✓Vehicle pulling to one side when braking.

Front brake pads

Front pads usually wear out first because most braking force is applied through the front wheels.

Rear brake pads

Rear pads often last longer, although electronic handbrakes and seized components can increase wear.

Check the discs too

Pads and discs work together. Fitting new pads to badly worn discs may reduce braking performance.

Can worn brake pads fail an MOT?

Yes. Brake pads can fail an MOT if they are excessively worn, damaged, contaminated or causing poor braking performance.

Read more in our guide to can brake pads fail an MOT.

How much does brake pad replacement cost in the UK?

If your pads need replacing, most drivers pay between £90 and £250 per axle, depending on the car and garage.

Best brake maintenance habits

1. Check during servicing

Ask for pad thickness, disc wear and brake fluid condition to be inspected.

2. Do not ignore noise

Grinding can mean the friction material has worn away.

3. Replace in axle pairs

Pads are normally replaced in pairs on the same axle.

4. Investigate uneven wear

Uneven wear can suggest a sticking caliper or slider problem.

Frequently asked questions

Can brake pads last only 20,000 miles?

Yes. Frequent town driving, heavy traffic and hard braking can shorten pad life.

Can brake pads last over 50,000 miles?

Yes. Lighter cars and gentle motorway driving can make pads last much longer.

Should brake discs be replaced with pads?

Not always, but discs should be checked for wear, scoring, corrosion and vibration.

How often should brake pads be checked?

They should be checked during servicing and whenever you notice warning signs.

Are cheap brake pads worth buying?

Lower-cost pads can save money, but quality and lifespan may differ. Reputable brands are usually the safer option.
