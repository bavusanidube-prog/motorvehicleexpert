Car Juddering When Accelerating

If your car shakes, vibrates or judders when accelerating, the cause may be engine misfire, clutch wear, fuel faults, turbo issues, driveshaft problems or wheel-related faults. This guide explains common reasons and what UK drivers should check next.

Quick answer

A car juddering when accelerating is commonly caused by engine misfire, clutch slip or clutch contamination, worn driveshaft joints, wheel imbalance, fuel delivery faults or turbo/boost problems.

If the engine warning light appears, the car shakes badly, loses power or makes loud noises, arrange diagnosis soon.

Common causes of juddering under acceleration

Engine misfire

Misfires can cause shaking and hesitation under load.

Clutch problem

Worn or contaminated clutches can judder as power is applied.

Driveshaft / CV joint

Wear can cause vibration during acceleration.

Wheel balance issue

Some vibrations become worse with speed and load.

Fuel delivery fault

Poor fueling can feel like juddering or surging.

Turbo boost fault

Turbo power fluctuations can cause shuddering.

Engine mount wear

Excess engine movement may be felt on throttle.

Tyre issue

Damaged tyres can mimic drivetrain judder.

Transmission fault

Some automatic gearboxes can shudder under load.

Engine or drivetrain: how to tell the difference

Engine-related

Jerking, hesitation, warning lights or rough running.

Clutch / gearbox

Judder during pull-away or gear changes.

Wheels / shafts

Vibration linked to road speed rather than engine revs.

When does the judder happen?

Only pulling away

Often clutch or mount related.

Only high speed

Wheel balance, tyres or shafts may be likely.

Only hard acceleration

Misfire, boost or drivetrain load faults are common.

Only uphill

Load can expose clutch slip or weak power delivery.

With warning light

Fault codes may identify engine issues.

With noise

Clicks or knocks can indicate joints or mounts.

When juddering needs faster attention

  • !Heavy shaking or unsafe driving feel.
  • !Flashing engine management light.
  • !Burning clutch smell.
  • !Loud knocking or grinding noises.
  • !Power loss becomes severe.
  • !The car struggles to move off.

What to check next

1. Notice speed vs revs

Road speed or engine speed clues matter.

2. Check warning lights

Codes can quickly identify misfires or sensors.

3. Look at tyre condition

Tyres can cause vibration symptoms.

4. Check service history

Old plugs or neglected servicing may contribute.

5. Avoid guessing parts

Several faults feel similar to drivers.

6. Arrange inspection

A road test often helps pinpoint the source.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car judder when accelerating?

Commonly engine misfire, clutch or drivetrain related.

Can spark plugs cause juddering?

Yes. Misfires can feel like shaking under load.

Can tyres cause this?

Yes. Tyre or balance faults can cause vibration.

Can clutch wear cause juddering?

Yes, especially pulling away or under load.

Should I keep driving?

Avoid if the shaking is severe or worsening.

Will diagnostics help?

Yes, especially if warning lights are present.
