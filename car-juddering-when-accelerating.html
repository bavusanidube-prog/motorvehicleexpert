Car Juddering When Accelerating

If your car judders, shakes or vibrates when accelerating, the cause may be an engine misfire, clutch fault, fuel delivery problem, turbo or boost issue, driveshaft wear, engine mount movement, tyre defect or wheel vibration.

Quick answer

Juddering under acceleration usually happens when the engine, clutch, gearbox, driveshafts, mounts, wheels or tyres cannot deliver power smoothly under load.

If the engine management light flashes, the car loses power, the shaking is severe, there is a burning smell, or the car feels unsafe, stop driving and arrange inspection as soon as possible.

Common causes of juddering under acceleration

Engine misfire

A misfire can make the car shake, hesitate or judder most noticeably under load.

Clutch problem

A worn, slipping or contaminated clutch can judder when power is applied.

Driveshaft or CV joint

Wear in driveshaft joints can create vibration during acceleration.

Fuel delivery fault

Poor fuel pressure or uneven fuel flow can feel like surging or juddering.

Turbo boost issue

Unstable boost can make turbocharged cars shudder or surge under throttle.

Engine mount wear

Weak mounts allow excessive engine movement when power is applied.

Wheel balance issue

Vibration that increases with road speed may be wheel or tyre related.

Damaged tyre

Bulges, flat spots or tyre damage can mimic drivetrain vibration.

Transmission fault

Some manual and automatic gearbox faults can shudder under load.

Engine or drivetrain: how to tell the difference

Engine-related judder

Often comes with hesitation, rough idle, warning lights, poor fuel economy or power loss.

Clutch or gearbox judder

Often strongest when pulling away, changing gear, climbing hills or reapplying power.

Wheel or driveshaft vibration

Often linked to road speed and may continue even if engine revs change.

When does the judder happen?

Only pulling away

Often clutch, flywheel, engine mount or low-speed drivability related.

Only hard acceleration

Misfire, fuel pressure, boost leaks or drivetrain load faults become more likely.

Only at high speed

Wheel balance, tyre condition, driveshafts or wheel bearings may be involved.

Only uphill

Load can expose weak clutch grip, fuel delivery issues or misfires.

Only when cold

Cold-running sensor, ignition or fuelling faults may feel worse before warm-up.

With clicking or knocking

Driveshaft, CV joint, mount or suspension checks become more important.

When juddering needs faster attention

  • !The engine management light is flashing.
  • !The car loses power or goes into limp mode.
  • !Juddering is severe enough to affect control.
  • !There is a burning clutch, fuel or electrical smell.
  • !You hear loud knocking, grinding or clicking noises.
  • !The car struggles to move off or stalls repeatedly.

What to check first

1. Notice speed vs revs

If the vibration follows engine revs, suspect engine or clutch. If it follows road speed, suspect wheels, tyres or shafts.

2. Check warning lights

Fault codes can quickly identify misfires, sensors, boost faults or fuelling issues.

3. Inspect tyres

Look for bulges, uneven wear, damage, low pressure or missing wheel weights.

4. Check clutch behaviour

Note slipping, burning smells, judder pulling away or a high bite point.

5. Listen for noises

Clicks, knocks or clunks can point towards CV joints, mounts or drivetrain wear.

6. Avoid guessing parts

Misfires, tyres, mounts, clutch faults and driveshafts can feel similar from the driver’s seat.

Can you keep driving?

If the judder is mild and occasional, drive gently and arrange inspection soon. Avoid hard acceleration, towing or long journeys until the cause is known.

Do not keep driving if the car shakes violently, loses power, smells of burning, shows a flashing warning light, makes loud mechanical noises or feels unsafe.

Repair advice

The right repair depends on whether the judder is caused by the engine, clutch, transmission, tyres or drivetrain. A proper road test plus fault-code scan and visual inspection usually gives the fastest answer.

  • 1Scan for misfire, sensor, fuel or boost fault codes.
  • 2Inspect plugs, coils and service items if an engine misfire is suspected.
  • 3Check clutch operation if the judder happens pulling away or uphill.
  • 4Inspect driveshafts, CV joints and engine mounts for movement or wear.
  • 5Check tyres, wheels and balancing if vibration is road-speed related.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car judder when accelerating?

Common causes include engine misfire, clutch faults, driveshaft wear, fuel delivery problems, turbo issues, worn mounts or tyre vibration.

Can spark plugs cause juddering?

Yes. Worn plugs or ignition coils can cause misfires that feel worse under acceleration.

Can tyres cause juddering under acceleration?

Yes. Damaged tyres, uneven wear or wheel imbalance can feel like acceleration judder.

Can clutch wear cause juddering?

Yes, especially when pulling away, changing gear or accelerating uphill.

Is juddering the same as hesitation?

Not always. Hesitation is usually delayed power, while juddering often feels like shaking or vibration.

Should I keep driving?

Avoid driving if the shaking is severe, warning lights flash, power drops or the car feels unsafe.
