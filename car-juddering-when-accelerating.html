Car Juddering When Accelerating

If your car shakes, vibrates or judders when accelerating, the cause may be an engine misfire, clutch fault, fuel delivery problem, turbo or boost issue, worn driveshaft, engine mount wear, tyre damage or wheel-related vibration.

Quick answer

A car juddering when accelerating is commonly caused by engine misfire, clutch slip or clutch contamination, worn driveshaft joints, engine mount movement, wheel imbalance, tyre damage, fuel delivery faults or turbo boost problems.

If the engine warning light appears, the car shakes badly, loses power, smells of burning or makes loud mechanical noises, arrange diagnosis promptly.

Common causes of juddering under acceleration

Engine misfire

Misfires can cause shaking, hesitation and juddering when the engine is under load.

Clutch problem

A worn, slipping or contaminated clutch can judder as power is applied.

Driveshaft or CV joint

Worn joints can cause vibration or shaking during acceleration.

Fuel delivery fault

Poor fuel pressure or uneven fuel flow can feel like juddering or surging.

Turbo boost fault

Boost leaks or unstable turbo control can cause shuddering under throttle.

Engine mount wear

Excess engine movement may be felt as a thump, shake or judder on acceleration.

Wheel balance issue

Wheel vibration often gets worse with road speed.

Tyre issue

Bulges, flat spots or uneven wear can mimic drivetrain judder.

Transmission fault

Some manual, automatic or dual-clutch gearboxes can shudder under load.

Engine or drivetrain: how to tell the difference

Engine-related

Often comes with jerking, hesitation, rough idle, warning lights or poor fuel economy.

Clutch or gearbox

Often strongest when pulling away, changing gear, accelerating uphill or reapplying power.

Wheels or shafts

Often linked to road speed rather than engine revs.

When does the judder happen?

Only pulling away

Often clutch, flywheel, engine mount or low-speed drivability related.

Only at higher speed

Wheel balance, tyres, driveshafts or wheel bearings may be likely.

Only hard acceleration

Misfire, boost, fuel pressure or drivetrain load faults are common.

Only uphill

Load can expose clutch slip, weak power delivery or fuel problems.

With warning light

Fault codes may identify misfires, sensors, boost or fuelling faults.

With noise

Clicks, knocks or clunks can indicate joints, mounts, shafts or suspension wear.

When juddering needs faster attention

  • !Heavy shaking or unsafe driving feel.
  • !Flashing engine management light.
  • !Burning clutch, fuel or electrical smell.
  • !Loud knocking, clicking or grinding noises.
  • !Power loss becomes severe.
  • !The car struggles to move off or stalls.

What to check first

1. Notice speed vs revs

If it follows engine revs, suspect engine or clutch. If it follows road speed, suspect tyres, wheels or shafts.

2. Check warning lights

Codes can quickly identify misfires, sensors, fuelling or boost faults.

3. Look at tyre condition

Check for bulges, uneven wear, flat spots, low pressure or visible damage.

4. Check service history

Old spark plugs, filters or neglected servicing may contribute.

5. Listen for noises

Clicks or knocks can point towards driveshaft, CV joint, mount or suspension wear.

6. Avoid guessing parts

Several faults feel similar, so a road test and inspection are usually best.

Can you keep driving?

If the judder is mild and occasional, drive gently and arrange inspection soon. Avoid hard acceleration, towing and long journeys until the cause is known.

Do not keep driving if the car shakes violently, loses power, shows a flashing warning light, smells of burning, makes loud mechanical noises or feels unsafe.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my car judder when accelerating?

Common causes include misfires, clutch faults, driveshaft wear, fuel delivery problems, turbo issues, mounts or tyre vibration.

Can spark plugs cause juddering?

Yes. Worn plugs or ignition coils can cause misfires that feel worse under acceleration.

Can tyres cause this?

Yes. Tyre damage, uneven wear or wheel balance faults can cause vibration.

Can clutch wear cause juddering?

Yes, especially when pulling away, changing gear or accelerating uphill.

Should I keep driving?

Avoid driving if the shaking is severe, warning lights flash, power drops or the car feels unsafe.

Will diagnostics help?

Yes, especially if warning lights, misfires, hesitation or power loss are present.
