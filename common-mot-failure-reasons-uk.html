Common MOT Failure Reasons in the UK

Most MOT failures are caused by everyday wear and tear that drivers ignore until test day. Tyres, brakes, lights, suspension, warning lights, rust and visibility problems are among the most common reasons cars fail MOT tests in the UK.

The good news is that many failures can be spotted before the MOT. A simple inspection around the car can often save money, avoid retest stress and prevent dangerous defects from getting worse.

Quick answer

The most common MOT failure reasons are worn tyres, faulty lights, brake problems, suspension wear, rust, warning lights, emissions faults and visibility issues.

Many MOT failures are avoidable with basic maintenance and pre-MOT checks. Warning lights, grinding brakes, knocking suspension, damaged tyres or visible leaks should never be ignored before the test.

Why do cars commonly fail MOT tests?

Cars usually fail MOT tests because wear and tear gradually builds up throughout the year. Drivers often ignore small advisories until they become larger failures.

Tyre wear

Tyres naturally wear down over mileage and are one of the biggest MOT fail areas.

Pothole damage

UK potholes commonly damage suspension, steering, wheels and tyre alignment.

Ignored advisories

Previous MOT advisories often become future MOT failures if left unresolved.

Rust and corrosion

Older vehicles commonly develop structural rust underneath the vehicle.

Electrical faults

Modern vehicles can fail because of dashboard warning lights or sensor faults.

Poor maintenance

Skipped servicing often leads to brake, tyre, fluid and emissions problems.

Items that can instantly fail an MOT

Some defects are considered serious safety risks and can fail immediately.

Dangerous tyres

Tyres below the legal limit or with exposed cords can fail instantly.

Brake defects

Poor braking force or brake fluid leaks are major safety concerns.

Major rust

Structural corrosion near suspension or seatbelt mounts is serious.

Failed lights

Brake lights and headlights are critical safety items.

Visibility issues

Large windscreen damage or failed wipers can affect visibility.

Warning lights

ABS, airbag or brake warning lights may trigger failures.

Simple checks before your MOT

  • ✓Check all exterior lights.
  • ✓Inspect tyre tread and tyre condition.

  • ✓Listen for grinding, knocking or humming noises.

Why MOT history matters when buying a used car

MOT history can reveal poor maintenance, repeated advisories, rust problems, mileage inconsistencies and ignored repairs.

  • ✓Look for repeated advisories every year.
  • ✓Check for corrosion history.
  • ✓Watch for repeated tyre or brake failures.
  • ✓Compare mileage records carefully.
  • ✓Be cautious of recent passes after multiple failures.

Best mechanic-style advice before an MOT

If your car already feels wrong before the MOT, there is usually a reason. Grinding brakes, knocking suspension, steering vibration, overheating, smoke or warning lights should never be ignored.

Many drivers wait until MOT day to investigate obvious symptoms. In reality, checking the car one or two weeks earlier gives you time to repair smaller issues before they become expensive failures.

Frequently asked questions

What are the most common MOT failures?

Tyres, brakes, lights, suspension, rust, warning lights and visibility problems are among the most common MOT failures.

Can warning lights fail an MOT?

Yes. ABS, airbag, brake, traction control, ESC and engine management warning lights may affect MOT results.

Can I avoid an MOT failure?

Many MOT failures can be avoided by checking lights, tyres, wipers, warning lights and fluid levels before the test.

Do advisories matter?

Yes. Advisories often become future MOT failures if ignored for another year.

What is the easiest MOT failure to avoid?

Bulbs, washer fluid, tyre pressure, wipers and number plate lights are among the easiest MOT failure risks to check before the test.

Can potholes cause MOT failures?

Yes. Potholes can damage tyres, suspension, steering and wheel alignment.
