Questions To Ask When Buying a Used Car

Asking the right questions before buying a used car can save you from expensive repairs, missing paperwork and dishonest sellers. Use this UK checklist to ask about service history, MOT records, mileage, faults, warning lights, accident damage and test drives before you commit.

Questions to ask before viewing the car

These questions can save you a wasted journey. Ask them before travelling, especially if the car is far away or the advert is short on detail.

  • 1How long have you owned the car?
  • 2Why are you selling it?
  • 3Does it have full or partial service history?
  • 4Are there any warning lights, faults or issues?
  • 5Has it been involved in an accident or insurance write-off?
  • 6Can I inspect it properly and take it for a test drive?

Questions to ask the seller

Are you the registered keeper?

The V5C should match the seller’s name and address where possible.

How long have you owned it?

Very short ownership is not always bad, but it deserves more questions.

Why are you selling?

A normal answer is fine. A vague or pressured answer can be a warning sign.

Is the price negotiable?

Useful to know before viewing, especially if work is due soon.

Where can I view the car?

For private sales, viewing at the V5C address is usually safer.

Can I bring someone with me?

A genuine seller should usually be comfortable with this.

Questions about history, mileage and paperwork

  • ✓Can I see the V5C logbook?
  • ✓Does the VIN match the car and paperwork?
  • ✓Is there service history with invoices or digital records?
  • ✓Does the MOT history match the mileage shown?
  • ✓Are there repeated MOT advisories?
  • ✓Are there two keys, manuals and locking wheel nut where relevant?

Before buying, read our guides to checking MOT history before buying, MOT advisory meaning and buying a car with no service history.

Questions about condition and maintenance

  • 1When was it last serviced?
  • 2When were the tyres and brakes last replaced?
  • 3Has the clutch, cambelt, battery or alternator ever been replaced?
  • 4Does it use oil or coolant between services?
  • 5Are there any leaks, noises, smells or starting problems?
  • 6Has any bodywork or paint repair been done?

If the seller mentions repair work, compare likely costs using our car repair costs guide UK.

Questions to ask before and during the test drive

  • ✓Can I start the car from cold?
  • ✓Can I drive it on different road types?
  • ✓Are there any warning lights after startup?
  • ✓Does the clutch slip, judder or smell burnt?
  • ✓Do the brakes grind, vibrate or pull to one side?
  • ✓Does the steering shake, pull or feel loose?

Use our used car test drive checklist and used car inspection checklist alongside these questions.

Red flag answers to watch for

  • !“I’m selling it for a friend” with no clear explanation.
  • !“No paperwork, but it has been well looked after.”
  • !“The warning light just needs resetting.”
  • !“It only overheats sometimes.”
  • !“No test drives until you pay.”
  • !“The price is low because I need it gone today.”

Good signs from a seller

  • ✓Clear answers that match the advert and paperwork.
  • ✓Service records, MOT history and invoices are available.
  • ✓The seller is happy for you to inspect and test drive the car.
  • ✓Known faults are explained honestly before viewing.
  • ✓The car is viewed at a sensible address and the V5C details make sense.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most important question to ask when buying a used car?

Ask whether the car has service history, current faults, warning lights or recent repairs. The answer should match the paperwork and condition.

Should I ask why the car is being sold?

Yes. It is a simple question that can reveal whether the sale sounds normal or suspicious.

Should I ask for service history before viewing?

Yes. It can save wasted time and help you judge whether the car has been properly maintained.

Is mileage the most important thing?

Not always. Mileage matters, but condition, service history and how the car has been used are often more important.

Can I negotiate after viewing?

Yes. Tyres, brakes, servicing, MOT advisories and obvious faults can all be fair reasons to negotiate.
