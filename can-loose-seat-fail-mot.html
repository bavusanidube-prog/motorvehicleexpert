MOT seat guide

Can a Loose Seat Fail MOT?

Yes, a loose seat can fail an MOT in the UK if it is insecure, does not lock properly, moves excessively or affects the driver’s ability to control the vehicle safely. Seat runners, mountings and adjustment mechanisms are important safety items.

Quick answer

A loose or insecure seat can fail an MOT if it is not properly attached, does not lock into position, rocks excessively or creates a safety risk.

The driver’s seat is especially important because it must allow safe use of the pedals, steering wheel and controls.

Common reasons a loose seat can fail MOT

Seat insecure

Loose bolts, damaged rails or broken mountings can cause failure.

Runner will not lock

If the seat slides unexpectedly, it may be unsafe.

Broken recliner

Backrest must hold securely in position.

Driver control affected

Unsafe seating position can affect pedal or steering control.

Corroded mounts

Rust around seat mounting points may weaken the fixing.

Seat belt affected

Seat faults matter more if restraint position is compromised.

Why the driver’s seat matters most

The driver’s seat must remain secure during braking, cornering and normal driving. If it rocks, tilts, slides back unexpectedly or collapses, safe vehicle control may be reduced.

Passenger seats can also fail if insecure, especially where seat belt anchorage or occupant safety is affected.

When a loose-looking seat may still pass

Some seats have a small amount of designed movement or flex. Cosmetic trim damage, worn fabric or minor squeaks do not automatically mean MOT failure.

What matters most is whether the seat frame, mountings and locking mechanisms are secure and functional.

Pre-MOT seat checks

  • 1Rock the seat gently and check for excessive movement.
  • 2Slide the seat forward and backward to confirm it locks properly.
  • 3Adjust the backrest and make sure it stays fixed.
  • 4Listen for clunks from runners or loose mountings.
  • 5Inspect visible bolts and brackets for looseness or rust.
  • 6Check the seat belt still sits correctly.
  • 7Ensure the driver can reach pedals and steering comfortably.
  • 8Repair movement before the MOT.

Should you repair a loose seat before MOT?

Yes. A moving seat is both a safety risk and a common avoidable MOT problem.

Simple faults may involve tightening bolts or repairing runners. More serious issues may need welding, replacement rails, brackets or a replacement seat frame.

Can you drive with a loose seat?

Driving with a loose driver’s seat can be dangerous because your seating position may shift during braking or cornering.

If the seat moves noticeably, repair it before regular driving.

Frequently asked questions

Will a loose driver seat fail MOT?

Yes, if it is insecure, moves excessively or does not lock safely.

Can broken seat runners fail MOT?

Yes, especially if the seat slides or cannot lock in place.

Can torn seat fabric fail MOT?

Usually not by itself unless it creates a safety issue.

Can a passenger seat fail MOT?

Yes, if insecure or affecting restraint safety.

Can missing seat bolts fail MOT?

Yes, missing or loose bolts can make the seat unsafe.

Should I fix it before the test?

Yes, seat security faults are safety-related and worth repairing first.
