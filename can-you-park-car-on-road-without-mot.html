UK MOT parking rules

Can You Park a Car on the Road Without MOT?

No. If your car legally needs an MOT and the MOT has expired, you cannot normally drive or park it on a public road. GOV.UK says you cannot drive or park your vehicle on the road if the MOT has run out, except for limited journeys such as travelling to a pre-arranged MOT test or repair.

This guide explains what happens if your car is parked on the road without MOT, whether police can detect it, what SORN means, and what you should do if the MOT has expired.

Quick answer

You cannot normally park a car on a public road without a valid MOT if the vehicle legally requires one. It does not matter whether you are driving it every day or simply leaving it outside your house.

GOV.UK says that if the MOT has run out, you cannot drive or park the vehicle on the road and you can be prosecuted if caught. The main exceptions are driving to or from somewhere for repair, or driving to a pre-arranged MOT test.

Important: A car with no MOT should usually be kept off the public road until it is repaired, tested and legal again.

Can you leave a car parked on the road with no MOT?

No. If the vehicle is on a public road, it generally needs to be legal for road use. That means it must normally have valid MOT, insurance and tax where required.

Public road

A vehicle parked on the road is still being kept on the public highway.

No MOT

Expired MOT can make the vehicle illegal to keep on the road.

Reported vehicle

Vehicles without MOT can be reported and investigated.

Can you be fined for parking without MOT?

Yes. GOV.UK’s report-no-MOT service says the police will investigate reports and the driver may get a fine, with the maximum fine being £1,000.

  • !A neighbour or member of the public can report a no-MOT vehicle.
  • !Police can check MOT status electronically.
  • !No MOT may also lead to wider checks for tax and insurance.
  • !Unsafe vehicles may face stronger action.

Can you SORN a car and leave it on the road?

No. SORN means Statutory Off Road Notification. GOV.UK explains that your vehicle is off the road if you do not keep or use it on a public road, for example if it is in a garage, on a drive or on private land.

SORN car allowed

Garage, driveway, private land or other off-road storage.

SORN car not allowed

Public road, street parking bay or public highway.

No road use

A SORN vehicle cannot be used on the road until taxed again.

MOT may still be needed

To tax the vehicle again, a valid MOT is normally needed if the car requires one.

Can you keep a no-MOT car on private land?

Yes, if the vehicle is genuinely off the public road. A private driveway, private garage or private land is normally the correct place to keep a car if the MOT has expired and you are not using it.

Driveway

Usually acceptable if it is private and not part of the public road.

Garage

A good option if the car is waiting for repairs or MOT.

Private land

Must genuinely be off the public highway.

Can police see if a parked car has no MOT?

Yes. Police can check MOT status using vehicle databases, roadside checks and ANPR systems. ANPR can detect registration details and flag vehicles that may not have valid MOT, tax or insurance.

What about insurance and tax?

MOT, insurance and tax are separate, but they work together in practice. A vehicle kept on the road normally needs to be taxed and insured, and if it requires an MOT, it should have a valid MOT as well.

Insurance

No MOT may create insurance issues if the vehicle is unsafe or illegally kept on the road.

Tax

You normally cannot renew vehicle tax if the MOT has expired.

SORN

SORN is only suitable if the vehicle is kept off the public road.

Are there any legal exceptions?

The main legal exceptions relate to driving the vehicle, not leaving it parked on the road. If the MOT has expired, the limited exceptions are usually driving directly to a pre-arranged MOT test or travelling to or from repairs.

  • ✓Drive directly to a pre-arranged MOT test.
  • ✓Drive to or from eligible repairs.
  • ✓Vehicle must still be insured.
  • ✓Vehicle must still be roadworthy.
  • !Do not use this as permission to park on the road without MOT.

What should you do if the MOT has expired?

  • 1Move the vehicle off the public road if possible.
  • 2Book an MOT test as soon as possible.
  • 3Do not use the car for normal journeys.
  • 4Repair obvious safety defects before driving.
  • 5Keep proof of any MOT appointment.
  • 6Tax the car after it passes, if tax is due.

Best practical advice

If your car has no MOT, do not leave it on the public road while you “sort it later”. Move it to private land if possible, book the MOT and avoid unnecessary driving.

The safest approach is to keep MOT dates organised, deal with advisories early and avoid letting the vehicle become untaxed, uninsured or unroadworthy.

Frequently asked questions

Can you park a car on the road without MOT?

No. If the car legally needs an MOT, it should not be parked on a public road after the MOT has expired.

Can you be fined for a parked car with no MOT?

Yes. A no-MOT vehicle can be reported and the driver may receive a fine.

Can police see no MOT on a parked car?

Yes. Police can check MOT status using registration details and vehicle databases.

Can you SORN a car parked on the road?

No. A SORN vehicle must be kept off public roads.

Can I keep a no-MOT car on my driveway?

Yes, if it is private land and the vehicle is not being used on the public road.

Can I drive it to an MOT test?

Yes, if the MOT is pre-arranged, the car is insured and it is roadworthy.
